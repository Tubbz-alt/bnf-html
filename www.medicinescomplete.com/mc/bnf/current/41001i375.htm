Levomepromazine

Levomepromazine has the following interaction information:

Desferrioxaminemanufacturer of levomepromazine advises avoid concomitant use with desferrioxamine

Levomepromazine belongs to Phenothiazines and will have the following interactions:

Adrenergic Neurone Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with adrenergic neurone blockers
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antacidsabsorption of phenothiazines reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Antidepressants, Tricyclicincreased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when phenothiazines given with tricyclics
Antimuscarinicsplasma concentration of phenothiazines reduced by antimuscarinics , but risk of antimuscarinic side-effects increasedMany drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
Beta-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Clonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with clonidine
Diazoxideenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with diazoxide
Disopyramideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with disopyramide
Diureticsenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with diuretics
Dronedaroneavoidance of phenothiazines advised by manufacturer of dronedarone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Droperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines that prolong the QT interval given with droperidol—avoid concomitant use
Hydralazineenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with hydralazine
Kaolinabsorption of phenothiazines possibly reduced by kaolin
Lithiumincreased risk of extrapyramidal side-effects and possibly neurotoxicity when phenothiazines given with lithium
Minoxidilenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with minoxidil
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Moxonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with moxonidine
Nitratesenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with nitrates
Pentamidine Isetionateincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with pentamidine isetionate
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Sodium Nitroprussideenhanced hypotensive effect when phenothiazines given with sodium nitroprusside
Sotalolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when phenothiazines given with sotalol
Sulfonylureas phenothiazines possibly antagonise hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas

Levomepromazine belongs to Antipsychotics and will have the following interactions:

Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
ACE Inhibitorsenhanced hypotensive effect when antipsychotics given with ACE inhibitors
Alcoholincreased sedative effect when antipsychotics given with alcohol
Alpha-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when antipsychotics given with alpha-blockers
Amantadineincreased risk of extrapyramidal side-effects when antipsychotics given with amantadine
Anaesthetics, Generalenhanced hypotensive effect when antipsychotics given with general anaesthetics See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonistsenhanced hypotensive effect when antipsychotics given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Anti-arrhythmicsincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval given with anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval
Antidepressants, Tricyclic antipsychotics increase plasma concentration of tricyclics—possibly increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias
Antiepileptics antipsychotics antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsincreased sedative effect when antipsychotics given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Apomorphine antipsychotics antagonise effects of apomorphine
Arsenic Trioxideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval given with arsenic trioxide
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of antipsychotics advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval given with atomoxetine
Bromocriptine antipsychotics antagonise hypoprolactinaemic and antiparkinsonian effects of bromocriptine
Cabergoline antipsychotics antagonise hypoprolactinaemic and antiparkinsonian effects of cabergoline
Calcium-channel Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when antipsychotics given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Cimetidineeffects of antipsychotics possibly enhanced by cimetidine
Histamine antipsychotics theoretically antagonise effects of histamine—manufacturer of histamine advises avoid concomitant use
Levodopa antipsychotics antagonise effects of levodopa
Memantineeffects of antipsychotics possibly reduced by memantine
Methadoneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval given with methadone
Methyldopaenhanced hypotensive effect when antipsychotics given with methyldopa (also increased risk of extrapyramidal effects)
Metoclopramideincreased risk of extrapyramidal side-effects when antipsychotics given with metoclopramide
Opioid Analgesicsenhanced hypotensive and sedative effects when antipsychotics given with opioid analgesics
Pergolide antipsychotics antagonise effects of pergolide
Pramipexoleavoidance of antipsychotics advised by manufacturer of pramipexole (antagonism of effect)
Ritonavirplasma concentration of antipsychotics possibly increased by ritonavir
Ropiniroleavoidance of antipsychotics advised by manufacturer of ropinirole (antagonism of effect)
Rotigotineavoidance of antipsychotics advised by manufacturer of rotigotine (antagonism of effect)
Sodium Oxybate antipsychotics possibly enhance effects of sodium oxybate
Sympathomimetics antipsychotics antagonise hypertensive effect of sympathomimetics
Tetrabenazineincreased risk of extrapyramidal side-effects when antipsychotics given with tetrabenazine
Tramadolincreased risk of convulsions when antipsychotics given with tramadol
