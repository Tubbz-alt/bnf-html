Vaccines

Vaccines has the following interaction information:

For a general warning on live vaccines and high doses of corticosteroids or other immunosuppressive drugs, see section 14.1; for advice on live vaccines and immunoglobulins, see under Normal Immunoglobulin, section 14.5.1
Abataceptavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with abatacept (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Adalimumabavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with adalimumab (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Anakinraavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with anakinra (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Belimumabavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with belimumab (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Certolizumab pegolavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with certolizumab pegol (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Corticosteroidsimmune response to vaccines impaired by high doses of corticosteroids , avoid concomitant use with live vaccines (see under Active immunity, section 14.1)Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Etanerceptavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with etanercept (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Golimumabavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with golimumab (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Infliximabavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with infliximab (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Interferon Gammaavoidance of vaccines advised by manufacturer of interferon gamma
Leflunomideavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with leflunomide (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)Increased risk of toxicity with other haematotoxic and hepatotoxic drugs
Tocilizumabavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with tocilizumab (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)
Ustekinumabavoid concomitant use of live vaccines with ustekinumab (see under Active Immunity, section 14.1)

Varicella-zoster Vaccine belongs to Vaccines and has the following interaction information:

Aspirinpossible risk of Reyes syndrome when varicella-zoster vaccine given with aspirin—manufacturers advise avoid aspirin for 6 weeks after giving varicella-zoster vaccine

Typhoid Vaccine (parenteral) belongs to Vaccines but has no specific interaction information.

Typhoid Vaccine (oral) belongs to Vaccines and has the following interaction information:

Antibacterials oral typhoid vaccine inactivated by antibacterials—see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4
Antimalarials oral typhoid vaccine inactivated by antimalarials—see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4

Influenza Vaccine belongs to Vaccines and has the following interaction information:

Phenytoin influenza vaccine enhances effects of phenytoin
Theophylline influenza vaccine possibly increases plasma concentration of theophylline
Warfarin influenza vaccine possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of warfarin
