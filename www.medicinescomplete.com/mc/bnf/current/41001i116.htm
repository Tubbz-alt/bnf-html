Anti-arrhythmics

Anti-arrhythmics has the following interaction information:

Anti-arrhythmicsincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with other anti-arrhythmics
Antipsychoticsincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT intervalIncreased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Beta-blockersincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Bupivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with bupivacaine
Levobupivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with levobupivacaine
Prilocaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with prilocaine
Ropivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with ropivacaine

Dronedarone belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antidepressants, Tricyclicmanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with tricyclics (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Atorvastatin dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of atorvastatin
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by carbamazepine—avoid concomitant use
Clarithromycinmanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with clarithromycin (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Coumarins dronedarone possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Dabigatran Etexilate dronedarone increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate—avoid concomitant use
Digoxin dronedarone increases plasma concentration of digoxin (halve dose of digoxin)
Diltiazemincreased risk of bradycardia and myocardial depression when dronedarone given with diltiazem
Disopyramideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with disopyramide—avoid concomitant use
Erythromycinplasma concentration of dronedarone increased by erythromycin (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Fingolimodpossible increased risk of bradycardia when dronedarone given with fingolimod
Grapefruit Juiceplasma concentration of dronedarone increased by grapefruit juice—avoid concomitant use
Itraconazolemanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with itraconazole
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of dronedarone increased by ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use
Metoprolol dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of metoprolol
Nifedipineplasma concentration of dronedarone increased by nifedipine
Phenindione dronedarone possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of phenindione Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by phenobarbital—avoid concomitant use
Phenothiazinesmanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with phenothiazines (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Phenytoinplasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by phenytoin—avoid concomitant use
Posaconazolemanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with posaconazole
Propranolol dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of propranolol
Rifampicinplasma concentration of dronedarone reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use
Ritonavirmanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with ritonavir
Rosuvastatin dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of rosuvastatin
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Simvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when dronedarone given with simvastatin
Sirolimusmanufacturer of dronedarone advises caution with sirolimus
Sotalolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with sotalol—avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Tacrolimusmanufacturer of dronedarone advises caution with tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Telithromycinmanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with telithromycin (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Verapamilincreased risk of bradycardia and myocardial depression when dronedarone given with verapamil
Voriconazolemanufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with voriconazole

Adenosine belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Possibility of interaction with drugs tending to impair myocardial conduction
Dipyridamoleeffect of adenosine enhanced and extended by dipyridamole (important risk of toxicity)
Nicotineeffects of adenosine possibly enhanced by nicotine
Theophyllineanti-arrhythmic effect of adenosine antagonised by theophylline

Amiodarone belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Agalsidase Alfa and Beta amiodarone possibly inhibits effects of agalsidase alfa and beta (manufacturers of agalsidase alfa and beta advise avoid concomitant use)
Amisulprideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with amisulpride—avoid concomitant use
Antidepressants, Tricyclicincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with tricyclics—avoid concomitant use
Arsenic Trioxideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with arsenic trioxide
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of amiodarone advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with atomoxetine
Benperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with benperidol—manufacturer of benperidol advises avoid concomitant use
Beta-blockersincreased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when amiodarone given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine—avoid concomitant use
Ciclosporin amiodarone possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Cimetidineplasma concentration of amiodarone increased by cimetidine
Colchicine amiodarone possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity
Coumarins amiodarone inhibits metabolism of coumarins (enhanced anticoagulant effect)Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Dabigatran Etexilate amiodarone increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate (reduce dose of dabigatran etexilate)
Digoxin amiodarone increases plasma concentration of digoxin (halve dose of digoxin)
Diltiazemincreased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when amiodarone given with diltiazem
Disopyramideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with disopyramide—avoid concomitant use
Diuretics, Loopincreased cardiac toxicity with amiodarone if hypokalaemia occurs with loop diuretics
Diuretics, Thiazide and relatedincreased cardiac toxicity with amiodarone if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics
Dronedaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with dronedarone—avoid concomitant use
Droperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with droperidol—avoid concomitant use
Eplerenone amiodarone increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)
Erythromycinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with parenteral erythromycin—avoid concomitant useInteractions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Fingolimodpossible increased risk of bradycardia when amiodarone given with fingolimod
Flecainide amiodarone increases plasma concentration of flecainide (halve dose of flecainide)
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of amiodarone possibly increased by fosamprenavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Grapefruit Juiceplasma concentration of amiodarone increased by grapefruit juice
Haloperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with haloperidol—avoid concomitant use
Indinavirplasma concentration of amiodarone possibly increased by indinavir—avoid concomitant use
Ivabradineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with ivabradine
Levofloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with levofloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Lithiummanufacturer of amiodarone advises avoid concomitant use with lithium (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Mefloquineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with mefloquine—avoid concomitant use
Mizolastineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Nelfinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with nelfinavir—avoid concomitant use
Orlistatplasma concentration of amiodarone possibly reduced by orlistat
Pentamidine Isetionateincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with pentamidine isetionate—avoid concomitant use
Phenindione amiodarone inhibits metabolism of phenindione (enhanced anticoagulant effect)Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Phenothiazinesincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with phenothiazines—avoid concomitant use
Phenytoin amiodarone inhibits metabolism of phenytoin (increased plasma concentration)
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quinineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with quinine—avoid concomitant use
Ritonavirplasma concentration of amiodarone increased by ritonavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Simvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when amiodarone given with simvastatin
Sotalolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with sotalol—avoid concomitant use
Sulfamethoxazolepossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with sulfamethoxazole (as co-trimoxazole)—manufacturer of amiodarone advises avoid concomitant use of co-trimoxazole

Verapamilincreased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when amiodarone given with verapamil
Zuclopenthixolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with zuclopenthixol—avoid concomitant use

Disopyramide belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Acetazolamideincreased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with acetazolamide
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antimuscarinicsincreased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when disopyramide given with antimuscarinics Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
Arsenic Trioxideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with arsenic trioxide
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with atomoxetine

Clarithromycinplasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by clarithromycin (increased risk of toxicity)

Diuretics, Thiazide and relatedincreased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics
Dronedaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with dronedarone—avoid concomitant use
Droperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with droperidol—avoid concomitant use

Fingolimodpossible increased risk of bradycardia when disopyramide given with fingolimod
Gliclazide disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of gliclazide
Haloperidolpossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with haloperidol—avoid concomitant use
Insulin disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of insulin
Itraconazoleavoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of itraconazole
Ivabradineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with ivabradine
Ketoconazoleincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use
Metformin disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of metformin
Mizolastineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Nitrates disopyramide reduces effects of sublingual tablets of nitrates (failure to dissolve under tongue owing to dry mouth)
Pentamidine Isetionatepossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with pentamidine isetionate
Phenobarbitalmetabolism of disopyramide accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced plasma concentration)
Phenothiazinesincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with phenothiazines

Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Sildenafilmanufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with sildenafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Sotalolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with sotalol—avoid concomitant use
Sulpirideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with sulpiride
Tadalafilmanufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with tadalafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Telapreviravoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Tolterodineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with tolterodine
Vardenafilmanufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with vardenafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Amiodaroneplasma concentration of flecainide increased by amiodarone (halve dose of flecainide)Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Atazanavirplasma concentration of lidocaine possibly increased by atazanavir
Cimetidineplasma concentration of lidocaine increased by cimetidine (increased risk of toxicity)
Darunavirplasma concentration of lidocaine possibly increased by darunavir—avoid concomitant use
Diuretics, Loopaction of lidocaine antagonised by hypokalaemia caused by loop diuretics
Diuretics, Thiazide and relatedaction of lidocaine antagonised by hypokalaemia caused by thiazides and related diuretics
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of lidocaine possibly increased by fosamprenavir—avoid concomitant useFosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Lopinavirplasma concentration of lidocaine possibly increased by lopinavir In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when lidocaine given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
