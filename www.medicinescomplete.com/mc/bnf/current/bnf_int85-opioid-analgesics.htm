Opioid Analgesics

Opioid Analgesics has the following interaction information:

Alcohol

enhanced hypotensive and sedative effects when opioid analgesics given with alcohol

Anaesthetics, General (intravenous)

opioid analgesics possibly enhance effects of intravenous general anaesthetics

Anaesthetics, General (volatile liquids)

opioid analgesics possibly enhance effects of volatile liquid general anaesthetics

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

sedative effects possibly increased when opioid analgesics given with tricyclics

Antihistamines, Sedating

sedative effects possibly increased when opioid analgesics given with sedating antihistamines

Antipsychotics

enhanced hypotensive and sedative effects when opioid analgesics given with antipsychotics

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

increased sedative effect when opioid analgesics given with anxiolytics and hypnotics

Cimetidine

metabolism of opioid analgesics inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Domperidone

opioid analgesics antagonise effects of domperidone on gastro-intestinal activity

MAOIs

possible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when opioid analgesics given with MAOIs —some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Metoclopramide

opioid analgesics antagonise effects of metoclopramide on gastro-intestinal activity

Moclobemide

possible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when opioid analgesics given with moclobemide —manufacturer of moclobemide advises consider reducing dose of opioid analgesics

Selegiline

avoidance of opioid analgesics advised by manufacturer of selegiline

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Sodium Oxybate

opioid analgesics enhance effects of sodium oxybate (avoid concomitant use)

Opioid Analgesics belongs to Analgesics but Analgesics has no interactions information.

Tapentadol belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Alfentanil belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Diltiazem

metabolism of alfentanil inhibited by diltiazem (risk of prolonged or delayed respiratory depression)

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of alfentanil increased by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Fluconazole

metabolism of alfentanil inhibited by fluconazole (risk of prolonged or delayed respiratory depression)

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Itraconazole

metabolism of alfentanil possibly inhibited by itraconazole

Rifampicin

metabolism of alfentanil accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of alfentanil increased by ritonavir

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when alfentanil given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of alfentanil increased by voriconazole (consider reducing dose of alfentanil)

Buprenorphine belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Ketoconazole

metabolism of buprenorphine inhibited by ketoconazole (reduce dose of buprenorphine)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of buprenorphine possibly increased by ritonavir

Tipranavir

buprenorphine possibly reduces plasma concentration of tipranavir

Codeine belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Antimuscarinics

possible increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when codeine given with antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Rifampicin

metabolism of codeine accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)

Dextromethorphan belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Memantine

increased risk of CNS toxicity when dextromethorphan given with memantine (manufacturer of memantine advises avoid concomitant use)

Moclobemide

possible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when dextromethorphan given with moclobemide —avoid concomitant use

Rasagiline

avoid concomitant use of dextromethorphan with rasagiline

Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Dextropropoxyphene belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

dextropropoxyphene enhances effects of carbamazepine

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of dextropropoxyphene increased by ritonavir (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Diamorphine belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Dihydrocodeine belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Diphenoxylate belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Dipipanone belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Fentanyl belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Antifungals, Triazole

plasma concentration of fentanyl possibly increased by triazoles

Baclofen

increased sedative effect when fentanyl given with baclofen

Etomidate

fentanyl inhibits metabolism of etomidate (consider reducing dose of etomidate)

Midazolam

fentanyl possibly inhibits metabolism of midazolam

Rifampicin

metabolism of fentanyl accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of fentanyl increased by ritonavir

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when fentanyl given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Hydromorphone belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Meptazinol belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Methadone belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Abacavir

plasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by abacavir

Amisulpride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with amisulpride —avoid concomitant use

Antipsychotics

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Atomoxetine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with atomoxetine

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of methadone reduced by carbamazepine

Didanosine

methadone possibly reduces plasma concentration of didanosine

Note: Antacids in tablet formulation may affect absorption of other drugs

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of methadone reduced by efavirenz

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of methadone increased by fluconazole

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fluoxetine

plasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by fluoxetine

Fluvoxamine

plasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by fluvoxamine

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of methadone reduced by fosamprenavir

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Nelfinavir

plasma concentration of methadone reduced by nelfinavir

Nevirapine

plasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by nevirapine

Paroxetine

plasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by paroxetine

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of methadone reduced by phenobarbital

Phenytoin

metabolism of methadone accelerated by phenytoin (reduced effect and risk of withdrawal effects)

Rifampicin

metabolism of methadone accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)

Rilpivirine

plasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by rilpivirine

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of methadone reduced by ritonavir

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Sertraline

plasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by sertraline

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by St John's wort

Telaprevir

caution with methadone advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Vandetanib

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with vandetanib —avoid concomitant use

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of methadone increased by voriconazole (consider reducing dose of methadone)

Zidovudine

methadone possibly increases plasma concentration of zidovudine

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Morphine belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Baclofen

increased sedative effect when morphine given with baclofen

Esmolol

morphine possibly increases plasma concentration of esmolol

Gabapentin

morphine increases bioavailability of gabapentin

Rifampicin

metabolism of morphine accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of morphine possibly reduced by ritonavir

Oxycodone belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Rifampicin

metabolism of oxycodone possibly accelerated by rifampicin

Telithromycin

metabolism of oxycodone inhibited by telithromycin

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of oxycodone increased by voriconazole

Papaveretum belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Pentazocine belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Pethidine belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Duloxetine

possible increased serotonergic effects when pethidine given with duloxetine

MAOIs

CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when pethidine given with MAOIs —avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Moclobemide

possible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when pethidine given with moclobemide —avoid concomitant use

Rasagiline

risk of CNS toxicity when pethidine given with rasagiline (avoid pethidine for 2 weeks after rasagiline)

Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of pethidine reduced by ritonavir , but plasma concentration of toxic pethidine metabolite increased (avoid concomitant use)

Selegiline

hyperpyrexia and CNS toxicity reported when pethidine given with selegiline (avoid concomitant use)

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Remifentanil belongs to Opioid Analgesics but has no specific interaction information.

Tramadol belongs to Opioid Analgesics and has the following interaction information:

Antidepressants, SSRI

increased risk of CNS toxicity when tramadol given with SSRIs

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased risk of CNS toxicity when tramadol given with tricyclics

Antipsychotics

increased risk of convulsions when tramadol given with antipsychotics

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Atomoxetine

possible increased risk of convulsions when tramadol given with atomoxetine

Carbamazepine

effects of tramadol reduced by carbamazepine

Coumarins

tramadol enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Duloxetine

possible increased serotonergic effects when tramadol given with duloxetine

MAOIs

possible increased serotonergic effects and increased risk of convulsions when tramadol given with MAOIs —some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Mirtazapine

possible increased serotonergic effects when tramadol given with mirtazapine

Ondansetron

effects of tramadol possibly antagonised by ondansetron

Venlafaxine

possible increased serotonergic effects when tramadol given with venlafaxine
