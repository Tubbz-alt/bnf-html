    Antacids

    Antacids has the following interaction information:

    Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

    ACE Inhibitors

    antacids possibly reduce absorption of ACE inhibitors

    Aspirin

    alkaline urine due to some antacids increases excretion of aspirin

    Atazanavir

    antacids possibly reduce plasma concentration of atazanavir

    Azithromycin

    antacids reduce absorption of azithromycin

    Bile Acids

    antacids possibly reduce absorption of bile acids

    Bisphosphonates

    antacids reduce absorption of bisphosphonates

    Captopril

    antacids reduce absorption of captopril

    Cefaclor

    antacids reduce absorption of cefaclor

    Cefpodoxime

    antacids reduce absorption of cefpodoxime

    Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine

    antacids reduce absorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine

    Ciprofloxacin

    antacids reduce absorption of ciprofloxacin

    Deferasirox

    antacids containing aluminium possibly reduce absorption of deferasirox (manufacturer of deferasirox advises avoid concomitant use)

    Deflazacort

    antacids reduce absorption of deflazacort

    Digoxin

    antacids possibly reduce absorption of digoxin

    Dipyridamole

    antacids possibly reduce absorption of dipyridamole

    Eltrombopag

    antacids reduce absorption of eltrombopag (give at least 4 hours apart)

    Enalapril

    antacids reduce absorption of enalapril

    Erlotinib

    antacids possibly reduce plasma concentration of erlotinib—give antacids at least 4 hours before or 2 hours after erlotinib

    Fexofenadine

    antacids reduce absorption of fexofenadine

    Fosinopril

    antacids reduce absorption of fosinopril

    Gabapentin

    antacids reduce absorption of gabapentin

    Isoniazid

    antacids reduce absorption of isoniazid

    Itraconazole

    antacids reduce absorption of itraconazole

    Ketoconazole

    antacids reduce absorption of ketoconazole

    Lansoprazole

    antacids possibly reduce absorption of lansoprazole

    Levofloxacin

    antacids reduce absorption of levofloxacin

    Levothyroxine

    antacids possibly reduce absorption of levothyroxine

    Methenamine

    avoid concomitant use of antacids with methenamine

    Moxifloxacin

    antacids reduce absorption of moxifloxacin

    Mycophenolate

    antacids reduce absorption of mycophenolate

    Norfloxacin

    antacids reduce absorption of norfloxacin

    Ofloxacin

    antacids reduce absorption of ofloxacin

    Penicillamine

    antacids reduce absorption of penicillamine

    Phenothiazines

    antacids reduce absorption of phenothiazines

    Phenytoin

    antacids reduce absorption of phenytoin

    Rifampicin

    antacids reduce absorption of rifampicin

    Rilpivirine

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises give antacids 2 hours before or 4 hours after rilpivirine

    Rosuvastatin

    antacids reduce absorption of rosuvastatin

    Sulpiride

    antacids reduce absorption of sulpiride

    Tetracyclines

    antacids reduce absorption of tetracyclines

    Tipranavir

    antacids reduce absorption of tipranavir

    Ulipristal

    avoidance of antacids advised by manufacturer of high-dose ulipristal (contraceptive effect of ulipristal possibly reduced)

    Aluminium Hydroxide belongs to Antacids and has the following interaction information:

    Polystyrene Sulfonate Resins

    risk of intestinal obstruction when aluminium hydroxide given with polystyrene sulfonate resins

    Hydrotalcite belongs to Antacids but has no specific interaction information.

    Magnesium Salts (oral) belongs to Antacids and has the following interaction information:

    Iron

    oral magnesium salts (as magnesium trisilicate) reduce absorption of oral iron

    Nitrofurantoin

    oral magnesium salts (as magnesium trisilicate) reduce absorption of nitrofurantoin

    Polystyrene Sulfonate Resins

    risk of metabolic alkalosis when oral magnesium salts given with polystyrene sulfonate resins

    Proguanil

    oral magnesium salts (as magnesium trisilicate) reduce absorption of proguanil

    Sodium Bicarbonate belongs to Antacids and has the following interaction information:

    Lithium

    sodium bicarbonate increases excretion of lithium (reduced plasma concentration)
