Protein Kinase Inhibitors

Protein Kinase Inhibitors has no specific interaction information.

Protein Kinase Inhibitors belongs to Cytotoxics and will have the following interactions:

Clozapineavoid concomitant use of cytotoxics with clozapine (increased risk of agranulocytosis)Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Pazopanib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavirmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with atazanavir
Bocepreviravoidance of pazopanib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Clarithromycinmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with clarithromycin
Grapefruit Juicemanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with grapefruit juice
Indinavirmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with indinavir
Itraconazolemanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with itraconazole
Ketoconazolemanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with ketoconazole
Lapatinibplasma concentration of pazopanib increased by lapatinib
Nelfinavirmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with nelfinavir
Rifampicinmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with rifampicin
Ritonavirmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with ritonavir
Saquinavirmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with saquinavir
Telithromycinmanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with telithromycin
Voriconazolemanufacturer of pazopanib advises avoid concomitant use with voriconazole

Bocepreviravoidance of gefitinib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Carbamazepinemanufacturer of gefitinib advises avoid concomitant use with carbamazepine
Itraconazoleplasma concentration of gefitinib increased by itraconazole
Phenobarbitalmanufacturer of gefitinib advises avoid concomitant use with phenobarbital
Phenytoinmanufacturer of gefitinib advises avoid concomitant use with phenytoin
Ranitidineplasma concentration of gefitinib reduced by ranitidine

St John's Wortmanufacturer of gefitinib advises avoid concomitant use with St John's wort
Warfarin gefitinib possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of warfarin

Everolimus belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavirplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by atazanavir—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Ciclosporinplasma concentration of everolimus increased by ciclosporin
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by clarithromycin—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Darunavirplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by darunavir—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Erythromycinplasma concentration of everolimus increased by erythromycin Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Grapefruit Juicemanufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use with grapefruit juice
Indinavirplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by indinavir—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Itraconazoleplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by itraconazole—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of everolimus increased by ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use
Nelfinavirplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by nelfinavir—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Posaconazoleplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by posaconazole—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Rifampicinplasma concentration of everolimus reduced by rifampicin
Ritonavirplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by ritonavir—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Saquinavirplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by saquinavir—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of everolimus possibly reduced by St John's wort—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Telithromycinplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by telithromycin—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Verapamilplasma concentration of both drugs may increase when everolimus given with verapamil
Voriconazoleplasma concentration of everolimus possibly increased by voriconazole—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use

Temsirolimus belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

The main active metabolite of temsirolimus is sirolimus—see also interactions of sirolimus and consult product literature
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of active metabolite of temsirolimus increased by ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use
Rifampicinplasma concentration of active metabolite of temsirolimus reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use

Lapatinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Bocepreviravoidance of lapatinib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of lapatinib reduced by carbamazepine—avoid concomitant use
Docetaxelpossible increased risk of neutropenia when lapatinib given with docetaxel
Grapefruit Juicemanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with grapefruit juice
Histamine H2-antagonistsabsorption of lapatinib possibly reduced by histamine H2-antagonists
Irinotecan lapatinib increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of irinotecan—consider reducing dose of irinotecan
Itraconazolemanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with itraconazole
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of lapatinib increased by ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use
Paclitaxelincreased risk of neutropenia when lapatinib given with paclitaxel
Pazopanib lapatinib increases plasma concentration of pazopanib
Phenytoinmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with phenytoin
Pimozidemanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with pimozide
Posaconazolemanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with posaconazole
Proton Pump Inhibitorsabsorption of lapatinib possibly reduced by proton pump inhibitors
Repaglinidemanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with repaglinide
Rifabutinmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with rifabutin
Rifampicinmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with rifampicin
Ritonavirmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with ritonavir
Saquinavirmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with saquinavir
St John's Wortmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with St John's wort
Telithromycinmanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with telithromycin
Voriconazolemanufacturer of lapatinib advises avoid concomitant use with voriconazole

Sunitinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Bocepreviravoidance of sunitinib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Ketoconazolemetabolism of sunitinib inhibited by ketoconazole (increased plasma concentration)
Rifampicinmetabolism of sunitinib accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

Sorafenib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Bocepreviravoidance of sorafenib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Coumarins sorafenib possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Docetaxel sorafenib increases plasma concentration of docetaxel
Doxorubicin sorafenib possibly increases plasma concentration of doxorubicin
Irinotecan sorafenib possibly increases plasma concentration of irinotecan
Neomycinbioavailability of sorafenib reduced by neomycin
Rifampicinplasma concentration of sorafenib reduced by rifampicin

Imatinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Bocepreviravoidance of imatinib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of imatinib reduced by carbamazepine—avoid concomitant use
Ciclosporin imatinib possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of imatinib increased by ketoconazole
Levothyroxine imatinib possibly reduces plasma concentration of levothyroxine
Oxcarbazepineplasma concentration of imatinib reduced by oxcarbazepine—avoid concomitant use
Paracetamolmanufacturer of imatinib advises caution with paracetamol
Phenytoinplasma concentration of imatinib reduced by phenytoin—avoid concomitant use
Rifampicinplasma concentration of imatinib reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use
Simvastatin imatinib increases plasma concentration of simvastatin
St John's Wortplasma concentration of imatinib reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Warfarinmanufacturer of imatinib advises replacement of warfarin with a heparin (possibility of enhanced warfarin effect)

Erlotinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

Antacidsplasma concentration of erlotinib possibly reduced by antacids—give antacids at least 4 hours before or 2 hours after erlotinibAntacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Bocepreviravoidance of erlotinib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Capecitabineplasma concentration of erlotinib possibly increased by capecitabine Capecitabine is a prodrug of fluorouracil
Cimetidinemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with cimetidine
Ciprofloxacinplasma concentration of erlotinib increased by ciprofloxacin
Coumarinsincreased risk of bleeding when erlotinib given with coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Esomeprazolemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with esomeprazole
Famotidinemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with famotidine
Ketoconazolemetabolism of erlotinib inhibited by ketoconazole (increased plasma concentration)
Lansoprazolemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with lansoprazole
Nizatidinemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with nizatidine
NSAIDsincreased risk of bleeding when erlotinib given with NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Omeprazoleplasma concentration of erlotinib reduced by omeprazole—manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use
Pantoprazolemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with pantoprazole
Rabeprazolemanufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with rabeprazole
Ranitidineplasma concentration of erlotinib reduced by ranitidine—manufacturer of erlotinib advises give at least 2 hours before or 10 hours after ranitidine
Rifampicinmetabolism of erlotinib accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)
