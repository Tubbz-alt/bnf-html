CO-BENELDOPA

Additional information interactions (Levodopa).

A mixture of benserazide hydrochloride and levodopa in mass proportions corresponding to 1 part of benserazide and 4 parts of levodopa

Indications

 

Parkinson’s disease, see notes above

Cautions

 

see notes above

Pregnancy

 

see notes above

Breast-feeding

 

see notes above

Side-effects

 

see notes above

Dose

 

Expressed as levodopa, initially 50 mg 3–4 times daily (100 mg 3 times daily in advanced disease), increased by 100 mg daily once or twice weekly according to response; usual maintenance dose 400–800 mg daily in divided doses; elderly initially 50 mg once or twice daily, increased by 50 mg daily every 3–4 days according to response

Note

 

When transferring patients from another levodopa/dopa-decarboxylase inhibitor preparation, the previous preparation should be discontinued 12 hours before (although interval can be shorter)

Note

 

When administered as an adjunct to other antiparkinsonian drugs, once therapeutic effect apparent, the other drugs may be reduced or withdrawn

Note

 

When switching from modified-release levodopa to dispersible co-beneldopa, reduce dose by approx. 30%
