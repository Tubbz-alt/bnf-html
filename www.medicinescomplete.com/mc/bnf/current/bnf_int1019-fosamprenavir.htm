Fosamprenavir

Fosamprenavir has the following interaction information:

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Amiodarone

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of amiodarone (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Apixaban

avoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of apixaban

Aripiprazole

fosamprenavir possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)

Artemether with Lumefantrine

caution with fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atorvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when fosamprenavir given with atorvastatin

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly reduced by carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Coumarins

fosamprenavir may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Darifenacin

avoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of darifenacin

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by efavirenz

Ergotamine and Methysergide

increased risk of ergotism when fosamprenavir given with ergotamine and methysergide —avoid concomitant use

Etravirine

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir increased by etravirine (consider reducing dose of fosamprenavir)

Flecainide

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of flecainide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Itraconazole

plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when fosamprenavir given with itraconazole

Ketoconazole

fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of ketoconazole (also plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly increased)

Lidocaine

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of lidocaine —avoid concomitant use

Note: Interactions less likely when lidocaine used topically

Lopinavir

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by lopinavir , effect on lopinavir plasma concentration not predictable—avoid concomitant use

Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Methadone

fosamprenavir reduces plasma concentration of methadone

Midazolam

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

Nevirapine

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly reduced by nevirapine

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly reduced by phenobarbital

Pimozide

fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of pimozide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Posaconazole

fosamprenavir possibly reduces plasma concentration of posaconazole

Propafenone

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of propafenone (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Quetiapine

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quetiapine —manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use

Quinine

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quinine (increased risk of toxicity)

Ranolazine

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine —manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use

Rifabutin

fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (reduce dose of rifabutin)

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir significantly reduced by rifampicin —avoid concomitant use

Rivaroxaban

avoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban

Rosuvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when fosamprenavir given with rosuvastatin —manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

Sildenafil

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of sildenafil

Simvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when fosamprenavir given with simvastatin —avoid concomitant use

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by St John's wort —avoid concomitant use

Tacrolimus

fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Tadalafil

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of tadalafil

Telaprevir

avoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Telithromycin

avoidance of concomitant fosamprenavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment advised by manufacturer of telithromycin

Tipranavir

plasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by tipranavir

Tolterodine

avoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of tolterodine

Vardenafil

fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of vardenafil

Fosamprenavir belongs to Antivirals but Antivirals has no interactions information.
