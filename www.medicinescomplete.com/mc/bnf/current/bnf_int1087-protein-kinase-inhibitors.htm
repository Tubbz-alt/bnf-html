    Protein Kinase Inhibitors

    Protein Kinase Inhibitors has no specific interaction information.

    Protein Kinase Inhibitors belongs to Cytotoxics and will have the following interaction information:

    Clozapine

    avoid concomitant use of cytotoxics with clozapine (increased risk of agranulocytosis)

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Crizotinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

    Alfentanil

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with alfentanil

    Atazanavir

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by atazanavir—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Beta-blockers

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with beta-blockers

    Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

    Carbamazepine

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly reduced by carbamazepine—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Ciclosporin

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with ciclosporin

    Clarithromycin

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by clarithromycin—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Clonidine

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with clonidine

    Digoxin

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with digoxin

    Diltiazem

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with diltiazem

    Ergot Alkaloids

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with ergot alkaloids

    Fentanyl

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with fentanyl

    Grapefruit Juice

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by grapefruit juice—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Indinavir

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by indinavir—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Itraconazole

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by itraconazole—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Ketoconazole

    plasma concentration of crizotinib increased by ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use

    Mefloquine

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with mefloquine

    Midazolam

    crizotinib increases plasma concentration of midazolam

    Oestrogens

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises contraceptive effect of oestrogens possibly reduced

    Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

    Phenobarbital

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly reduced by phenobarbital—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Phenytoin

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly reduced by phenytoin—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Pilocarpine

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with pilocarpine

    Pimozide

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with pimozide

    Progestogens

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises contraceptive effect of progestogens possibly reduced

    Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

    Rifabutin

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly reduced by rifabutin—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Rifampicin

    plasma concentration of crizotinib reduced by rifampicin—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Ritonavir

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by ritonavir—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Saquinavir

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by saquinavir—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Sirolimus

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with sirolimus

    St John's Wort

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly reduced by St John's wort—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Tacrolimus

    manufacturer of crizotinib advises caution with tacrolimus

    Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

    Telithromycin

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by telithromycin—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Verapamil

    possible increased risk of bradycardia when crizotinib given with verapamil

    Voriconazole

    plasma concentration of crizotinib possibly increased by voriconazole—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Axitinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

    Dexamethasone

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly decreased by dexamethasone (increase dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Erythromycin

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly increased by erythromycin (reduce dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Grapefruit Juice

    Ketoconazole

    plasma concentration of axitinib increased by ketoconazole (reduce dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Phenobarbital

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly decreased by phenobarbital (increase dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Phenytoin

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly decreased by phenytoin (increase dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Rifabutin

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly decreased by rifabutin (increase dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Rifampicin

    plasma concentration of axitinib decreased by rifampicin (increase dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Ritonavir

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly increased by ritonavir (reduce dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Saquinavir

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly increased by saquinavir (reduce dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    St John's Wort

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly reduced by St John's wort—consider increasing dose of axitinib

    Telithromycin

    plasma concentration of axitinib possibly increased by telithromycin (reduce dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Vandetanib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

    Amiodarone

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant use

    Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

    Amisulpride

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with amisulpride—avoid concomitant use

    Arsenic Trioxide

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with arsenic trioxide—avoid concomitant use

    Artemether with Lumefantrine

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with artemether with lumefantrine—avoid concomitant use

    Carbamazepine

    manufacturer of vandetanib advises avoid concomitant use with carbamazepine (plasma concentration of vandetanib possibly reduced)

    Chlorpromazine

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with chlorpromazine—avoid concomitant use

    Disopyramide

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with disopyramide—avoid concomitant use

    Erythromycin

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with parenteral erythromycin—avoid concomitant use

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Haloperidol

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with haloperidol—avoid concomitant use

    Methadone

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with methadone—avoid concomitant use

    Mizolastine

    Moxifloxacin

    Ondansetron

    increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with ondansetron—avoid concomitant use

    Pentamidine Isetionate

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with pentamidine isetionate—avoid concomitant use

    Phenobarbital

    manufacturer of vandetanib advises avoid concomitant use with phenobarbital (plasma concentration of vandetanib possibly reduced)

    Pimozide

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use

    Proton Pump Inhibitors

    manufacturer of vandetanib advises avoid concomitant use with proton pump inhibitors (absorption of vandetanib possibly reduced)

    Rifampicin

    manufacturer of vandetanib advises avoid concomitant use with rifampicin (plasma concentration of vandetanib reduced)

    Sotalol

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with sotalol—avoid concomitant use

    St John's Wort

    manufacturer of vandetanib advises avoid concomitant use with St John's wort (plasma concentration of vandetanib possibly reduced)

    Sulpiride

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with sulpiride—avoid concomitant use

    Toremifene

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with toremifene—avoid concomitant use

    Zuclopenthixol

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when vandetanib given with zuclopenthixol—avoid concomitant use

    Sorafenib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

    Boceprevir

    avoidance of sorafenib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir

    Coumarins

    sorafenib possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Docetaxel

    sorafenib increases plasma concentration of docetaxel

    Doxorubicin

    sorafenib possibly increases plasma concentration of doxorubicin

    Irinotecan

    sorafenib possibly increases plasma concentration of irinotecan

    Neomycin

    bioavailability of sorafenib reduced by neomycin

    Rifampicin

    plasma concentration of sorafenib reduced by rifampicin

    Erlotinib belongs to Protein Kinase Inhibitors and has the following interaction information:

    Antacids

    plasma concentration of erlotinib possibly reduced by antacids—give antacids at least 4 hours before or 2 hours after erlotinib

    Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

    Boceprevir

    avoidance of erlotinib advised by manufacturer of boceprevir

    Capecitabine

    plasma concentration of erlotinib possibly increased by capecitabine

    Note: Capecitabine is a prodrug of fluorouracil

    Cimetidine

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with cimetidine

    Ciprofloxacin

    plasma concentration of erlotinib increased by ciprofloxacin

    Coumarins

    increased risk of bleeding when erlotinib given with coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Esomeprazole

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with esomeprazole

    Famotidine

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with famotidine

    Ketoconazole

    metabolism of erlotinib inhibited by ketoconazole (increased plasma concentration)

    Lansoprazole

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with lansoprazole

    NSAIDs

    increased risk of bleeding when erlotinib given with NSAIDs

    Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

    Nizatidine

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with nizatidine

    Omeprazole

    plasma concentration of erlotinib reduced by omeprazole—manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Pantoprazole

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with pantoprazole

    Rabeprazole

    manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use with rabeprazole

    Ranitidine

    plasma concentration of erlotinib reduced by ranitidine—manufacturer of erlotinib advises give at least 2 hours before or 10 hours after ranitidine

    Rifampicin

    metabolism of erlotinib accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)
