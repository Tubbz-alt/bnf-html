Significant changes

Significant changes have been made in the following sections for BNF No. 64:

The spelling of the stem 'sulph' has been amended to'sulf' throughout BNF to reflect the monographs of the British Pharmacopoeia 2012

Paracetamol poisoning [updated advice on management], Emergency Treatment of Poisoning

Proton pump inhibitors [increased risk of fractures with long-term use], section 1.3.5

Dabigatran for the prevention of stroke and systemic embolism in atrial fibrillation [NICE guidance], section 2.8.2

Apixaban for the prevention of venous thromboembolism after total hip or knee replacement [NICE guidance], section 2.8.2

Rivaroxaban for the prevention of stroke and systemic embolism in atrial fibrillation [NICE guidance], section 2.8.2

Roflumilast for the management of severe chronic obstructive pulmonary disease [NICE guidance], section 3.3.3

Adrenaline auto-injectors for anaphylaxis [brand prescribing recommended], section 3.4.3

Prevention of secondary case of Haemophilus influenzae type b disease, section 5.1, Table 2

HIV infection [initiation of treatment], section 5.3.1

Exenatide modified-release for the treatment of type 2 diabetes [NICE guidance], section 6.1.2.3

Epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) inhibitors: serious cases of keratitis and ulcerative keratitis [MHRA/CHM advice], section 8.1.5

Cetuximab, bevacizumab and panitumumab for the treatment of metastatic colorectal cancer after first-line chemotherapy [NICE guidance], section 8.1.5

Eribulin for the treatment of locally advanced or metastatic breast cancer [NICE guidance], section 8.1.5

Dasatinib, nilotinib and standard-dose imatinib for the first-line treatment of chronic myeloid leukaemia [NICE guidance], section 8.1.5

Dasatinib, high-dose imatinib and nilotinib for the treatment of imatinib-resistant chronic myeloid leukaemia (CML), and dasatinib and nilotinib for people with CML for whom treatment with imatinib has failed because of intolerance [NICE guidance], section 8.1.5

Cabazitaxel for hormone-refractory metastatic prostate cancer previously treated with a docetaxel-containing regimen [NICE guidance], section 8.1.5

Oral tacrolimus products: prescribe and dispense by brand name only, to minimise the risk of inadvertent switching between products, which has been associated with reports of toxicity and graft rejection [MHRA/CHM advice], section 8.2.2

Rituximab for the first-line treatment of stage III-IV follicular lymphoma [NICE guidance], section 8.2.3

Fingolimod: not recommended for patients at known risk of cardiovascular events. Advice for extended monitoring for those with significant bradycardia or heart block after the first dose [MHRA/CHM advice], section 8.2.4

Fingolimod for the treatment of highly active relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis [NICE guidance], section 8.2.4

Tocilizumab for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis [NICE guidance updated], section 10.1.3

Tocilizumab for the treatment of systemic juvenile idiopathic arthritis [NICE guidance], section 10.1.3

Febuxostat: serious hypersensitivity reactions [MHRA/CHM advice], section 10.1.4

Ranibizumab and pegaptanib for the treatment of age-related macular degeneration [NICE guidance updated], section 11.8.2

Haemophilus type b conjugate vaccine [invasive Haemophilus influenzae type b disease], section 14.4

Changes to drug interactions [new or amended drug entries will no longer be underlined in print versions], Appendix 1
