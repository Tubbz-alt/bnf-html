Calcium-channel Blockers

Calcium-channel Blockers has the following interaction information:

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

ACE Inhibitors

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with ACE inhibitors

Adrenergic Neurone Blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with adrenergic neurone blockers

Alcohol

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with alcohol

Aldesleukin

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with aldesleukin

Alpha-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with alpha-blockers , also increased risk of first-dose hypotension with post-synaptic alpha-blockers such as prazosin

Alprostadil

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with alprostadil

Anaesthetics, General

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with general anaesthetics

Note: See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists

Antipsychotics

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with antipsychotics

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with anxiolytics and hypnotics

Baclofen

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with baclofen

Beta-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Cimetidine

metabolism of calcium-channel blockers possibly inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Clarithromycin

metabolism of calcium-channel blockers possibly inhibited by clarithromycin (increased risk of side-effects)

Clonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with clonidine

Corticosteroids

hypotensive effect of calcium-channel blockers antagonised by corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Diazoxide

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with diazoxide

Diuretics

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with diuretics

Erythromycin

metabolism of calcium-channel blockers possibly inhibited by erythromycin (increased risk of side-effects)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Hydralazine

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with hydralazine

Isoflurane

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with isoflurane

Itraconazole

negative inotropic effect possibly increased when calcium-channel blockers given with itraconazole

Levodopa

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with levodopa

MAOIs

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Mefloquine

possible increased risk of bradycardia when calcium-channel blockers given with mefloquine

Methyldopa

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with methyldopa

Minoxidil

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with minoxidil

Moxisylyte

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with moxisylyte

Moxonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with moxonidine

Muscle Relaxants, non-depolarising

calcium-channel blockers possibly enhance effects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants

NSAIDs

hypotensive effect of calcium-channel blockers antagonised by NSAIDs

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Nitrates

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with nitrates

Oestrogens

hypotensive effect of calcium-channel blockers antagonised by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Phenobarbital

effects of calcium-channel blockers probably reduced by phenobarbital

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of calcium-channel blockers possibly increased by ritonavir

Sodium Nitroprusside

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with sodium nitroprusside

Telithromycin

metabolism of calcium-channel blockers possibly inhibited by telithromycin (increased risk of side-effects)

Theophylline

calcium-channel blockers possibly increase plasma concentration of theophylline (enhanced effect)

Tizanidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when calcium-channel blockers given with tizanidine

Diltiazem belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Alfentanil

diltiazem inhibits metabolism of alfentanil (risk of prolonged or delayed respiratory depression)

Amiodarone

increased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when diltiazem given with amiodarone

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

diltiazem possibly increases plasma concentration of tricyclics

Aprepitant

plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when diltiazem given with aprepitant

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of diltiazem increased by atazanavir (reduce dose of diltiazem)

Atorvastatin

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of atorvastatin —possible increased risk of myopathy

Beta-blockers

increased risk of AV block and bradycardia when diltiazem given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Buspirone

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)

Carbamazepine

diltiazem enhances effects of carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Cilostazol

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of cilostazol (consider reducing dose of cilostazol)

Colchicine

diltiazem possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)

Dantrolene

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when diltiazem given with intravenous dantrolene —manufacturer of diltiazem advises avoid concomitant use

Digoxin

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of digoxin

Dronedarone

increased risk of bradycardia and myocardial depression when diltiazem given with dronedarone

Dutasteride

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of dutasteride

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of diltiazem reduced by efavirenz

Eplerenone

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)

Fingolimod

possible increased risk of bradycardia when diltiazem given with fingolimod

Imipramine

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of imipramine

Ivabradine

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of ivabradine —avoid concomitant use

Lithium

neurotoxicity may occur when diltiazem given with lithium without increased plasma concentration of lithium

Methylprednisolone

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of methylprednisolone

Midazolam

diltiazem inhibits metabolism of midazolam (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Nifedipine

plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when diltiazem given with nifedipine

Phenytoin

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of phenytoin but also effect of diltiazem reduced

Ranolazine

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of ranolazine (consider reducing dose of ranolazine)

Rifampicin

metabolism of diltiazem accelerated by rifampicin (plasma concentration significantly reduced)

Simvastatin

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of simvastatin —possible increased risk of myopathy

Sirolimus

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of sirolimus

Tacrolimus

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

caution with diltiazem advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Theophylline

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of theophylline

Ticagrelor

diltiazem increases plasma concentration of ticagrelor

Verapamil belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Alcohol

verapamil possibly increases plasma concentration of alcohol

Aliskiren

verapamil increases plasma concentration of aliskiren

Amiodarone

increased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when verapamil given with amiodarone

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Anaesthetics, General

hypotensive effect of verapamil enhanced by general anaesthetics (also AV delay)

Note: See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

verapamil possibly increases plasma concentration of tricyclics

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of verapamil possibly increased by atazanavir

Beta-blockers

asystole, severe hypotension and heart failure when verapamil given with beta-blockers (see under Verapamil hydrochloride, section 2.6.2)

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Buspirone

verapamil increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)

Carbamazepine

verapamil enhances effects of carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

verapamil increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Colchicine

verapamil possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)

Dabigatran Etexilate

verapamil possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate (reduce dose of dabigatran etexilate)

Dantrolene

manufacturer of verapamil advises avoid concomitant use of intravenous dantrolene

Darifenacin

avoidance of verapamil advised by manufacturer of darifenacin

Digoxin

verapamil increases plasma concentration of digoxin , also increased risk of AV block and bradycardia

Disopyramide

increased risk of myocardial depression and asystole when verapamil given with disopyramide

Doxorubicin

verapamil possibly increases plasma concentration of doxorubicin

Dronedarone

increased risk of bradycardia and myocardial depression when verapamil given with dronedarone

Dutasteride

verapamil increases plasma concentration of dutasteride

Eplerenone

verapamil increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)

Everolimus

plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when verapamil given with everolimus

Fidaxomicin

avoidance of verapamil advised by manufacturer of fidaxomicin

Fingolimod

possible increased risk of bradycardia when verapamil given with fingolimod

Flecainide

increased risk of myocardial depression and asystole when verapamil given with flecainide

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of verapamil increased by grapefruit juice

Imipramine

verapamil increases plasma concentration of imipramine

Ivabradine

verapamil increases plasma concentration of ivabradine —avoid concomitant use

Lithium

neurotoxicity may occur when verapamil given with lithium without increased plasma concentration of lithium

Midazolam

verapamil inhibits metabolism of midazolam (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Muscle Relaxants, non-depolarising

verapamil enhances effects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants

Phenytoin

effects of verapamil reduced by phenytoin

Ranolazine

verapamil increases plasma concentration of ranolazine (consider reducing dose of ranolazine)

Rifampicin

metabolism of verapamil accelerated by rifampicin (plasma concentration significantly reduced)

Simvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when verapamil given with simvastatin

Sirolimus

plasma concentration of both drugs increased when verapamil given with sirolimus

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of verapamil significantly reduced by St John's wort

Sulfinpyrazone

plasma concentration of verapamil reduced by sulfinpyrazone

Suxamethonium

verapamil enhances effects of suxamethonium

Tacrolimus

verapamil possibly increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

caution with verapamil advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Theophylline

verapamil increases plasma concentration of theophylline (enhanced effect)

Calcium-channel Blockers (dihydropyridines) belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

effects of dihydropyridines probably reduced by carbamazepine

Itraconazole

metabolism of dihydropyridines possibly inhibited by itraconazole (increased plasma concentration)

Ketoconazole

metabolism of dihydropyridines possibly inhibited by ketoconazole (increased plasma concentration)

Amlodipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of amlodipine possibly increased by grapefruit juice

Sildenafil

enhanced hypotensive effect when amlodipine given with sildenafil

Simvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when amlodipine given with simvastatin

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of amlodipine possibly reduced by St John's wort

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of amlodipine increased by telaprevir (consider reducing dose of amlodipine)

Felodipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

effects of felodipine reduced by carbamazepine

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of felodipine increased by grapefruit juice

Itraconazole

metabolism of felodipine inhibited by itraconazole (increased plasma concentration)

Ketoconazole

metabolism of felodipine inhibited by ketoconazole (increased plasma concentration)

Phenytoin

effects of felodipine reduced by phenytoin

Tacrolimus

felodipine possibly increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

caution with felodipine advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Isradipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

effects of isradipine reduced by carbamazepine

Cimetidine

plasma concentration of isradipine increased by cimetidine (halve dose of isradipine)

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of isradipine increased by grapefruit juice

Phenobarbital

manufacturer of isradipine advises avoid concomitant use with phenobarbital

Phenytoin

manufacturer of isradipine advises avoid concomitant use with phenytoin

Rifampicin

metabolism of isradipine possibly accelerated by rifampicin (possible significantly reduced plasma concentration)

Lacidipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of lacidipine increased by grapefruit juice

Lercanidipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Ciclosporin

combination of lercanidipine with ciclosporin may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)—avoid concomitant use

Digoxin

lercanidipine increases plasma concentration of digoxin

Erythromycin

manufacturer of lercanidipine advises avoid concomitant use with erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of lercanidipine increased by grapefruit juice

Itraconazole

manufacturer of lercanidipine advises avoid concomitant use with itraconazole

Ketoconazole

manufacturer of lercanidipine advises avoid concomitant use with ketoconazole

Midazolam

absorption of lercanidipine increased by midazolam

Ritonavir

manufacturer of lercanidipine advises avoid concomitant use with ritonavir

Nicardipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

effects of nicardipine probably reduced by carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

nicardipine increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Digoxin

nicardipine increases plasma concentration of digoxin

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of nicardipine increased by grapefruit juice

Rifampicin

metabolism of nicardipine possibly accelerated by rifampicin (possible significantly reduced plasma concentration)

Tacrolimus

nicardipine possibly increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

caution with nicardipine advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Nifedipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Beta-blockers

possible severe hypotension and heart failure when nifedipine given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Carbamazepine

effects of nifedipine probably reduced by carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

plasma concentration of nifedipine possibly increased by ciclosporin (increased risk of toxicity including gingival hyperplasia)

Digoxin

nifedipine possibly increases plasma concentration of digoxin

Diltiazem

plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when nifedipine given with diltiazem

Dronedarone

nifedipine increases plasma concentration of dronedarone

Fluoxetine

metabolism of nifedipine possibly inhibited by fluoxetine (increased plasma concentration)

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of nifedipine increased by grapefruit juice

Insulin

glucose tolerance occasionally impaired when nifedipine given with insulin

Magnesium (parenteral)

profound hypotension reported with concomitant use of nifedipine and parenteral magnesium in pre-eclampsia

Micafungin

plasma concentration of nifedipine increased by micafungin

Rifampicin

metabolism of nifedipine accelerated by rifampicin (plasma concentration significantly reduced)

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of nifedipine reduced by St John's wort

Tacrolimus

nifedipine increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

caution with nifedipine advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Vardenafil

enhanced hypotensive effect when nifedipine given with vardenafil

Vincristine

nifedipine possibly inhibits metabolism of vincristine

Nimodipine belongs to Calcium-channel Blockers and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

manufacturer of nimodipine advises avoid concomitant use with carbamazepine (plasma concentration of nimodipine possibly reduced)

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of nimodipine increased by grapefruit juice

Phenobarbital

manufacturer of nimodipine advises avoid concomitant use with phenobarbital (plasma concentration of nimodipine reduced)

Phenytoin

manufacturer of nimodipine advises avoid concomitant use with phenytoin (plasma concentration of nimodipine possibly reduced)

Rifampicin

metabolism of nimodipine accelerated by rifampicin (plasma concentration significantly reduced)
