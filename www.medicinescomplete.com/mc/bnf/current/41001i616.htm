Muscle Relaxants

Muscle Relaxants has the following interaction information:

Lithiumeffects of muscle relaxants enhanced by lithium
Propranololeffects of muscle relaxants enhanced by propranolol

Baclofen belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

ACE Inhibitorsenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with ACE inhibitors
Adrenergic Neurone Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with adrenergic neurone blockers
Alcoholincreased sedative effect when baclofen given with alcohol
Alpha-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with alpha-blockers
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonistsenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Antidepressants, Tricyclicmuscle relaxant effect of baclofen enhanced by tricyclics
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsincreased sedative effect when baclofen given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Beta-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Calcium-channel Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Clonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with clonidine
Diazoxideenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with diazoxide
Diureticsenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with diuretics
Fentanylincreased sedative effect when baclofen given with fentanyl
Hydralazineenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with hydralazine
Ibuprofenexcretion of baclofen reduced by ibuprofen (increased risk of toxicity)
Levodopapossible agitation, confusion and hallucinations when baclofen given with levodopa
Lithium baclofen possibly aggravates hyperkinesis caused by lithium
Memantineeffects of baclofen possibly modified by memantine
Methyldopaenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with methyldopa
Minoxidilenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with minoxidil
Morphineincreased sedative effect when baclofen given with morphine
Moxonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with moxonidine
Nitratesenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with nitrates
NSAIDsexcretion of baclofen possibly reduced by NSAIDs (increased risk of toxicity)See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Sodium Nitroprussideenhanced hypotensive effect when baclofen given with sodium nitroprusside

Dantrolene belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

Diltiazempossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when intravenous dantrolene given with diltiazem—manufacturer of diltiazem advises avoid concomitant use
Memantineeffects of dantrolene possibly modified by memantine
Verapamilavoidance of intravenous dantrolene advised by manufacturer of verapamil

Methocarbamol belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

Alcoholincreased sedative effect when methocarbamol given with alcohol

Tizanidine belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

ACE Inhibitorsenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with ACE inhibitors
Adrenergic Neurone Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with adrenergic neurone blockers
Alcoholincreased sedative effect when tizanidine given with alcohol
Alpha-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with alpha-blockers
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonistsenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsincreased sedative effect when tizanidine given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Beta-blockerspossible enhanced hypotensive effect and bradycardia when tizanidine given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Calcium-channel Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Cardiac Glycosidespossible increased risk of bradycardia when tizanidine given with cardiac glycosides
Ciprofloxacinplasma concentration of tizanidine increased by ciprofloxacin (increased risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Clonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with clonidine
Diazoxideenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with diazoxide
Diureticsenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with diuretics
Fluvoxamineplasma concentration of tizanidine increased by fluvoxamine (increased risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Hydralazineenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with hydralazine
Methyldopaenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with methyldopa
Minoxidilenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with minoxidil
Moxonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with moxonidine
Nitratesenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with nitrates
Norfloxacinplasma concentration of tizanidine possibly increased by norfloxacin (increased risk of toxicity)
Oestrogensplasma concentration of tizanidine possibly increased by oestrogens (increased risk of toxicity)Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Progestogensplasma concentration of tizanidine possibly increased by progestogens (increased risk of toxicity)Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5
Sodium Nitroprussideenhanced hypotensive effect when tizanidine given with sodium nitroprusside

Muscle Relaxants, non-depolarising belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

Aminoglycosideseffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by aminoglycosides
Anaesthetics, General (volatile liquids)effects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by volatile liquid general anaesthetics
Calcium-channel Blockerseffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants possibly enhanced by calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Carbamazepinemuscle relaxant effect of non-depolarising muscle relaxants antagonised by carbamazepine (accelerated recovery from neuromuscular blockade)
Clindamycineffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by clindamycin
Donepezileffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants possibly antagonised by donepezil
Edrophoniumeffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants antagonised by edrophonium
Magnesium (parenteral)effects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by parenteral magnesium
Neostigmineeffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants antagonised by neostigmine
Phenytoinmuscle relaxant effect of non-depolarising muscle relaxants antagonised by phenytoin (accelerated recovery from neuromuscular blockade)
Piperacillineffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by piperacillin
Polymyxinseffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by polymyxins
Pyridostigmineeffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants antagonised by pyridostigmine
Rivastigmineeffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants antagonised by rivastigmine
Verapamileffects of non-depolarising muscle relaxants enhanced by verapamil

Atracurium belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

Ketamineeffects of atracurium enhanced by ketamine

Cisatracurium belongs to Muscle Relaxants but has no specific interaction information.

Mivacurium belongs to Muscle Relaxants but has no specific interaction information.

Pancuronium belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

Corticosteroidseffects of pancuronium possibly antagonised by corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Rocuronium belongs to Muscle Relaxants but has no specific interaction information.

Vecuronium belongs to Muscle Relaxants and has the following interaction information:

Corticosteroidseffects of vecuronium possibly antagonised by corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Muscle Relaxants, depolarising belongs to Muscle Relaxants but has no specific interaction information.

Magnesium (parenteral)effects of suxamethonium enhanced by parenteral magnesium
Metoclopramideeffects of suxamethonium enhanced by metoclopramide
Neostigmineeffects of suxamethonium enhanced by neostigmine
Phenelzineeffects of suxamethonium enhanced by phenelzine

Promazineeffects of suxamethonium possibly enhanced by promazine

Pyridostigmineeffects of suxamethonium enhanced by pyridostigmine

Rivastigmineeffects of suxamethonium enhanced by rivastigmine
