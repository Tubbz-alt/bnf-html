Erythromycin

Erythromycin has the following interaction information:

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Alfentanil

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of alfentanil

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Amisulpride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with amisulpride —avoid concomitant use

Arsenic Trioxide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with arsenic trioxide

Atomoxetine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with atomoxetine

Atorvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when erythromycin given with atorvastatin

Bromocriptine

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of bromocriptine (increased risk of toxicity)

Buspirone

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)

Cabergoline

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of cabergoline (increased risk of toxicity)

Calcium-channel Blockers

erythromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of calcium-channel blockers (increased risk of side-effects)

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Carbamazepine

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

erythromycin inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)

Cilostazol

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of cilostazol (consider reducing dose of cilostazol)

Cimetidine

plasma concentration of erythromycin increased by cimetidine (increased risk of toxicity, including deafness)

Clopidogrel

erythromycin possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

Clozapine

erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of clozapine (possible increased risk of convulsions)

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Colchicine

erythromycin possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)

Corticosteroids

erythromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Coumarins

erythromycin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Darifenacin

erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of darifenacin

Dienogest

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of dienogest

Disopyramide

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of disopyramide (increased risk of toxicity)

Docetaxel

in vitro studies suggest a possible interaction between erythromycin and docetaxel (consult docetaxel product literature)

Domperidone

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with domperidone

Dronedarone

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of dronedarone (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Eletriptan

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Eplerenone

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)

Estradiol

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of estradiol

Everolimus

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of everolimus

Fidaxomicin

avoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of fidaxomicin

Fluconazole

avoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of fluconazole

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Galantamine

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of galantamine

Ivabradine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with ivabradine —avoid concomitant use

Lercanidipine

avoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine

Loratadine

manufacturer of loratadine advises erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of loratadine

Methylprednisolone

erythromycin inhibits the metabolism of methylprednisolone

Midazolam

erythromycin inhibits metabolism of midazolam (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Mizolastine

erythromycin inhibits metabolism of mizolastine —avoid concomitant use

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Pentamidine Isetionate

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with pentamidine isetionate

Pimozide

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Pravastatin

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of pravastatin

Quetiapine

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of quetiapine —manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use

Rifabutin

erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of toxicity—reduce rifabutin dose)

Rilpivirine

avoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of rilpivirine (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly increased)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of erythromycin possibly increased by ritonavir

Rosuvastatin

erythromycin reduces plasma concentration of rosuvastatin

Rupatadine

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of rupatadine

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Sildenafil

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of sildenafil —reduce initial dose of sildenafil

Simvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when erythromycin given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)

Sirolimus

plasma concentration of both drugs increased when erythromycin given with sirolimus

Sulpiride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with sulpiride

Tacrolimus

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Tadalafil

erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of tadalafil

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of both drugs possibly increased when erythromycin given with telaprevir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Theophylline

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of theophylline (also theophylline may reduce absorption of oralerythromycin)

Ticagrelor

erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of ticagrelor

Tolterodine

avoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of tolterodine

Valproate

erythromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of valproate (increased plasma concentration)

Vandetanib

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with vandetanib —avoid concomitant use

Vardenafil

erythromycin increases plasma concentration of vardenafil (reduce dose of vardenafil)

Vinblastine

erythromycin increases toxicity of vinblastine —avoid concomitant use

Zafirlukast

erythromycin reduces plasma concentration of zafirlukast

Zopiclone

erythromycin inhibits the metabolism of zopiclone

Zuclopenthixol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with zuclopenthixol —avoid concomitant use

Erythromycin belongs to Macrolides and will have the following interaction information:

Note: See also Telithromycin

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of macrolides advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Bromocriptine

macrolides possibly increase plasma concentration of bromocriptine (increased risk of toxicity)

Cabergoline

macrolides possibly increase plasma concentration of cabergoline (increased risk of toxicity)

Ciclosporin

macrolides possibly inhibit metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)

Digoxin

macrolides increase plasma concentration of digoxin (increased risk of toxicity)

Droperidol

avoidance of macrolides advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Ergotamine and Methysergide

increased risk of ergotism when macrolides given with ergotamine and methysergide —avoid concomitant use

Mizolastine

macrolides possibly inhibit metabolism of mizolastine (avoid concomitant use)

Reboxetine

avoidance of macrolides advised by manufacturer of reboxetine

Erythromycin belongs to Antibacterials and will have the following interaction information:

Typhoid Vaccine (oral)

antibacterials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine —see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4
