Cyclopentolate

Cyclopentolate has no specific interaction information.

Cyclopentolate belongs to Antimuscarinics and will have the following interaction information:

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with tricyclics

Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)

possible increased antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with tricyclic-related antidepressants

Antihistamines

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with antihistamines

Note: Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)

Clozapine

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with clozapine

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Codeine

possible increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with codeine

Disopyramide

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with disopyramide

Domperidone

antimuscarinics antagonise effects of domperidone on gastro-intestinal activity

Haloperidol

antimuscarinics possibly reduce effects of haloperidol

Ketoconazole

antimuscarinics reduce absorption of ketoconazole

Levodopa

antimuscarinics possibly reduce absorption of levodopa

MAOIs

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Memantine

effects of antimuscarinics possibly enhanced by memantine

Metoclopramide

antimuscarinics antagonise effects of metoclopramide on gastro-intestinal activity

Nefopam

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antimuscarinics given with nefopam

Nitrates

antimuscarinics possibly reduce effects of sublingual tablets of nitrates (failure to dissolve under tongue owing to dry mouth)

Parasympathomimetics

antimuscarinics antagonise effects of parasympathomimetics

Phenothiazines

antimuscarinics reduce plasma concentration of phenothiazines , but risk of antimuscarinic side-effects increased
