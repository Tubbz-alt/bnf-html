Fosamprenavir

Fosamprenavir has the following interaction information:

Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Amiodarone fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of amiodarone (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Apixabanavoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Aripiprazole fosamprenavir possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)
Artemether with Lumefantrinecaution with fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when fosamprenavir given with atorvastatin
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly reduced by carbamazepine
Ciclosporin fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Coumarins fosamprenavir may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Darifenacinavoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of darifenacin
Efavirenzplasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by efavirenz
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when fosamprenavir given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Etravirineplasma concentration of fosamprenavir increased by etravirine (consider reducing dose of fosamprenavir)
Flecainide fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of flecainide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Itraconazoleplasma concentration of both drugs may increase when fosamprenavir given with itraconazole
Ketoconazole fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of ketoconazole (also plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly increased)
Lidocaine fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of lidocaine—avoid concomitant useInteractions less likely when lidocaine used topically
Lopinavirplasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by lopinavir , effect on lopinavir plasma concentration not predictable—avoid concomitant useIn combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Methadone fosamprenavir reduces plasma concentration of methadone
Midazolam fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oral midazolam)
Nevirapineplasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly reduced by nevirapine
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly reduced by phenobarbital
Pimozide fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of pimozide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Propafenone fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of propafenone (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Quetiapine fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Quinine fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quinine (increased risk of toxicity)
Ranolazine fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use
Rifabutin fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (reduce dose of rifabutin)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of fosamprenavir significantly reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use
Rivaroxabanavoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban
Rosuvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when fosamprenavir given with rosuvastatin—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Sildenafil fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of sildenafil
Simvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when fosamprenavir given with simvastatin—avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Tacrolimus fosamprenavir increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Tadalafil fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of tadalafil
Telapreviravoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of telaprevir
Telithromycinavoidance of concomitant fosamprenavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment advised by manufacturer of telithromycin
Tipranavirplasma concentration of fosamprenavir reduced by tipranavir
Tolterodineavoidance of fosamprenavir advised by manufacturer of tolterodine
Vardenafil fosamprenavir possibly increases plasma concentration of vardenafil

Fosamprenavir belongs to Antivirals but Antivirals has no interactions information.
