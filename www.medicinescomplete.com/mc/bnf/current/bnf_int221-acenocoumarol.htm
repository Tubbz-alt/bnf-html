Acenocoumarol

Acenocoumarol has no specific interaction information.

Acenocoumarol belongs to Coumarins and will have the following interaction information:

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Acitretin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly reduced by acitretin

Alcohol

anticoagulant control with coumarins may be affected by major changes in consumption of alcohol

Allopurinol

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by allopurinol

Amiodarone

metabolism of coumarins inhibited by amiodarone (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Anabolic Steroids

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by anabolic steroids

Antidepressants, SSRI

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by SSRIs

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may be enhanced or reduced by tricyclics

Aspirin

increased risk of bleeding when coumarins given with aspirin (due to antiplatelet effect)

Azathioprine

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly reduced by azathioprine

Azithromycin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by azithromycin

Aztreonam

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by aztreonam

Bicalutamide

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by bicalutamide

Bosentan

monitoring anticoagulant effect of coumarins recommended by manufacturer of bosentan

Carbamazepine

metabolism of coumarins accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced anticoagulant effect)

Cephalosporins

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by cephalosporins

Chloral

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may transiently be enhanced by chloral

Chloramphenicol

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by chloramphenicol

Cimetidine

metabolism of coumarins inhibited by cimetidine (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

Ciprofloxacin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by ciprofloxacin

Clarithromycin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by clarithromycin

Clopidogrel

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced due to antiplatelet action of clopidogrel

Colestyramine

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may be enhanced or reduced by colestyramine

Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption

Corticosteroids

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may be enhanced or reduced by corticosteroids (high-dose corticosteroids enhance anticoagulant effect)

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Cranberry Juice

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by cranberry juice —avoid concomitant use

Danazol

metabolism of coumarins inhibited by danazol (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

Dipyridamole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced due to antiplatelet action of dipyridamole

Disulfiram

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by disulfiram

Dronedarone

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by dronedarone

Enteral Foods

anticoagulant effect of coumarins antagonised by vitamin K (present in some enteral feeds )

Erlotinib

increased risk of bleeding when coumarins given with erlotinib

Erythromycin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Esomeprazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by esomeprazole

Etoposide

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by etoposide

Ezetimibe

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by ezetimibe

Fibrates

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by fibrates

Fluconazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by fluconazole

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fluorouracil

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by fluorouracil

Flutamide

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by flutamide

Fluvastatin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by fluvastatin

Fosamprenavir

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may be enhanced or reduced by fosamprenavir

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Griseofulvin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins reduced by griseofulvin

Ifosfamide

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by ifosfamide

Iloprost

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by iloprost

Itraconazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by itraconazole

Ketoconazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by ketoconazole

Lactulose

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by lactulose

Levofloxacin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by levofloxacin

Mercaptopurine

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly reduced by mercaptopurine

Methylphenidate

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by methylphenidate

Metronidazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by metronidazole

Note: Interactions do not apply to topical metronidazole preparations

Miconazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by miconazole (miconazole oral gel and possibly vaginal and topical formulations absorbed)

Mitotane

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly reduced by mitotane

NSAIDs

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by NSAIDs

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Nalidixic Acid

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by nalidixic acid

Neomycin

experience in anticoagulant clinics suggests that INR possibly altered when coumarins are given with neomycin (given for local action on gut)

Norfloxacin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by norfloxacin

Oestrogens

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may be enhanced or reduced by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Ofloxacin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by ofloxacin

Omeprazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by omeprazole

Orlistat

monitoring anticoagulant effect of coumarins recommended by manufacturer of orlistat

Pantoprazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by pantoprazole

Paracetamol

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by prolonged regular use of paracetamol

Penicillins

an interaction between coumarins and broad-spectrum penicillins has not been demonstrated in studies, but common experience in anticoagulant clinics is that INR can be altered

Phenobarbital

metabolism of coumarins accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced anticoagulant effect)

Phenytoin

metabolism of coumarins accelerated by phenytoin (possibility of reduced anticoagulant effect, but enhancement also reported)

Prasugrel

possible increased risk of bleeding when coumarins given with prasugrel

Progestogens

anticoagulant effect of coumarins may be enhanced or reduced by progestogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

Propafenone

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by propafenone

Raloxifene

anticoagulant effect of coumarins antagonised by raloxifene

Rifamycins

metabolism of coumarins accelerated by rifamycins (reduced anticoagulant effect)

Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

Ritonavir

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by ritonavir

Rosuvastatin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by rosuvastatin

Simvastatin

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by simvastatin

Sorafenib

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by sorafenib

St John's Wort

anticoagulant effect of coumarins reduced by St John's wort (avoid concomitant use)

Sucralfate

absorption of coumarins possibly reduced by sucralfate (reduced anticoagulant effect)

Sulfinpyrazone

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by sulfinpyrazone

Sulfonamides

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by sulfonamides

Sulfonylureas

coumarins possibly enhance hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas , also possible changes to anticoagulant effect

Tamoxifen

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by tamoxifen

Testolactone

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by testolactone

Testosterone

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by testosterone

Tetracyclines

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by tetracyclines

Thyroid Hormones

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by thyroid hormones

Tigecycline

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by tigecycline

Toremifene

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by toremifene

Tramadol

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by tramadol

Trimethoprim

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by trimethoprim

Valproate

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by valproate

Vitamin E

anticoagulant effect of coumarins possibly enhanced by vitamin E

Vitamin K (Phytomenadione)

anticoagulant effect of coumarins antagonised by vitamin K

Voriconazole

anticoagulant effect of coumarins enhanced by voriconazole

Acenocoumarol belongs to Anticoagulants and will have the following interaction information:

Diclofenac

increased risk of haemorrhage when anticoagulants given with intravenous diclofenac (avoid concomitant use, including low-dose heparins)

Ketorolac

increased risk of haemorrhage when anticoagulants given with ketorolac (avoid concomitant use, including low-dose heparins)
