Tipranavir

Tipranavir has the following interaction information:

Abacavir tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of abacavir
Antacidsabsorption of tipranavir reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Apixabanavoidance of tipranavir advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Artemether with Lumefantrinecaution with tipranavir advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atazanavirplasma concentration of tipranavir increased by atazanavir (also plasma concentration of atazanavir reduced)
Atorvastatin tipranavir increases plasma concentration of atorvastatin (increased risk of myopathy—reduce dose of atorvastatin)
Bosentanmanufacturer of tipranavir advises avoid concomitant use with bosentan
Buprenorphineplasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by buprenorphine
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by carbamazepine
Clarithromycin tipranavir increases plasma concentration of clarithromycin (reduce dose of clarithromycin in renal impairment), also plasma concentration of tipranavir increased by clarithromycin
Darifenacinavoidance of tipranavir advised by manufacturer of darifenacin
Didanosine tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of didanosine Antacids in tablet formulation may affect absorption of other drugs
Esomeprazole tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of esomeprazole
Etravirine tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of etravirine , also plasma concentration of tipranavir increased (avoid concomitant use)
Fluconazoleplasma concentration of tipranavir increased by fluconazole In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Fosamprenavir tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of fosamprenavir Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Lopinavir tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of lopinavir In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Metoprololmanufacturer of tipranavir advises avoid concomitant use with metoprolol for heart failure
Omeprazole tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of omeprazole
Quetiapine tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Quinine tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quinine (increased risk of toxicity)
Ranolazine tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use
Rifabutin tipranavir increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (reduce dose of rifabutin)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use
Rivaroxabanavoidance of tipranavir advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban
Rosuvastatin tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of rosuvastatin—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Salmeterolmanufacturer of tipranavir advises avoid concomitant use with salmeterol
Saquinavir tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of saquinavir
Simvastatin tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of simvastatin—avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Telithromycinavoidance of concomitant tipranavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment advised by manufacturer of telithromycin
Vardenafilmanufacturer of tipranavir advises caution with vardenafil
Vitamin Eincreased risk of bleeding when tipranavir given with high doses of vitamin E
Zidovudine tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of zidovudine Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Tipranavir belongs to Antivirals but Antivirals has no interactions information.
