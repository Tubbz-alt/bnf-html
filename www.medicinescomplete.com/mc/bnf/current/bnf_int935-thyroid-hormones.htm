Thyroid Hormones

Thyroid Hormones has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone

for concomitant use of thyroid hormones and amiodarone see section 2.3.2

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Amitriptyline

thyroid hormones enhance effects of amitriptyline

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

thyroid hormones possibly enhance effects of tricyclics

Carbamazepine

metabolism of thyroid hormones accelerated by carbamazepine (may increase requirements for thyroid hormones in hypothyroidism)

Colestipol

absorption of thyroid hormones reduced by colestipol

Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestipol to reduce possible interference with absorption

Colestyramine

absorption of thyroid hormones reduced by colestyramine

Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption

Coumarins

thyroid hormones enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Imipramine

thyroid hormones enhance effects of imipramine

Oestrogens

requirements for thyroid hormones in hypothyroidism may be increased by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Phenindione

thyroid hormones enhance anticoagulant effect of phenindione

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Phenobarbital

metabolism of thyroid hormones accelerated by phenobarbital (may increase requirements for thyroid hormones in hypothyroidism)

Phenytoin

metabolism of thyroid hormones accelerated by phenytoin (may increase requirements in hypothyroidism), also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased

Levothyroxine belongs to Thyroid Hormones and has the following interaction information:

Antacids

absorption of levothyroxine possibly reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Calcium Salts

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by calcium salts

Note: see also Antacids

Cimetidine

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by cimetidine

Colesevelam

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by colesevelam

Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption

Imatinib

plasma concentration of levothyroxine possibly reduced by imatinib

Iron

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by oral iron (give at least 2 hours apart)

Lanthanum

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by lanthanum (give at least 2 hours apart)

Orlistat

possible increased risk of hypothyroidism when levothyroxine given with orlistat

Polystyrene Sulfonate Resins

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by polystyrene sulfonate resins

Propranolol

levothyroxine accelerates metabolism of propranolol

Rifampicin

metabolism of levothyroxine accelerated by rifampicin (may increase requirements for levothyroxine in hypothyroidism)

Sevelamer

absorption of levothyroxine possibly reduced by sevelamer

Sucralfate

absorption of levothyroxine reduced by sucralfate

Liothyronine belongs to Thyroid Hormones but has no specific interaction information.
