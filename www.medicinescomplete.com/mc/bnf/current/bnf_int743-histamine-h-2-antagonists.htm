    Histamine H2-antagonists

    Histamine H2-antagonists has the following interaction information:

    Atazanavir

    histamine H2-antagonists reduce plasma concentration of atazanavir

    Cefpodoxime

    histamine H2-antagonists reduce absorption of cefpodoxime

    Histamine

    histamine H2-antagonists theoretically antagonise effects of histamine—manufacturer of histamine advises avoid concomitant use

    Itraconazole

    histamine H2-antagonists reduce absorption of itraconazole

    Ketoconazole

    histamine H2-antagonists reduce absorption of ketoconazole

    Lapatinib

    histamine H2-antagonists possibly reduce absorption of lapatinib

    Pazopanib

    histamine H2-antagonists possibly reduce absorption of pazopanib—manufacturer of pazopanib advises give at least 2 hours before or 10 hours after histamine H2-antagonists

    Posaconazole

    avoidance of histamine H2-antagonists advised by manufacturer of posaconazole (plasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced)

    Raltegravir

    histamine H2-antagonists possibly increase plasma concentration of raltegravir—manufacturer of raltegravir advises avoid concomitant use

    Rilpivirine

    avoidance of histamine H2-antagonists for 12 hours before or 4 hours after rilpivirine advised by manufacturer of rilpivirine—consult product literature

    Ulipristal

    avoidance of histamine H2-antagonists advised by manufacturer of high-dose ulipristal (contraceptive effect of ulipristal possibly reduced)

    Histamine H2-antagonists belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs but Ulcer-healing Drugs has no interactions information.

    Cimetidine belongs to Histamine H2-antagonists and has the following interaction information:

    Amiodarone

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of amiodarone

    Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

    Amitriptyline

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of amitriptyline (increased plasma concentration)

    Antidepressants, Tricyclic

    cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of tricyclics

    Antipsychotics

    cimetidine possibly enhances effects of antipsychotics

    Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

    Artemether with Lumefantrine

    avoidance of cimetidine advised by manufacturer of artemether with lumefantrine

    Benzodiazepines

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of benzodiazepines (increased plasma concentration)

    Calcium-channel Blockers

    cimetidine possibly inhibits metabolism of calcium-channel blockers (increased plasma concentration)

    Carbamazepine

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of carbamazepine (increased plasma concentration)

    Carmustine

    cimetidine possibly enhances myelosuppressive effects of carmustine

    Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine (increased plasma concentration)

    Chlorpromazine

    cimetidine possibly enhances effects of chlorpromazine

    Ciclosporin

    cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

    Citalopram

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of citalopram

    Clomethiazole

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of clomethiazole (increased plasma concentration)

    Clopidogrel

    cimetidine possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

    Clozapine

    cimetidine possibly enhances effects of clozapine

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Coumarins

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of coumarins (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Doxepin

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of doxepin (increased plasma concentration)

    Epirubicin

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of epirubicin

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of ergotism when cimetidine given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use

    Erlotinib

    avoidance of cimetidine advised by manufacturer of erlotinib

    Erythromycin

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of erythromycin (increased risk of toxicity, including deafness)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Escitalopram

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of escitalopram

    Fampridine

    avoidance of cimetidine advised by manufacturer of fampridine

    Flecainide

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of flecainide (increased plasma concentration)

    Fluorouracil

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of fluorouracil (increased plasma concentration)

    Imipramine

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of imipramine (increased plasma concentration)

    Isradipine

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of isradipine (halve dose of isradipine)

    Labetalol

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of labetalol

    Levothyroxine

    cimetidine reduces absorption of levothyroxine

    Lidocaine

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of lidocaine (increased risk of toxicity)

    Note: Interactions less likely when lidocaine used topically

    Lomustine

    cimetidine possibly enhances myelosuppressive effects of lomustine

    Loratadine

    manufacturer of loratadine advises cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of loratadine

    Mebendazole

    cimetidine possibly inhibits metabolism of mebendazole (increased plasma concentration)

    Melatonin

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of melatonin

    Metformin

    cimetidine reduces excretion of metformin (increased plasma concentration)

    Metoprolol

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of metoprolol

    Metronidazole

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of metronidazole (increased plasma concentration)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to topical metronidazole preparations

    Mirtazapine

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of mirtazapine

    Moclobemide

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of moclobemide (halve dose of moclobemide)

    Nortriptyline

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of nortriptyline (increased plasma concentration)

    Octreotide

    absorption of cimetidine possibly delayed by octreotide

    Opioid Analgesics

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of opioid analgesics (increased plasma concentration)

    Phenytoin

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of phenytoin (increased plasma concentration)

    Posaconazole

    cimetidine reduces plasma concentration of posaconazole

    Pramipexole

    cimetidine reduces excretion of pramipexole (increased plasma concentration)

    Propafenone

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of propafenone

    Propranolol

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of propranolol

    Quinine

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of quinine (increased plasma concentration)

    Rifampicin

    metabolism of cimetidine accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

    Roflumilast

    cimetidine inhibits the metabolism of roflumilast

    Saquinavir

    cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of saquinavir

    Sertraline

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of sertraline

    Sildenafil

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of sildenafil (consider reducing dose of sildenafil)

    Sulfonylureas

    cimetidine enhances hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas

    Terbinafine

    cimetidine increases plasma concentration of terbinafine

    Theophylline

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of theophylline (increased plasma concentration)

    Tolazoline

    cimetidine antagonises effects of tolazoline

    Valproate

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of valproate (increased plasma concentration)

    Zaleplon

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of zaleplon (increased plasma concentration)

    Zolmitriptan

    cimetidine inhibits metabolism of zolmitriptan (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

    Famotidine belongs to Histamine H2-antagonists and has the following interaction information:

    Dasatinib

    famotidine possibly reduces plasma concentration of dasatinib

    Erlotinib

    avoidance of famotidine advised by manufacturer of erlotinib

    Nizatidine belongs to Histamine H2-antagonists and has the following interaction information:

    Erlotinib

    avoidance of nizatidine advised by manufacturer of erlotinib

    Ranitidine belongs to Histamine H2-antagonists and has the following interaction information:

    Erlotinib

    ranitidine reduces plasma concentration of erlotinib—manufacturer of erlotinib advises give at least 2 hours before or 10 hours after ranitidine

    Gefitinib

    ranitidine reduces plasma concentration of gefitinib

    Tolazoline

    ranitidine antagonises effects of tolazoline
