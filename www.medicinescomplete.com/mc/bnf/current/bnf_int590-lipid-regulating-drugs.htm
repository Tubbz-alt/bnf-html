    Lipid-regulating Drugs

    Lipid-regulating Drugs has no specific interaction information.

    Ezetimibe belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Ciclosporin

    plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when ezetimibe given with ciclosporin

    Coumarins

    ezetimibe possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Fibrates

    increased risk of cholelithiasis and gallbladder disease when ezetimibe given with fibrates—discontinue if suspected

    Bile Acid Sequestrants belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs but has no specific interaction information.

    Colesevelam belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption

    Ciclosporin

    colesevelam reduces absorption of ciclosporin

    Ethinylestradiol

    colesevelam reduces absorption of ethinylestradiol

    Glibenclamide

    colesevelam reduces absorption of glibenclamide

    Levothyroxine

    colesevelam reduces absorption of levothyroxine

    Phenytoin

    colesevelam possibly reduces absorption of phenytoin

    Colestipol belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestipol to reduce possible interference with absorption

    Bile Acids

    colestipol possibly reduces absorption of bile acids

    Cardiac Glycosides

    colestipol possibly reduces absorption of cardiac glycosides

    Diuretics, Thiazide and related

    colestipol reduces absorption of thiazides and related diuretics (give at least 2 hours apart)

    Tetracycline

    colestipol possibly reduces absorption of tetracycline

    Thyroid Hormones

    colestipol reduces absorption of thyroid hormones

    Colestyramine belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption

    Acarbose

    colestyramine possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of acarbose

    Bile Acids

    colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of bile acids

    Cardiac Glycosides

    colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of cardiac glycosides

    Coumarins

    colestyramine may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Diuretics, Thiazide and related

    colestyramine reduces absorption of thiazides and related diuretics (give at least 2 hours apart)

    Leflunomide

    colestyramine significantly decreases effect of leflunomide (enhanced elimination)—avoid unless drug elimination desired

    Note: Increased risk of toxicity with other haematotoxic and hepatotoxic drugs

    Meloxicam

    colestyramine increases the excretion of meloxicam

    Mycophenolate

    colestyramine reduces absorption of mycophenolate

    Paracetamol

    colestyramine reduces absorption of paracetamol

    Phenindione

    colestyramine may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of phenindione

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Raloxifene

    colestyramine reduces absorption of raloxifene (manufacturer of raloxifene advises avoid concomitant administration)

    Tetracycline

    colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of tetracycline

    Thyroid Hormones

    colestyramine reduces absorption of thyroid hormones

    Valproate

    colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of valproate

    Vancomycin

    colestyramine antagonises effects of oral vancomycin

    Fibrates belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Colchicine

    possible increased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with colchicine

    Coumarins

    fibrates enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Daptomycin

    increased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with daptomycin (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Ezetimibe

    increased risk of cholelithiasis and gallbladder disease when fibrates given with ezetimibe—discontinue if suspected

    Insulin

    fibrates may improve glucose tolerance and have an additive effect with insulin

    Phenindione

    fibrates enhance anticoagulant effect of phenindione

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Rosuvastatin

    increased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with rosuvastatin (see Dose under Rosuvastatin, section 2.12)

    Statins

    increased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with statins

    Sulfonylureas

    fibrates may improve glucose tolerance and have an additive effect with sulfonylureas

    Bezafibrate belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of renal impairment when bezafibrate given with ciclosporin

    Simvastatin

    possible increased risk of myopathy when bezafibrate given with simvastatin (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Ciprofibrate belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Simvastatin

    possible increased risk of myopathy when ciprofibrate given with simvastatin (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Fenofibrate belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of renal impairment when fenofibrate given with ciclosporin

    Gemfibrozil belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Atorvastatin

    increased risk of myopathy when gemfibrozil given with atorvastatin (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Bexarotene

    gemfibrozil increases plasma concentration of bexarotene—avoid concomitant use

    Fluvastatin

    increased risk of myopathy when gemfibrozil given with fluvastatin (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Montelukast

    gemfibrozil increases plasma concentration of montelukast

    Nateglinide

    gemfibrozil possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide

    Pravastatin

    increased risk of myopathy when gemfibrozil given with pravastatin (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Repaglinide

    increased risk of severe hypoglycaemia when gemfibrozil given with repaglinide—avoid concomitant use

    Simvastatin

    increased risk of myopathy when gemfibrozil given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)

    Statins belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Colchicine

    possible increased risk of myopathy when statins given with colchicine

    Daptomycin

    increased risk of myopathy when statins given with daptomycin (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Fibrates

    increased risk of myopathy when statins given with fibrates

    Fusidic Acid

    risk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis when statins given with fusidic acid—avoid concomitant use and for 7 days after last fusidic acid dose

    Nicotinic Acid

    increased risk of myopathy when statins given with nicotinic acid (applies to lipid regulating doses of nicotinic acid)

    Note: Interactions apply to lipid-regulating doses of nicotinic acid

    Rosuvastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Antacids

    absorption of rosuvastatin reduced by antacids

    Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

    Atazanavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with atazanavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with ciclosporin (avoid concomitant use)

    Coumarins

    rosuvastatin possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Darunavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with darunavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Dronedarone

    plasma concentration of rosuvastatin possibly increased by dronedarone

    Eltrombopag

    plasma concentration of rosuvastatin increased by eltrombopag (consider reducing dose of rosuvastatin)

    Erythromycin

    plasma concentration of rosuvastatin reduced by erythromycin

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Ethinylestradiol

    rosuvastatin increases plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol

    Fibrates

    increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with fibrates (see Dose under Rosuvastatin, section 2.12)

    Fosamprenavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with fosamprenavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

    Indinavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with indinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Lopinavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with lopinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

    Norgestimate

    rosuvastatin increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of norgestimate

    Norgestrel

    rosuvastatin increases plasma concentration of norgestrel

    Phenindione

    rosuvastatin possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of phenindione

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Ritonavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with ritonavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Saquinavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with saquinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Tipranavir

    plasma concentration of rosuvastatin possibly increased by tipranavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

    Atorvastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Antifungals, Imidazole

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with imidazoles

    Antifungals, Triazole

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with triazoles

    Atazanavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with atazanavir

    Boceprevir

    enhanced effects and increased toxicity of atorvastatin when given with boceprevir (reduce dose of atorvastatin)

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with ciclosporin (see Dose under Atorvastatin, section 2.12)

    Clarithromycin

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin increased by clarithromycin

    Darunavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with darunavir

    Digoxin

    atorvastatin possibly increases plasma concentration of digoxin

    Diltiazem

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin increased by diltiazem—possible increased risk of myopathy

    Dronedarone

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin possibly increased by dronedarone

    Efavirenz

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin reduced by efavirenz

    Erythromycin

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with erythromycin

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Ethinylestradiol

    atorvastatin increases plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol

    Etravirine

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin possibly reduced by etravirine

    Fosamprenavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with fosamprenavir

    Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

    Gemfibrozil

    increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with gemfibrozil (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Grapefruit Juice

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin possibly increased by grapefruit juice

    Indinavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with indinavir

    Itraconazole

    increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with itraconazole

    Lopinavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with lopinavir

    Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

    Midazolam

    atorvastatin possibly increases plasma concentration of midazolam

    Norethisterone

    atorvastatin increases plasma concentration of norethisterone

    Posaconazole

    increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with posaconazole (avoid concomitant use)

    Rifampicin

    plasma concentration of atorvastatin possibly reduced by rifampicin

    Ritonavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with ritonavir

    Saquinavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with saquinavir

    Telaprevir

    avoidance of atorvastatin advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

    Telithromycin

    increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with telithromycin (avoid concomitant use)

    Tipranavir

    increased risk of myopathy when atorvastatin given with tipranavir (see Dose under Atorvastatin, section 2.12)

    Warfarin

    atorvastatin may transiently reduce anticoagulant effect of warfarin

    Fluvastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of myopathy when fluvastatin given with ciclosporin

    Coumarins

    fluvastatin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Fluconazole

    plasma concentration of fluvastatin increased by fluconazole—possible increased risk of myopathy

    Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

    Gemfibrozil

    increased risk of myopathy when fluvastatin given with gemfibrozil (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Glibenclamide

    fluvastatin possibly increases plasma concentration of glibenclamide

    Phenytoin

    combination of fluvastatin with phenytoin may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)

    Rifampicin

    metabolism of fluvastatin accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)

    Pravastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Atazanavir

    possible increased risk of myopathy when pravastatin given with atazanavir

    Boceprevir

    plasma concentration of pravastatin increased by boceprevir

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of myopathy when pravastatin given with ciclosporin

    Clarithromycin

    plasma concentration of pravastatin increased by clarithromycin

    Darunavir

    plasma concentration of pravastatin possibly increased by darunavir

    Efavirenz

    plasma concentration of pravastatin reduced by efavirenz

    Erythromycin

    plasma concentration of pravastatin increased by erythromycin

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Gemfibrozil

    increased risk of myopathy when pravastatin given with gemfibrozil (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Telithromycin

    possible increased risk of myopathy when pravastatin given with telithromycin

    Simvastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

    Alitretinoin

    plasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by alitretinoin

    Amiodarone

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with amiodarone (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

    Amlodipine

    possible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with amlodipine (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Atazanavir

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with atazanavir (avoid concomitant use)

    Bezafibrate

    possible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with bezafibrate (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Boceprevir

    manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use of simvastatin with boceprevir

    Bosentan

    plasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by bosentan

    Carbamazepine

    plasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by carbamazepine—consider increasing dose of simvastatin

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with ciclosporin (avoid concomitant use)

    Ciprofibrate

    possible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with ciprofibrate (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Clarithromycin

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with clarithromycin (avoid concomitant use)

    Coumarins

    simvastatin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Dasatinib

    plasma concentration of simvastatin possibly increased by dasatinib

    Diltiazem

    possible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with diltiazem (see Dose under Simvastatin, section 2.12)

    Dronedarone

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with dronedarone

    Efavirenz

    plasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by efavirenz

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Fluconazole

    Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

    Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with gemfibrozil (avoid concomitant use)

    plasma concentration of simvastatin increased by imatinib

    Itraconazole

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with ketoconazole (avoid concomitant use)

    increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with posaconazole (avoid concomitant use)
