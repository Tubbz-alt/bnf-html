Ulcer-healing Drugs

Ulcer-healing Drugs has no specific interaction information.

Histamine H2-antagonists belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavir histamine H2-antagonists reduce plasma concentration of atazanavir
Cefpodoxime histamine H2-antagonists reduce absorption of cefpodoxime
Histamine histamine H2-antagonists theoretically antagonise effects of histamine—manufacturer of histamine advises avoid concomitant use
Itraconazole histamine H2-antagonists reduce absorption of itraconazole
Ketoconazole histamine H2-antagonists reduce absorption of ketoconazole
Lapatinib histamine H2-antagonists possibly reduce absorption of lapatinib
Posaconazoleavoidance of histamine H2-antagonists advised by manufacturer of posaconazole (plasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced)
Raltegravir histamine H2-antagonists possibly increase plasma concentration of raltegravir—manufacturer of raltegravir advises avoid concomitant use
Ulipristalavoidance of histamine H2-antagonists advised by manufacturer of ulipristal (plasma concentration of ulipristal possibly reduced)

Cimetidine belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone cimetidine increases plasma concentration of amiodarone Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Amitriptyline cimetidine inhibits metabolism of amitriptyline (increased plasma concentration)
Antidepressants, Tricyclic cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of tricyclics
Antipsychotics cimetidine possibly enhances effects of antipsychotics Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of cimetidine advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Benzodiazepines cimetidine inhibits metabolism of benzodiazepines (increased plasma concentration)
Calcium-channel Blockers cimetidine possibly inhibits metabolism of calcium-channel blockers (increased plasma concentration)Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Carbamazepine cimetidine inhibits metabolism of carbamazepine (increased plasma concentration)
Carmustine cimetidine possibly enhances myelosuppressive effects of carmustine
Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine cimetidine inhibits metabolism of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine (increased plasma concentration)
Chlorpromazine cimetidine possibly enhances effects of chlorpromazine
Ciclosporin cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Citalopram cimetidine increases plasma concentration of citalopram
Clomethiazole cimetidine inhibits metabolism of clomethiazole (increased plasma concentration)
Clopidogrel cimetidine possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Clozapine cimetidine possibly enhances effects of clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Coumarins cimetidine inhibits metabolism of coumarins (enhanced anticoagulant effect)Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Doxepin cimetidine inhibits metabolism of doxepin (increased plasma concentration)
Epirubicin cimetidine increases plasma concentration of epirubicin
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when cimetidine given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Erlotinibavoidance of cimetidine advised by manufacturer of erlotinib
Erythromycin cimetidine increases plasma concentration of erythromycin (increased risk of toxicity, including deafness)Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Escitalopram cimetidine increases plasma concentration of escitalopram
Fampridineavoidance of cimetidine advised by manufacturer of fampridine
Flecainide cimetidine inhibits metabolism of flecainide (increased plasma concentration)
Fluorouracil cimetidine inhibits metabolism of fluorouracil (increased plasma concentration)
Imipramine cimetidine inhibits metabolism of imipramine (increased plasma concentration)
Isradipine cimetidine increases plasma concentration of isradipine (halve dose of isradipine)
Labetalol cimetidine increases plasma concentration of labetalol
Levothyroxine cimetidine reduces absorption of levothyroxine
Lidocaine cimetidine increases plasma concentration of lidocaine (increased risk of toxicity)Interactions less likely when lidocaine used topically
Lomustine cimetidine possibly enhances myelosuppressive effects of lomustine
Loratadinemanufacturer of loratadine advises cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of loratadine
Mebendazole cimetidine possibly inhibits metabolism of mebendazole (increased plasma concentration)
Melatonin cimetidine increases plasma concentration of melatonin
Metformin cimetidine reduces excretion of metformin (increased plasma concentration)
Metoprolol cimetidine increases plasma concentration of metoprolol
Metronidazole cimetidine inhibits metabolism of metronidazole (increased plasma concentration)Interactions do not apply to topical metronidazole preparations
Mirtazapine cimetidine increases plasma concentration of mirtazapine
Moclobemide cimetidine increases plasma concentration of moclobemide (halve dose of moclobemide)
Nortriptyline cimetidine inhibits metabolism of nortriptyline (increased plasma concentration)
Octreotideabsorption of cimetidine possibly delayed by octreotide
Opioid Analgesics cimetidine inhibits metabolism of opioid analgesics (increased plasma concentration)
Phenytoin cimetidine inhibits metabolism of phenytoin (increased plasma concentration)
Posaconazole cimetidine reduces plasma concentration of posaconazole
Pramipexole cimetidine reduces excretion of pramipexole (increased plasma concentration)
Propafenone cimetidine increases plasma concentration of propafenone
Propranolol cimetidine increases plasma concentration of propranolol
Quinine cimetidine inhibits metabolism of quinine (increased plasma concentration)
Rifampicinmetabolism of cimetidine accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)
Roflumilast cimetidine inhibits the metabolism of roflumilast
Saquinavir cimetidine possibly increases plasma concentration of saquinavir
Sertraline cimetidine increases plasma concentration of sertraline
Sildenafil cimetidine increases plasma concentration of sildenafil (consider reducing dose of sildenafil)
Sulfonylureas cimetidine enhances hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas
Terbinafine cimetidine increases plasma concentration of terbinafine
Theophylline cimetidine inhibits metabolism of theophylline (increased plasma concentration)
Tolazoline cimetidine antagonises effects of tolazoline
Valproate cimetidine inhibits metabolism of valproate (increased plasma concentration)
Zaleplon cimetidine inhibits metabolism of zaleplon (increased plasma concentration)
Zolmitriptan cimetidine inhibits metabolism of zolmitriptan (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

Famotidine belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Dasatinib famotidine possibly reduces plasma concentration of dasatinib
Erlotinibavoidance of famotidine advised by manufacturer of erlotinib

Nizatidine belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Erlotinibavoidance of nizatidine advised by manufacturer of erlotinib

Ranitidine belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Erlotinib ranitidine reduces plasma concentration of erlotinib—manufacturer of erlotinib advises give at least 2 hours before or 10 hours after ranitidine
Gefitinib ranitidine reduces plasma concentration of gefitinib
Tolazoline ranitidine antagonises effects of tolazoline

Proton Pump Inhibitors belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavir proton pump inhibitors reduce plasma concentration of atazanavir—avoid or adjust dose of both drugs (consult product literature)
Digoxin proton pump inhibitors possibly slightly increase plasma concentration of digoxin
Itraconazole proton pump inhibitors reduce absorption of itraconazole
Ketoconazole proton pump inhibitors reduce absorption of ketoconazole
Lapatinib proton pump inhibitors possibly reduce absorption of lapatinib
Posaconazoleavoidance of proton pump inhibitors advised by manufacturer of posaconazole (plasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced)
Raltegravir proton pump inhibitors possibly increase plasma concentration of raltegravir—manufacturer of raltegravir advises avoid concomitant use
Ulipristalavoidance of proton pump inhibitors advised by manufacturer of ulipristal (plasma concentration of ulipristal possibly reduced)

Esomeprazole belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Clopidogrel esomeprazole reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Coumarins esomeprazole possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam esomeprazole possibly inhibits metabolism of diazepam (increased plasma concentration)
Erlotinibavoidance of esomeprazole advised by manufacturer of erlotinib
Nelfinavir esomeprazole possibly reduces plasma concentration of nelfinavir—manufacturer of esomeprazole advises avoid concomitant use
Phenytoin esomeprazole enhances effects of phenytoin
Saquinavir esomeprazole possibly increases plasma concentration of saquinavir—manufacturer of saquinavir advises avoid concomitant use
Tipranavirplasma concentration of esomeprazole reduced by tipranavir
Voriconazoleplasma concentration of esomeprazole possibly increased by voriconazole

Omeprazole belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Ciclosporin omeprazole possibly affects plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Cilostazol omeprazole increases plasma concentration of cilostazol (consider reducing dose of cilostazol)
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of both drugs increased when omeprazole given with clarithromycin
Clopidogrel omeprazole reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Clozapine omeprazole possibly reduces plasma concentration of clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Coumarins omeprazole possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam omeprazole possibly inhibits metabolism of diazepam (increased plasma concentration)
Erlotinib omeprazole reduces plasma concentration of erlotinib—manufacturer of erlotinib advises avoid concomitant use
Escitalopram omeprazole increases plasma concentration of escitalopram
Methotrexate omeprazole possibly reduces excretion of methotrexate (increased risk of toxicity)
Nelfinavir omeprazole reduces plasma concentration of nelfinavir—avoid concomitant use
Phenytoin omeprazole possibly enhances effects of phenytoin
Raltegravir omeprazole increases plasma concentration of raltegravir—avoid concomitant use
Saquinavir omeprazole increases plasma concentration of saquinavir—manufacturer of saquinavir advises avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of omeprazole possibly reduced by St John's wort
Tacrolimus omeprazole possibly increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Tipranavirplasma concentration of omeprazole reduced by tipranavir
Voriconazoleplasma concentration of omeprazole increased by voriconazole (consider reducing dose of omeprazole)

Lansoprazole belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Antacidsabsorption of lansoprazole possibly reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Clopidogrel lansoprazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Erlotinibavoidance of lansoprazole advised by manufacturer of erlotinib
Fluvoxamineplasma concentration of lansoprazole possibly increased by fluvoxamine
Saquinavir lansoprazole possibly increases plasma concentration of saquinavir—manufacturer of saquinavir advises avoid concomitant use
Sucralfateabsorption of lansoprazole possibly reduced by sucralfate

Sucralfate belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Cardiac Glycosides sucralfate possibly reduces absorption of cardiac glycosides
Ciprofloxacin sucralfate reduces absorption of ciprofloxacin
Coumarins sucralfate possibly reduces absorption of coumarins (reduced anticoagulant effect)Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Ketoconazole sucralfate reduces absorption of ketoconazole
Lansoprazole sucralfate possibly reduces absorption of lansoprazole
Levofloxacin sucralfate reduces absorption of levofloxacin
Levothyroxine sucralfate reduces absorption of levothyroxine
Moxifloxacin sucralfate reduces absorption of moxifloxacin
Norfloxacin sucralfate reduces absorption of norfloxacin
Ofloxacin sucralfate reduces absorption of ofloxacin
Phenytoin sucralfate reduces absorption of phenytoin
Sulpiride sucralfate reduces absorption of sulpiride
Tetracyclines sucralfate reduces absorption of tetracyclines
Theophylline sucralfate possibly reduces absorption of theophylline (give at least 2 hours apart)

Tripotassium Dicitratobismuthate belongs to Ulcer-healing Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Tetracyclines tripotassium dicitratobismuthate reduces absorption of tetracyclines
