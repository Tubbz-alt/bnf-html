Cilazapril

Cilazapril has no specific interaction information.

Cilazapril belongs to ACE Inhibitors and will have the following interaction information:

Alcohol

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with alcohol

Aldesleukin

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with aldesleukin

Allopurinol

increased risk of leucopenia and hypersensitivity reactions when ACE inhibitors given with allopurinol especially in renal impairment

Alpha-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with alpha-blockers

Alprostadil

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with alprostadil

Anaesthetics, General

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with general anaesthetics

Note: See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists

increased risk of hyperkalaemia when ACE inhibitors given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists

Antacids

absorption of ACE inhibitors possibly reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Antipsychotics

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with antipsychotics

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with anxiolytics and hypnotics

Baclofen

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with baclofen

Beta-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Calcium-channel Blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with calcium-channel blockers

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Ciclosporin

increased risk of hyperkalaemia when ACE inhibitors given with ciclosporin

Clonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with clonidine

Corticosteroids

hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors antagonised by corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Diazoxide

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with diazoxide

Diuretics

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with diuretics

Diuretics, Potassium-sparing and Aldosterone Antagonists

increased risk of severe hyperkalaemia when ACE inhibitors given with potassium-sparing diuretics and aldosterone antagonists (monitor potassium concentration with low-dose spironolactone in heart failure)

Heparins

increased risk of hyperkalaemia when ACE inhibitors given with heparins

Hydralazine

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with hydralazine

Insulin

ACE inhibitors possibly enhance hypoglycaemic effect of insulin

Levodopa

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with levodopa

Lithium

ACE inhibitors reduce excretion of lithium (increased plasma concentration)

MAOIs

hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors possibly enhanced by MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Metformin

ACE inhibitors possibly enhance hypoglycaemic effect of metformin

Methyldopa

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with methyldopa

Minoxidil

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with minoxidil

Moxisylyte

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with moxisylyte

Moxonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with moxonidine

NSAIDs

increased risk of renal impairment when ACE inhibitors given with NSAIDs , also hypotensive effect antagonised

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Nitrates

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with nitrates

Oestrogens

hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors antagonised by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Potassium Salts

increased risk of severe hyperkalaemia when ACE inhibitors given with potassium salts

Note: Includes salt substitutes

Sodium Aurothiomalate

flushing and hypotension reported when ACE inhibitors given with sodium aurothiomalate

Sodium Nitroprusside

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with sodium nitroprusside

Sulfonylureas

ACE inhibitors possibly enhance hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas

Tizanidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when ACE inhibitors given with tizanidine

Trimethoprim

possible increased risk of hyperkalaemia when ACE inhibitors given with trimethoprim
