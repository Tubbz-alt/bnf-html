Oestrogens

Oestrogens has the following interaction information:

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

ACE Inhibitors

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors

Adrenergic Neurone Blockers

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers

Alpha-blockers

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of alpha-blockers

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of angiotensin-II receptor antagonists

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

oestrogens antagonise antidepressant effect of tricyclics (but side-effects of tricyclics possibly increased due to increased plasma concentration)

Antidiabetics

oestrogens antagonise hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics

Antifungals, Imidazole

anecdotal reports of contraceptive failure when oestrogens given with imidazoles

Aprepitant

possible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing oestrogens when given with aprepitant (alternative contraception recommended)

Beta-blockers

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Boceprevir

possible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing oestrogens when given with boceprevir (alternative contraception recommended)

Bosentan

possible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing oestrogens when given with bosentan (alternative contraception recommended)

Calcium-channel Blockers

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of calcium-channel blockers

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Carbamazepine

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Ciclosporin

oestrogens possibly increase plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Clonidine

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of clonidine

Corticosteroids

oral contraceptives containing oestrogens increase plasma concentration of corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Coumarins

oestrogens may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Diazepam

oestrogens possibly increase plasma concentration of diazepam

Diuretics

oestrogens antagonise diuretic effect of diuretics

Eslicarbazepine

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by eslicarbazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Griseofulvin

anecdotal reports of contraceptive failure and menstrual irregularities when oestrogens given with griseofulvin

Hydralazine

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of hydralazine

Lamotrigine

oestrogens reduce plasma concentration of lamotrigine —consider increasing dose of lamotrigine

Melatonin

oestrogens increase plasma concentration of melatonin

Methyldopa

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of methyldopa

Minoxidil

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of minoxidil

Modafinil

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by modafinil (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Moxonidine

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of moxonidine

Nelfinavir

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by nelfinavir (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Nevirapine

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by nevirapine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Nitrates

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of nitrates

Oxcarbazepine

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by oxcarbazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Phenindione

oestrogens antagonise anticoagulant effect of phenindione

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Phenobarbital

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Phenytoin

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by phenytoin (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Rifamycins

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by rifamycins (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

Ritonavir

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by ritonavir (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Ropinirole

oestrogens increase plasma concentration of ropinirole

Rufinamide

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by rufinamide (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Selegiline

oestrogens increase plasma concentration of selegiline —manufacturer of selegiline advises avoid concomitant use

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Sodium Nitroprusside

oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of sodium nitroprusside

Somatropin

oestrogens (when used as oral replacement therapy) may increase dose requirements of somatropin

St John's Wort

contraceptive effect of oestrogens reduced by St John's wort (avoid concomitant use)

Terbinafine

occasional reports of breakthrough bleeding when oestrogens (used for contraception) given with terbinafine

Theophylline

oestrogens increase plasma concentration of theophylline (consider reducing dose of theophylline)

Thyroid Hormones

oestrogens may increase requirements for thyroid hormones in hypothyroidism

Tizanidine

oestrogens possibly increase plasma concentration of tizanidine (increased risk of toxicity)

Topiramate

metabolism of oestrogens accelerated by topiramate (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Vemurafenib

possible reduction in contraceptive effect of oestrogens advised by manufacturer of vemurafenib

Voriconazole

oestrogens increase plasma concentration of voriconazole

Estradiol belongs to Oestrogens and has the following interaction information:

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of estradiol increased by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Estriol belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.

Estrone belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.

Ethinylestradiol belongs to Oestrogens and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by atazanavir

Atorvastatin

plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by atorvastatin

Colesevelam

absorption of ethinylestradiol reduced by colesevelam

Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption

Etoricoxib

plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by etoricoxib

Rosuvastatin

plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by rosuvastatin

Tacrolimus

ethinylestradiol possibly increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol possibly reduced by telaprevir —manufacturer of telaprevir advises additional contraceptive precautions

Valproate

ethinylestradiol possibly reduces plasma concentration of valproate

Mestranol belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.

Oestrogens, conjugated belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.
