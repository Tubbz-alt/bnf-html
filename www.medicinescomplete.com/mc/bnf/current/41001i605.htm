Simvastatin

Simvastatin has the following interaction information:

Alitretinoinplasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by alitretinoin
Amiodaroneincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with amiodarone Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Amlodipinepossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with amlodipine
Atazanavirincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with atazanavir (avoid concomitant use)
Bosentanplasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by bosentan
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by carbamazepine—consider increasing dose of simvastatin
Clarithromycinincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with clarithromycin (avoid concomitant use)
Coumarins simvastatin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Danazolpossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with danazol
Dasatinibplasma concentration of simvastatin possibly increased by dasatinib
Diltiazemplasma concentration of simvastatin increased by diltiazem—possible increased risk of myopathy
Dronedaroneincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with dronedarone
Efavirenzplasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by efavirenz
Erythromycinincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with erythromycin (avoid concomitant use)Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Eslicarbazepineplasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by eslicarbazepine—consider increasing dose of simvastatin
Fluconazolepossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with fluconazole—avoid concomitant useIn general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Fosamprenavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with fosamprenavir—avoid concomitant useFosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Grapefruit Juiceplasma concentration of simvastatin increased by grapefruit juice—avoid concomitant use
Imatinibplasma concentration of simvastatin increased by imatinib
Indinavirincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with indinavir (avoid concomitant use)
Itraconazoleincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with itraconazole (avoid concomitant use)
Ketoconazoleincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with ketoconazole (avoid concomitant use)
Lopinavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with lopinavir—avoid concomitant useIn combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Miconazolepossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with miconazole—avoid concomitant use
Nelfinavirincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with nelfinavir (avoid concomitant use)
Posaconazoleincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with posaconazole (avoid concomitant use)
Ranolazineplasma concentration of simvastatin increased by ranolazine (consider reducing dose of simvastatin)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of simvastatin possibly reduced by rifampicin
Ritonavirincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with ritonavir (avoid concomitant use)
Saquinavirincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with saquinavir (avoid concomitant use)
St John's Wortplasma concentration of simvastatin reduced by St John's wort
Telapreviravoidance of simvastatin advised by manufacturer of telaprevir
Telithromycinincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with telithromycin (avoid concomitant use)
Ticagrelorplasma concentration of simvastatin increased by ticagrelor (increased risk of toxicity)
Tipranavirplasma concentration of simvastatin possibly increased by tipranavir—avoid concomitant use
Verapamilincreased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with verapamil
Voriconazolepossible increased risk of myopathy when simvastatin given with voriconazole

Simvastatin belongs to Statins and will have the following interactions:

Ciclosporinincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with ciclosporin
Colchicinepossible increased risk of myopathy when statins given with colchicine
Daptomycinincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with daptomycin (preferably avoid concomitant use)
Fibratesincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with fibrates
Fusidic Acidrisk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis when statins given with fusidic acid—avoid concomitant use and for 7 days after last fusidic acid dose
Gemfibrozilincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with gemfibrozil (preferably avoid concomitant use)
Nicotinic Acidincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with nicotinic acid (applies to lipid regulating doses of nicotinic acid)Interactions apply to lipid-regulating doses of nicotinic acid

Simvastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs but Lipid-regulating Drugs has no interactions information.
