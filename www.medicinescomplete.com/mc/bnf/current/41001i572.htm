5HT1-receptor Agonists

5HT1-receptor Agonists has the following interaction information:

Citalopramincreased risk of CNS toxicity when 5HT1 agonists given with citalopram (manufacturer of citalopram advises avoid concomitant use)
Duloxetinepossible increased serotonergic effects when 5HT1 agonists given with duloxetine
Selegilineavoidance of 5HT1 agonists advised by manufacturer of selegiline Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
St John's Wortincreased serotonergic effects when 5HT1 agonists given with St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Venlafaxinepossible increased serotonergic effects when 5HT1 agonists given with venlafaxine

Frovatriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when frovatriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 24 hours after frovatriptan, avoid frovatriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)
Fluvoxaminemetabolism of frovatriptan inhibited by fluvoxamine

Eletriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Clarithromycinplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by clarithromycin (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when eletriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 24 hours after eletriptan, avoid eletriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)
Erythromycinplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by erythromycin (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant useInteractions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Indinavirplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by indinavir (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Itraconazoleplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by itraconazole (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by ketoconazole (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Nelfinavirplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by nelfinavir (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Ritonavirplasma concentration of eletriptan increased by ritonavir (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Almotriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when almotriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after almotriptan, avoid almotriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of almotriptan increased by ketoconazole (increased risk of toxicity)

Naratriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Antidepressants, SSRIpossible increased serotonergic effects when naratriptan given with SSRIs
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when naratriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 24 hours after naratriptan, avoid naratriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

Rizatriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when rizatriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after rizatriptan, avoid rizatriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)
MAOIsrisk of CNS toxicity when rizatriptan given with MAOIs (avoid rizatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Moclobemiderisk of CNS toxicity when rizatriptan given with moclobemide (avoid rizatriptan for 2 weeks after moclobemide)
Propranololplasma concentration of rizatriptan increased by propranolol (manufacturer of rizatriptan advises halve dose and avoid within 2 hours of propranolol)

Sumatriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Citalopramincreased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with citalopram
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when sumatriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after sumatriptan, avoid sumatriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)
Escitalopramincreased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with escitalopram
Fluoxetineincreased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with fluoxetine
Fluvoxamineincreased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with fluvoxamine
Lithiumpossible risk of toxicity when sumatriptan given with lithium
MAOIsrisk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with MAOIs (avoid sumatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Moclobemiderisk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with moclobemide (avoid sumatriptan for 2 weeks after moclobemide)
Paroxetineincreased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with paroxetine
SertralineCNS toxicity reported when sumatriptan given with sertraline

Zolmitriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidinemetabolism of zolmitriptan inhibited by cimetidine (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of vasospasm when zolmitriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after zolmitriptan, avoid zolmitriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)
Fluvoxaminemetabolism of zolmitriptan possibly inhibited by fluvoxamine (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)
MAOIsincreased risk of CNS toxicity when zolmitriptan given with MAOIs For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Moclobemiderisk of CNS toxicity when zolmitriptan given with moclobemide (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)
Quinolonesmetabolism of zolmitriptan possibly inhibited by quinolones (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)
