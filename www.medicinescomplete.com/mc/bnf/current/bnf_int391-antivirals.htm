    Antivirals

    Antivirals has no specific interaction information.

    Rilpivirine belongs to Antivirals and has the following interaction information:

    Antacids

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises give antacids 2 hours before or 4 hours after rilpivirine

    Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

    Calcium Salts

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises give calcium salts 2 hours before or 4 hours after rilpivirine

    Note: see also Antacids

    Carbamazepine

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with carbamazepine (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Clarithromycin

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with clarithromycin (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly increased)

    Dabigatran Etexilate

    rilpivirine possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate

    Dexamethasone

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with dexamethasone (except when given as a single dose)

    Didanosine

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises give didanosine 2 hours before or 4 hours after rilpivirine

    Note: Antacids in tablet formulation may affect absorption of other drugs

    Digoxin

    rilpivirine possibly increases plasma concentration of digoxin

    Erythromycin

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with erythromycin (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly increased)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Esomeprazole

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with esomeprazole (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Histamine H2-antagonists

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid histamine H2-antagonists for 12 hours before or 4 hours after rilpivirine—consult product literature

    Lansoprazole

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with lansoprazole (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Metformin

    rilpivirine possibly increases plasma concentration of metformin

    Methadone

    rilpivirine possibly reduces plasma concentration of methadone

    Omeprazole

    plasma concentration of rilpivirine reduced by omeprazole—avoid concomitant use

    Oxcarbazepine

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with oxcarbazepine (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Pantoprazole

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with pantoprazole (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Phenobarbital

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with phenobarbital (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Phenytoin

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with phenytoin (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Rabeprazole

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with rabeprazole (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Rifabutin

    plasma concentration of rilpivirine reduced by rifabutin—avoid concomitant use

    Rifampicin

    plasma concentration of rilpivirine reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use

    St John's Wort

    manufacturer of rilpivirine advises avoid concomitant use with St John's wort (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Telaprevir belongs to Antivirals and has the following interaction information:

    Alfuzosin

    manufacturer of telaprevir advises avoid concomitant use with alfuzosin

    Amiodarone

    manufacturer of telaprevir advises avoid concomitant use with amiodarone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

    Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

    avoidance of telaprevir advised by manufacturer of apixaban

    plasma concentration of both drugs possibly increased when telaprevir given with clarithromycin (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
