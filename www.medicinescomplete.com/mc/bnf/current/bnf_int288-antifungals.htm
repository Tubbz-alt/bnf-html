Antifungals

Antifungals has no specific interaction information.

Micafungin belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Amphotericin

micafungin possibly increases plasma concentration of amphotericin

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Ciclosporin

micafungin possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Itraconazole

micafungin increases plasma concentration of itraconazole (consider reducing dose of itraconazole)

Nifedipine

micafungin increases plasma concentration of nifedipine

Sirolimus

micafungin increases plasma concentration of sirolimus

Caspofungin belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by carbamazepine —consider increasing dose of caspofungin

Ciclosporin

plasma concentration of caspofungin increased by ciclosporin (manufacturer of caspofungin recommends monitoring liver enzymes)

Dexamethasone

plasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by dexamethasone —consider increasing dose of caspofungin

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by efavirenz —consider increasing dose of caspofungin

Nevirapine

plasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by nevirapine —consider increasing dose of caspofungin

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by phenytoin —consider increasing dose of caspofungin

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of caspofungin initially increased and then reduced by rifampicin (consider increasing dose of caspofungin)

Tacrolimus

caspofungin reduces plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Antifungals, Polyene belongs to Antifungals but has no specific interaction information.

Amphotericin belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Aminoglycosides

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when amphotericin given with aminoglycosides

Antifungals, Imidazole

effects of amphotericin possibly antagonised by imidazoles

Antifungals, Triazole

effects of amphotericin possibly antagonised by triazoles

Arsenic Trioxide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amphotericin given with arsenic trioxide

Cardiac Glycosides

hypokalaemia caused by amphotericin increases cardiac toxicity with cardiac glycosides

Ciclosporin

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when amphotericin given with ciclosporin

Corticosteroids

increased risk of hypokalaemia when amphotericin given with corticosteroids —avoid concomitant use unless corticosteroids needed to control reactions

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Diuretics, Loop

increased risk of hypokalaemia when amphotericin given with loop diuretics

Diuretics, Thiazide and related

increased risk of hypokalaemia when amphotericin given with thiazides and related diuretics

Flucytosine

amphotericin reduces renal excretion and increases cellular uptake of flucytosine (toxicity possibly increased)

Micafungin

plasma concentration of amphotericin possibly increased by micafungin

Pentamidine Isetionate

possible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when amphotericin given with pentamidine isetionate

Polymyxins

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when amphotericin given with polymyxins

Tacrolimus

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when amphotericin given with tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Vancomycin

possible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when amphotericin given with vancomycin

Flucytosine belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Amphotericin

renal excretion of flucytosine decreased and cellular uptake increased by amphotericin (toxicity possibly increased)

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Cytarabine

plasma concentration of flucytosine possibly reduced by cytarabine

Griseofulvin belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Alcohol

griseofulvin possibly enhances effects of alcohol

Ciclosporin

griseofulvin possibly reduces plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Coumarins

griseofulvin reduces anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Oestrogens

anecdotal reports of contraceptive failure and menstrual irregularities when griseofulvin given with oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Phenobarbital

absorption of griseofulvin reduced by phenobarbital (reduced effect)

Progestogens

anecdotal reports of contraceptive failure and menstrual irregularities when griseofulvin given with progestogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
