Voriconazole

Voriconazole has the following interaction information:

Alfentanil voriconazole increases plasma concentration of alfentanil (consider reducing dose of alfentanil)
Apixabanavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Cabazitaxelavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly reduced by carbamazepine—avoid concomitant use
Ciclosporin voriconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Clopidogrel voriconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Coumarins voriconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam voriconazole increases plasma concentration of diazepam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Diclofenac voriconazole increases plasma concentration of diclofenac
Dronedaroneavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of dronedarone
Efavirenzplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by efavirenz , also plasma concentration of efavirenz increased (increase voriconazole dose and reduce efavirenz dose)
Esomeprazole voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of esomeprazole
Everolimus voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Ibuprofen voriconazole increases plasma concentration of ibuprofen
Lapatinibavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of lapatinib
Methadone voriconazole increases plasma concentration of methadone (consider reducing dose of methadone)
Midazolam voriconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Nilotinibavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of nilotinib
Oestrogensplasma concentration of voriconazole increased by oestrogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Omeprazole voriconazole increases plasma concentration of omeprazole (consider reducing dose of omeprazole)
Oxycodone voriconazole increases plasma concentration of oxycodone
Pazopanibavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly reduced by phenobarbital—avoid concomitant use
Phenytoin voriconazole increases plasma concentration of phenytoin , also phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole (increase dose of voriconazole and also monitor for phenytoin toxicity)
Progestogensplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly increased by progestogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5
Ranolazine voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use
Rifabutin voriconazole increases plasma concentration of rifabutin , also rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole (increase dose of voriconazole and also monitor for rifabutin toxicity)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use
Ritonavirplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by ritonavir—avoid concomitant use
Rivaroxabanavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban
Simvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when voriconazole given with simvastatin
Sirolimus voriconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus—avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Sulfonylureas voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of sulfonylureas
Tacrolimus voriconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Telaprevirplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly affected by telaprevir (possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Voriconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and will have the following interactions:

Amphotericin triazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when triazoles given with atorvastatin
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when triazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Fentanyl triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of fentanyl
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when triazoles given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quetiapine triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Reboxetineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine
Rifabutin triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)
Saquinavir triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Voriconazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.
