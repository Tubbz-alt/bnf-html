Fosphenytoin

Fosphenytoin has no specific interaction information.

Fosphenytoin interactions as for phenytoin

Fosphenytoin belongs to Phenytoin and will have the following interactions:

Abacavir phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of abacavir
Acetazolamideplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by acetazolamide
Alcoholplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly reduced by chronic heavy consumption of alcohol
Amiodaronemetabolism of phenytoin inhibited by amiodarone (increased plasma concentration)Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antacidsabsorption of phenytoin reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Antidepressants, Tricyclic phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of tricyclics
Aprepitant phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of aprepitant
Aripiprazole phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of aripiprazole—increase dose of aripiprazole
Aspirineffects of phenytoin enhanced by aspirin
Benzodiazepinesplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased or decreased by benzodiazepines
Bocepreviravoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of boceprevir (plasma concentration of boceprevir possibly reduced)
Bupropion phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of bupropion
Busulfan phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of busulfan
Cabazitaxelavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of both drugs often reduced when phenytoin given with carbamazepine , also plasma concentration of phenytoin may be increased
Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitorsincreased risk of osteomalacia when phenytoin given with carbonic anhydrase inhibitors
Caspofungin phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of caspofungin—consider increasing dose of caspofungin
Chloramphenicolplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by chloramphenicol (increased risk of toxicity)
Chlorpromazineplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased or decreased by chlorpromazine
Ciclosporin phenytoin accelerates metabolism of ciclosporin (reduced plasma concentration)
Cimetidinemetabolism of phenytoin inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)
Ciprofloxacinplasma concentration of phenytoin increased or decreased by ciprofloxacin
Cisplatinplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly reduced by cisplatin
Clarithromycinmetabolism of phenytoin inhibited by clarithromycin (increased plasma concentration)
Clonazepam phenytoin often reduces plasma concentration of clonazepam
Clozapine phenytoin accelerates metabolism of clozapine (reduced plasma concentration)Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Colesevelamabsorption of phenytoin possibly reduced by colesevelam Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption
Corticosteroids phenytoin accelerates metabolism of corticosteroids (reduced effect)Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Coumarins phenytoin accelerates metabolism of coumarins (possibility of reduced anticoagulant effect, but enhancement also reported)Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Dabigatran Etexilate phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate—manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use
Darunavir phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of darunavir
Diazepamplasma concentration of phenytoin increased or decreased by diazepam
Diazoxideplasma concentration of phenytoin reduced by diazoxide , also effect of diazoxide may be reduced
Digoxin phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of digoxin
Diltiazemplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by diltiazem but also effect of diltiazem reduced
Disopyramide phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of disopyramide
Disulfirammetabolism of phenytoin inhibited by disulfiram (increased risk of toxicity)
Doxycycline phenytoin accelerates metabolism of doxycycline (reduced plasma concentration)
Dronedarone phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of dronedarone—avoid concomitant use
Enteral Foodsabsorption of phenytoin possibly reduced by enteral feeds
Eplerenone phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of eplerenone—avoid concomitant use
Eribulin phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of eribulin
Eslicarbazepine phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of eslicarbazepine , also plasma concentration of phenytoin increased
Esomeprazoleeffects of phenytoin enhanced by esomeprazole
Ethosuximideplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by ethosuximide , also plasma concentration of ethosuximide possibly reduced
Etoposide phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of etoposide
Etravirineavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of etravirine
Felodipine phenytoin reduces effects of felodipine
Fluconazoleplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by fluconazole (consider reducing dose of phenytoin)In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Fluorouracilmetabolism of phenytoin possibly inhibited by fluorouracil (increased risk of toxicity)
Fluoxetineplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by fluoxetine
Fluvastatincombination of phenytoin with fluvastatin may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)
Fluvoxamineplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by fluvoxamine
Folatesplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly reduced by folates
Furosemide phenytoin antagonises effects of furosemide
Gefitinibavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of gefitinib
Haloperidol phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of haloperidol
Imatinib phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of imatinib—avoid concomitant use
Indinavir phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of indinavir , also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased
Influenza Vaccineeffects of phenytoin enhanced by influenza vaccine
Irinotecan phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of irinotecan and its active metabolite
Isoniazidmetabolism of phenytoin possibly inhibited by isoniazid (increased risk of toxicity)
Isradipineavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of isradipine
Itraconazole phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of itraconazole—avoid concomitant use
Ketoconazole phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of ketoconazole
Lamotrigine phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of lamotrigine
Lapatinibavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of lapatinib
Leflunomideplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by leflunomide Increased risk of toxicity with other haematotoxic and hepatotoxic drugs
Levamisoleplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by levamisole
Levodopa phenytoin possibly reduces effects of levodopa
Lithiumneurotoxicity may occur when phenytoin given with lithium without increased plasma concentration of lithium
Lopinavir phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of lopinavir In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Methadone phenytoin accelerates metabolism of methadone (reduced effect and risk of withdrawal effects)
Methotrexate phenytoin increases antifolate effect of methotrexate
Methylphenidateplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by methylphenidate
Metronidazolemetabolism of phenytoin possibly inhibited by metronidazole (increased plasma concentration)Interactions do not apply to topical metronidazole preparations
Mianserin phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of mianserin
Miconazoleanticonvulsant effect of phenytoin enhanced by miconazole (plasma concentration of phenytoin increased)
Mirtazapine phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of mirtazapine
Modafinilplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by modafinil
Muscle Relaxants, non-depolarising phenytoin antagonises muscle relaxant effect of non-depolarising muscle relaxants (accelerated recovery from neuromuscular blockade)
Nelfinavirplasma concentration of phenytoin reduced by nelfinavir
Nimodipineavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of nimodipine (plasma concentration of nimodipine possibly reduced)
Oestrogens phenytoin accelerates metabolism of oestrogens (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Omeprazoleeffects of phenytoin possibly enhanced by omeprazole
Ondansetron phenytoin accelerates metabolism of ondansetron (reduced effect)
Oxcarbazepineplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by oxcarbazepine , also plasma concentration of an active metabolite of oxcarbazepine reduced
Paracetamol phenytoin possibly accelerates metabolism of paracetamol (also isolated reports of hepatotoxicity)
Paroxetine phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of paroxetine
Phenobarbital phenytoin often increases plasma concentration of phenobarbital , plasma concentration of phenytoin often reduced but may be increased
Posaconazole phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of posaconazole
Progestogens phenytoin accelerates metabolism of progestogens (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5
Pyrimethamineanticonvulsant effect of phenytoin antagonised by pyrimethamine , also increased antifolate effect
Quetiapine phenytoin accelerates metabolism of quetiapine (reduced plasma concentration)
Retigabine phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of retigabine
Rifamycinsmetabolism of phenytoin accelerated by rifamycins (reduced plasma concentration)Interactions do not apply to rifaximin
Ritonavir phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of ritonavir , also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly affected
Rufinamide phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of rufinamide , also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased
Saquinavir phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of saquinavir
Sertralineplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by sertraline , also plasma concentration of sertraline possibly reduced
Stiripentolplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by stiripentol
Sucralfateabsorption of phenytoin reduced by sucralfate
Sulfinpyrazoneplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by sulfinpyrazone
Sulfonamidesplasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased by sulfonamides
Tacrolimus phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of tacrolimus , also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increasedInteractions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Telapreviravoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of telaprevir
Telithromycin phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of telithromycin (avoid during and for 2 weeks after phenytoin)
Theophyllineplasma concentration of both drugs reduced when phenytoin given with theophylline
Thyroid Hormones phenytoin accelerates metabolism of thyroid hormones (may increase requirements in hypothyroidism), also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased
Tiagabine phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of tiagabine
Tibolone phenytoin accelerates metabolism of tibolone
Ticagrelor phenytoin possibly reduces plasma concentration of ticagrelor
Tolbutamideplasma concentration of phenytoin transiently increased by tolbutamide (possibility of toxicity)
Topiramateplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by topiramate (also plasma concentration of topiramate reduced)
Toremifene phenytoin possibly accelerates metabolism of toremifene
Trimethoprimplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by trimethoprim (also increased antifolate effect)
Ulipristalavoidance of phenytoin advised by manufacturer of ulipristal (contraceptive effect of ulipristal possibly reduced)
Valproateplasma concentration of phenytoin increased or possibly reduced when given with valproate , also plasma concentration of valproate reduced
Verapamil phenytoin reduces effects of verapamil
Vigabatrinplasma concentration of phenytoin reduced by vigabatrin
Vitamin D phenytoin possibly increases requirements for vitamin D
Voriconazoleplasma concentration of phenytoin increased by voriconazole , also phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole (increase dose of voriconazole and also monitor for phenytoin toxicity)
Zidovudineplasma concentration of phenytoin increased or decreased by zidovudine Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature
Zonisamide phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of zonisamide

Fosphenytoin belongs to Antiepileptics and will have the following interactions:

Antidepressants, SSRIanticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by SSRIs (convulsive threshold lowered)
Antidepressants, Tricyclicanticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by tricyclics (convulsive threshold lowered)
Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics possibly antagonised by tricyclic-related antidepressants (convulsive threshold lowered)
Antipsychoticsanticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by antipsychotics (convulsive threshold lowered)Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquinepossible increased risk of convulsions when antiepileptics given with chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine
MAOIsanticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics possibly antagonised by MAOIs (convulsive threshold lowered)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Mefloquineanticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by mefloquine
Orlistatpossible increased risk of convulsions when antiepileptics given with orlistat
St John's Wortavoid concomitant use of antiepileptics with St John's wort
