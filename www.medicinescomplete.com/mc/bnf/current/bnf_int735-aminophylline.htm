Aminophylline

Aminophylline has no specific interaction information.

Aminophylline belongs to Theophylline and will have the following interaction information:

Acetazolamide

increased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with acetazolamide

Aciclovir

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by aciclovir

Note: Interactions do not apply to topical aciclovir preparations

Adenosine

theophylline antagonises anti-arrhythmic effect of adenosine

Note: Possibility of interaction with drugs tending to impair myocardial conduction

Allopurinol

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by allopurinol

Benzodiazepines

theophylline possibly reduces effects of benzodiazepines

Calcium-channel Blockers

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by calcium-channel blockers (enhanced effect)

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Carbamazepine

metabolism of theophylline accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced effect)

Cimetidine

metabolism of theophylline inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Ciprofloxacin

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by ciprofloxacin

Clarithromycin

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by clarithromycin

Corticosteroids

increased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Deferasirox

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by deferasirox (consider reducing dose of theophylline)

Diltiazem

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by diltiazem

Disulfiram

metabolism of theophylline inhibited by disulfiram (increased risk of toxicity)

Diuretics, Loop

increased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with loop diuretics

Diuretics, Thiazide and related

increased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with thiazides and related diuretics

Doxapram

increased CNS stimulation when theophylline given with doxapram

Ephedrine

manufacturer of theophylline advises avoid concomitant use with ephedrine in children

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by erythromycin (also theophylline may reduce absorption of oralerythromycin)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Febuxostat

caution with theophylline advised by manufacturer of febuxostat

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by fluconazole

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fluvoxamine

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by fluvoxamine (concomitant use should usually be avoided, but where not possible halve theophylline dose and monitor plasma-theophylline concentration)

Halothane

increased risk of arrhythmias when theophylline given with halothane

Influenza Vaccine

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by influenza vaccine

Interferon Alfa

metabolism of theophylline inhibited by interferon alfa (consider reducing dose of theophylline)

Isoniazid

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by isoniazid

Ketamine

increased risk of convulsions when theophylline given with ketamine

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by ketoconazole

Lithium

theophylline increases excretion of lithium (reduced plasma concentration)

Methotrexate

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by methotrexate

Norfloxacin

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by norfloxacin

Oestrogens

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by oestrogens (consider reducing dose of theophylline)

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Pentoxifylline

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by pentoxifylline

Phenobarbital

metabolism of theophylline accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced effect)

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of both drugs reduced when theophylline given with phenytoin

Propafenone

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by propafenone

Quinolones

possible increased risk of convulsions when theophylline given with quinolones

Rifampicin

metabolism of theophylline accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

Ritonavir

metabolism of theophylline accelerated by ritonavir (reduced plasma concentration)

Roflumilast

avoidance of theophylline advised by manufacturer of roflumilast

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of theophylline reduced by St John's wort —avoid concomitant use

Sucralfate

absorption of theophylline possibly reduced by sucralfate (give at least 2 hours apart)

Sulfinpyrazone

plasma concentration of theophylline reduced by sulfinpyrazone

Sympathomimetics, Beta2

increased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with high doses of beta2 sympathomimetics —see Hypokalaemia, section 3.1.1.1

Verapamil

plasma concentration of theophylline increased by verapamil (enhanced effect)

Zafirlukast

plasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by zafirlukast , also plasma concentration of zafirlukast reduced
