Flecainide

Flecainide has the following interaction information:

Acetazolamideincreased cardiac toxicity with flecainide if hypokalaemia occurs with acetazolamide
Amiodaroneplasma concentration of flecainide increased by amiodarone (halve dose of flecainide)Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antidepressants, Tricyclicincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when flecainide given with tricyclics
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of flecainide advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Beta-blockersincreased risk of myocardial depression and bradycardia when flecainide given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Cimetidinemetabolism of flecainide inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)
Clozapineincreased risk of arrhythmias when flecainide given with clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Diuretics, Loopincreased cardiac toxicity with flecainide if hypokalaemia occurs with loop diuretics
Diuretics, Thiazide and relatedincreased cardiac toxicity with flecainide if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics
Fluoxetineplasma concentration of flecainide increased by fluoxetine
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of flecainide possibly increased by fosamprenavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Indinavirplasma concentration of flecainide possibly increased by indinavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Lopinavirplasma concentration of flecainide possibly increased by lopinavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Mizolastineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when flecainide given with mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Quinineplasma concentration of flecainide increased by quinine
Ritonavirplasma concentration of flecainide possibly increased by ritonavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when flecainide given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Telaprevircaution with flecainide advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Tolterodineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when flecainide given with tolterodine
Verapamilincreased risk of myocardial depression and asystole when flecainide given with verapamil

Flecainide belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and will have the following interactions:

Anti-arrhythmicsincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with other anti-arrhythmics
Antipsychoticsincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT intervalIncreased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Beta-blockersincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Bupivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with bupivacaine
Levobupivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with levobupivacaine
Prilocaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with prilocaine
Ropivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with ropivacaine
