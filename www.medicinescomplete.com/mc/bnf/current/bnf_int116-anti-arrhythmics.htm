Anti-arrhythmics

Anti-arrhythmics has the following interaction information:

Anti-arrhythmics

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with other anti-arrhythmics

Antipsychotics

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Beta-blockers

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Bupivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with bupivacaine

Levobupivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with levobupivacaine

Prilocaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with prilocaine

Ropivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with ropivacaine

Dronedarone belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with tricyclics (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Atorvastatin

dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of atorvastatin

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by carbamazepine —avoid concomitant use

Clarithromycin

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with clarithromycin (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Coumarins

dronedarone possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Dabigatran Etexilate

dronedarone increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate —avoid concomitant use

Digoxin

dronedarone increases plasma concentration of digoxin (halve dose of digoxin)

Diltiazem

increased risk of bradycardia and myocardial depression when dronedarone given with diltiazem

Disopyramide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with disopyramide —avoid concomitant use

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of dronedarone increased by erythromycin (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Fidaxomicin

avoidance of dronedarone advised by manufacturer of fidaxomicin

Fingolimod

possible increased risk of bradycardia when dronedarone given with fingolimod

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of dronedarone increased by grapefruit juice —avoid concomitant use

Itraconazole

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with itraconazole

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of dronedarone increased by ketoconazole —avoid concomitant use

Metoprolol

dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of metoprolol

Nifedipine

plasma concentration of dronedarone increased by nifedipine

Phenindione

dronedarone possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of phenindione

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by phenobarbital —avoid concomitant use

Phenothiazines

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with phenothiazines (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by phenytoin —avoid concomitant use

Posaconazole

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with posaconazole

Propranolol

dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of propranolol

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of dronedarone reduced by rifampicin —avoid concomitant use

Ritonavir

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with ritonavir

Rosuvastatin

dronedarone possibly increases plasma concentration of rosuvastatin

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Simvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when dronedarone given with simvastatin

Sirolimus

manufacturer of dronedarone advises caution with sirolimus

Sotalol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when dronedarone given with sotalol —avoid concomitant use

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of dronedarone possibly reduced by St John's wort —avoid concomitant use

Tacrolimus

manufacturer of dronedarone advises caution with tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telithromycin

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with telithromycin (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Verapamil

increased risk of bradycardia and myocardial depression when dronedarone given with verapamil

Voriconazole

manufacturer of dronedarone advises avoid concomitant use with voriconazole

Adenosine belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Note: Possibility of interaction with drugs tending to impair myocardial conduction

Dipyridamole

effect of adenosine enhanced and extended by dipyridamole (important risk of toxicity)

Nicotine

effects of adenosine possibly enhanced by nicotine

Theophylline

anti-arrhythmic effect of adenosine antagonised by theophylline

Amiodarone belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Agalsidase Alfa and Beta

amiodarone possibly inhibits effects of agalsidase alfa and beta (manufacturers of agalsidase alfa and beta advise avoid concomitant use)

Amisulpride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with amisulpride —avoid concomitant use

Disopyramide belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Acetazolamide

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

Antimuscarinics

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when disopyramide given with antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Flecainide belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and has the following interaction information:

Acetazolamide

increased cardiac toxicity with flecainide if hypokalaemia occurs with acetazolamide

Amiodarone

plasma concentration of flecainide increased by amiodarone (halve dose of flecainide)

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when flecainide given with tricyclics

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of flecainide advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Beta-blockers

increased risk of myocardial depression and bradycardia when flecainide given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Cimetidine

metabolism of flecainide inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Clozapine

increased risk of arrhythmias when flecainide given with clozapine

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Diuretics, Loop

increased cardiac toxicity with flecainide if hypokalaemia occurs with loop diuretics

increased cardiac toxicity with flecainide if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics

plasma concentration of flecainide possibly increased by indinavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

plasma concentration of flecainide possibly increased by lopinavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Mizolastine
