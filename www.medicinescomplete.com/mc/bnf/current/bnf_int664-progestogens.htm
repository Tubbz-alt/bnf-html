Progestogens

Progestogens has the following interaction information:

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

Antidiabetics

progestogens antagonise hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics

Aprepitant

possible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing progestogens when given with aprepitant (alternative contraception recommended)

Bosentan

possible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing progestogens when given with bosentan (alternative contraception recommended)

Carbamazepine

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Ciclosporin

progestogens possibly increase plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Coumarins

progestogens may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Diazepam

progestogens possibly increase plasma concentration of diazepam

Efavirenz

contraceptive effect of progestogens possibly reduced by efavirenz

Eslicarbazepine

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by eslicarbazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Griseofulvin

anecdotal reports of contraceptive failure and menstrual irregularities when progestogens given with griseofulvin

Nelfinavir

contraceptive effect of progestogens possibly reduced by nelfinavir

Nevirapine

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by nevirapine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Oxcarbazepine

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by oxcarbazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Phenindione

progestogens antagonise anticoagulant effect of phenindione

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Phenobarbital

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Phenytoin

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by phenytoin (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Rifamycins

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by rifamycins (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

Rufinamide

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by rufinamide (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Selegiline

progestogens increase plasma concentration of selegiline —manufacturer of selegiline advises avoid concomitant use

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

St John's Wort

contraceptive effect of progestogens reduced by St John's wort (avoid concomitant use)

Sugammadex

plasma concentration of progestogens possibly reduced by sugammadex —manufacturer of sugammadex advises additional contraceptive precautions

Terbinafine

occasional reports of breakthrough bleeding when progestogens (used for contraception) given with terbinafine

Tizanidine

progestogens possibly increase plasma concentration of tizanidine (increased risk of toxicity)

Topiramate

metabolism of progestogens accelerated by topiramate (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Ulipristal

contraceptive effect of progestogens possibly reduced by ulipristal

Vemurafenib

possible reduction in contraceptive effect of progestogens advised by manufacturer of vemurafenib

Voriconazole

progestogens possibly increase plasma concentration of voriconazole

Dienogest belongs to Progestogens and has the following interaction information:

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of dienogest increased by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Norelgestromin belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Drospirenone belongs to Progestogens and has the following interaction information:

Boceprevir

plasma concentration of drospirenone increased by boceprevir (increased risk of toxicity)

Diuretics, Potassium-sparing and Aldosterone Antagonists

risk of hyperkalaemia when drospirenone given with potassium-sparing diuretics and aldosterone antagonists (monitor serum potassium during first cycle)

Desogestrel belongs to Progestogens and has the following interaction information:

Lamotrigine

desogestrel possibly increases plasma concentration of lamotrigine

Dydrogesterone belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Etonogestrel belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Etynodiol belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Gestodene belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Levonorgestrel belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Medroxyprogesterone belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Megestrol belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.

Norethisterone belongs to Progestogens and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of norethisterone increased by atazanavir

Atorvastatin

plasma concentration of norethisterone increased by atorvastatin

Norgestimate belongs to Progestogens and has the following interaction information:

Rosuvastatin

plasma concentration of active metabolite of norgestimate increased by rosuvastatin

Norgestrel belongs to Progestogens and has the following interaction information:

Rosuvastatin

plasma concentration of norgestrel increased by rosuvastatin

Progesterone belongs to Progestogens but has no specific interaction information.
