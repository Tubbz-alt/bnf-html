Phenindione

Phenindione has the following interaction information:

Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Alcoholanticoagulant control with phenindione may be affected by major changes in consumption of alcohol
Amiodaronemetabolism of phenindione inhibited by amiodarone (enhanced anticoagulant effect)Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Anabolic Steroidsanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by anabolic steroids
Aspirinincreased risk of bleeding when phenindione given with aspirin (due to antiplatelet effect)
Clopidogrelanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced due to antiplatelet action of clopidogrel
Colestyramineanticoagulant effect of phenindione may be enhanced or reduced by colestyramine Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption
Corticosteroidsanticoagulant effect of phenindione may be enhanced or reduced by corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Dipyridamoleanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced due to antiplatelet action of dipyridamole
Dronedaroneanticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by dronedarone
Enteral Foodsanticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by vitamin K (present in some enteral feeds )
Fibratesanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by fibrates
Iloprostincreased risk of bleeding when phenindione given with iloprost
Levofloxacinanticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by levofloxacin
Neomycinexperience in anticoagulant clinics suggests that INR possibly altered when phenindione is given with neomycin (given for local action on gut)
NSAIDsanticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Oestrogensanticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by oestrogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Penicillinsan interaction between phenindione and broad-spectrum penicillins has not been demonstrated in studies, but common experience in anticoagulant clinics is that INR can be altered
Prasugrelpossible increased risk of bleeding when phenindione given with prasugrel
Progestogensanticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by progestogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5
Ritonaviranticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by ritonavir
Rosuvastatinanticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by rosuvastatin
Sulfonamidesmetabolism of phenindione possibly inhibited by sulfonamides
Testolactoneanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by testolactone
Testosteroneanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by testosterone
Tetracyclinesanticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by tetracyclines
Thyroid Hormonesanticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by thyroid hormones
Vitamin K (Phytomenadione)anticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by vitamin K

Phenindione belongs to Anticoagulants and will have the following interactions:

Diclofenacincreased risk of haemorrhage when anticoagulants given with intravenous diclofenac (avoid concomitant use, including low-dose heparins)
Ketorolacincreased risk of haemorrhage when anticoagulants given with ketorolac (avoid concomitant use, including low-dose heparins)
