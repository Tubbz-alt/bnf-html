    LANSOPRAZOLE

    Additional information

     

    interactions (Lansoprazole).

    Indications

     

    see under Dose

    Cautions

     

    see notes above; interactions: Appendix 1 (proton pump inhibitors)

    Hepatic impairment

     

    use half normal dose in moderate to severe liver disease

    Pregnancy

     

    manufacturer advises avoid

    Breast-feeding

     

    avoid—present in milk in animal studies

    Side-effects

     

    see notes above; also glossitis, pancreatitis, anorexia, restlessness, tremor, impotence, petechiae, and purpura; very rarely colitis, raised serum cholesterol or triglycerides

    Dose

    • Benign gastric ulcer, 30 mg daily in the morning for 8 weeks

    • Duodenal ulcer, 30 mg daily in the morning for 4 weeks; maintenance 15 mg daily

    • NSAID-associated duodenal or gastric ulcer, 30 mg once daily for 4 weeks, continued for further 4 weeks if not fully healed; prophylaxis, 15–30 mg once daily

    • Eradication of Helicobacter pylori associated with duodenal ulcer or ulcer-like dyspepsia, see Recommended Regimens for Helicobacter pylori Eradication

    • Zollinger-Ellison syndrome (and other hypersecretory conditions), initially 60 mg once daily adjusted according to response; daily doses of 120 mg or more given in two divided doses

    • Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease, 30 mg daily in the morning for 4 weeks, continued for further 4 weeks if not fully healed; maintenance 15–30 mg daily

    • Acid-related dyspepsia, 15–30 mg daily in the morning for 2–4 weeks

    • child under 18 years see BNF for Children

    Note

     

    Lansoprazole doses in BNF may differ from those in product literature
