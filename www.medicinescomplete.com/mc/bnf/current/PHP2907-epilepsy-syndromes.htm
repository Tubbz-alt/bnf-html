Epilepsy syndromes

Some drugs are licensed for use in particular epilepsy syndromes, such as lamotrigine and rufinamide in Lennox-Gastaut syndrome. The epilepsy syndromes are specific types of epilepsy that are characterised according to a number of features including seizure type, age of onset, and EEG characteristics.

For more information on epilepsy syndromes in children, see BNF for Children, section 4.8.1. Prescribing information for stiripentol (Diacomit®) in severe myoclonic epilepsy of infancy (Dravet syndrome) can also be found in BNF for Children.
