Antimalarials

Antimalarials has the following interaction information:

Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of antimalarials advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Histamineavoidance of antimalarials advised by manufacturer of histamine
Typhoid Vaccine (oral) antimalarials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine—see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4

Artemether with Lumefantrine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Amiodaronemanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with amiodarone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antidepressantsmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with antidepressants
Antifungals, Imidazolemanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with imidazoles
Antifungals, Triazolemanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with triazoles
Antimalarialsmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with antimalarials
Antipsychoticsmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with antipsychotics Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Atazanavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with atazanavir
Bocepreviravoidance of artemether/lumefantrine advised by manufacturer of boceprevir
Cimetidinemanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with cimetidine
Darunavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with darunavir
Disopyramidemanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with disopyramide (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Flecainidemanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with flecainide (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Fosamprenavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with fosamprenavir Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Grapefruit Juiceplasma concentration of artemether/lumefantrine possibly increased by grapefruit juice
Indinavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with indinavir
Lopinavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with lopinavir In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Macrolidesmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with macrolides See also Telithromycin
Metoprololmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with metoprolol
Nelfinavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with nelfinavir
Quinineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when artemether/lumefantrine given with quinine
Quinolonesmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with quinolones
Ritonavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with ritonavir
Saquinavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with saquinavir
Sotalolmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with sotalol
Tipranavirmanufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with tipranavir

Lumefantrine belongs to Antimalarials but has no specific interaction information.

Lumefantrine interactions as for artemether with lumefantrine

Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Agalsidase Alfa and Beta chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly inhibit effects of agalsidase alfa and beta (manufacturers of agalsidase alfa and beta advise avoid concomitant use)
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antacidsabsorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Antiepilepticspossible increased risk of convulsions when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with antiepileptics
Ciclosporin chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine increase plasma concentration of ciclosporin (increased risk of toxicity)
Cimetidinemetabolism of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)
Digoxin chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly increase plasma concentration of digoxin
Droperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with droperidol—avoid concomitant use
Kaolinabsorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine reduced by kaolin
Lanthanumabsorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly reduced by lanthanum (give at least 2 hours apart)
Laronidase chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly inhibit effects of laronidase (manufacturer of laronidase advises avoid concomitant use)
Mefloquineincreased risk of convulsions when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with mefloquine
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Neostigmine chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine have potential to increase symptoms of myasthenia gravis and thus diminish effect of neostigmine
Pyridostigmine chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine have potential to increase symptoms of myasthenia gravis and thus diminish effect of pyridostigmine

Hydroxychloroquine belongs to Antimalarials but has no specific interaction information.

Hydroxychloroquine interactions as for chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine

Mefloquine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Antiepileptics mefloquine antagonises anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with atomoxetine
Beta-blockersincreased risk of bradycardia when mefloquine given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Calcium-channel Blockerspossible increased risk of bradycardia when mefloquine given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquineincreased risk of convulsions when mefloquine given with chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine
Digoxinpossible increased risk of bradycardia when mefloquine given with digoxin
Haloperidolpossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with haloperidol—avoid concomitant use
Ivabradineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with ivabradine
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of mefloquine increased by ketoconazole
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quinineincreased risk of convulsions when mefloquine given with quinine (but should not prevent the use of intravenous quinine in severe cases)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of mefloquine reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use

Primaquine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Mepacrineplasma concentration of primaquine increased by mepacrine (increased risk of toxicity)

Proguanil belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Magnesium Salts (oral)absorption of proguanil reduced by oral magnesium salts (as magnesium trisilicate)
Pyrimethamineincreased antifolate effect when proguanil given with pyrimethamine
Warfarinisolated reports that proguanil may enhance anticoagulant effect of warfarin

Pyrimethamine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Methotrexate pyrimethamine increases antifolate effect of methotrexate
Pemetrexed pyrimethamine increases antifolate effect of pemetrexed
Phenytoin pyrimethamine antagonises anticonvulsant effect of phenytoin , also increased antifolate effect
Proguanilincreased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with proguanil
Sulfonamidesincreased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with sulfonamides
Trimethoprimincreased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with trimethoprim
Zidovudineincreased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with zidovudine Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Quinine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Amantadine quinine possibly increases plasma concentration of amantadine
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Artemether with Lumefantrineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with artemether/lumefantrine
Atazanavirplasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by atazanavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Cimetidinemetabolism of quinine inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)
Darunavirplasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by darunavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Digoxin quinine increases plasma concentration of digoxin
Droperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with droperidol—avoid concomitant use
Flecainide quinine increases plasma concentration of flecainide
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by fosamprenavir (increased risk of toxicity)Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Haloperidolpossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with haloperidol—avoid concomitant use
Indinavirplasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by indinavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Mefloquineincreased risk of convulsions when quinine given with mefloquine (but should not prevent the use of intravenous quinine in severe cases)
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Nelfinavirplasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by nelfinavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Rifampicinplasma concentration of quinine reduced by rifampicin
Ritonavirplasma concentration of quinine increased by ritonavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Suxamethonium quinine possibly enhances effects of suxamethonium
Tipranavirplasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by tipranavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Warfarinplasma concentration of both drugs increased when quinine given with warfarin
