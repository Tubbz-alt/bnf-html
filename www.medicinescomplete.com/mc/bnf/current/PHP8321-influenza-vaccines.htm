INFLUENZA VACCINES

Additional information

 

interactions (Influenza Vaccine).

Indications

 

annual immunisation against seasonal influenza

Cautions

 

see section 14.1; increased risk of fever in child under 5 years with Viroflu®, and in child 5–9 years with Enzira® or preparations marketed by Pfizer or CSL Biotherapies; interactions: Appendix 1 (vaccines)

Contra-indications

 

see section 14.1; avoid Enzira® or preparations marketed by Pfizer, or CSL Biotherapies in child under 5 years—increased risk of febrile convulsions

Pregnancy

 

see section 14.1; inactivated vaccines not known to be harmful; avoid Fluenz®

Breast-feeding

 

see section 14.1; inactivated vaccines not known to be harmful; avoid Fluenz®

Side-effects

 

see section 14.1; also reported febrile convulsions and transient thrombocytopenia

Dose

  • By intramuscular injection, adult and child over 13 years, 0.5 mL as a single dose; child 6 months–3 years, 0.25–0.5 mL; 3–13 years 0.5 mL; for children 6 months to 13 years who have not received seasonal influenza vaccine previously, repeat after 4–6 weeks

  • By intradermal injection, see under Intanza® below

  • Intranasally, see under Fluenz® below
