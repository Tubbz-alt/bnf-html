Lipid-regulating Drugs

Lipid-regulating Drugs has no specific interaction information.

Ezetimibe belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Ciclosporinplasma concentration of both drugs may increase when ezetimibe given with ciclosporin
Coumarins ezetimibe possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Fibratesincreased risk of cholelithiasis and gallbladder disease when ezetimibe given with fibrates—discontinue if suspected

Bile Acid Sequestrants belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs but has no specific interaction information.

Colesevelam belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption
Ciclosporin colesevelam reduces absorption of ciclosporin
Ethinylestradiol colesevelam reduces absorption of ethinylestradiol
Glibenclamide colesevelam reduces absorption of glibenclamide
Levothyroxine colesevelam reduces absorption of levothyroxine
Phenytoin colesevelam possibly reduces absorption of phenytoin

Colestipol belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestipol to reduce possible interference with absorption
Bile Acids colestipol possibly reduces absorption of bile acids
Cardiac Glycosides colestipol possibly reduces absorption of cardiac glycosides
Diuretics, Thiazide and related colestipol reduces absorption of thiazides and related diuretics (give at least 2 hours apart)
Tetracycline colestipol possibly reduces absorption of tetracycline
Thyroid Hormones colestipol reduces absorption of thyroid hormones

Colestyramine belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption
Acarbose colestyramine possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of acarbose
Bile Acids colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of bile acids
Cardiac Glycosides colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of cardiac glycosides
Coumarins colestyramine may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diuretics, Thiazide and related colestyramine reduces absorption of thiazides and related diuretics (give at least 2 hours apart)
Leflunomide colestyramine significantly decreases effect of leflunomide (enhanced elimination)—avoid unless drug elimination desiredIncreased risk of toxicity with other haematotoxic and hepatotoxic drugs
Meloxicam colestyramine increases the excretion of meloxicam
Mycophenolate colestyramine reduces absorption of mycophenolate
Paracetamol colestyramine reduces absorption of paracetamol
Phenindione colestyramine may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of phenindione Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Raloxifene colestyramine reduces absorption of raloxifene (manufacturer of raloxifene advises avoid concomitant administration)
Tetracycline colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of tetracycline
Thyroid Hormones colestyramine reduces absorption of thyroid hormones
Valproate colestyramine possibly reduces absorption of valproate
Vancomycin colestyramine antagonises effects of oral vancomycin

Fibrates belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Colchicinepossible increased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with colchicine
Coumarins fibrates enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Daptomycinincreased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with daptomycin (preferably avoid concomitant use)
Ezetimibeincreased risk of cholelithiasis and gallbladder disaese when fibrates given with ezetimibe—discontinue if suspected
Insulin fibrates may improve glucose tolerance and have an additive effect with insulin
Phenindione fibrates enhance anticoagulant effect of phenindione Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Statinsincreased risk of myopathy when fibrates given with statins
Sulfonylureas fibrates may improve glucose tolerance and have an additive effect with sulfonylureas

Bezafibrate belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Ciclosporinincreased risk of renal impairment when bezafibrate given with ciclosporin

Ciprofibrate belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs but has no specific interaction information.

Fenofibrate belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Ciclosporinincreased risk of renal impairment when fenofibrate given with ciclosporin

Gemfibrozil belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Bexarotene gemfibrozil increases plasma concentration of bexarotene—avoid concomitant use
Nateglinide gemfibrozil possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide
Repaglinideincreased risk of severe hypoglycaemia when gemfibrozil given with repaglinide—avoid concomitant use
Statinsincreased risk of myopathy when gemfibrozil given with statins (preferably avoid concomitant use)

Statins belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Ciclosporinincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with ciclosporin
Colchicinepossible increased risk of myopathy when statins given with colchicine
Daptomycinincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with daptomycin (preferably avoid concomitant use)
Fibratesincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with fibrates
Fusidic Acidrisk of myopathy and rhabdomyolysis when statins given with fusidic acid—avoid concomitant use and for 7 days after last fusidic acid dose
Gemfibrozilincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with gemfibrozil (preferably avoid concomitant use)
Nicotinic Acidincreased risk of myopathy when statins given with nicotinic acid (applies to lipid regulating doses of nicotinic acid)Interactions apply to lipid-regulating doses of nicotinic acid

Antacidsabsorption of rosuvastatin reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Atazanavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with atazanavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Ciclosporinincreased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with ciclosporin (avoid concomitant use)
Coumarins rosuvastatin possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Darunavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with darunavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Dronedaroneplasma concentration of rosuvastatin possibly increased by dronedarone
Eltrombopagplasma concentration of rosuvastatin increased by eltrombopag (consider reducing dose of rosuvastatin)
Erythromycinplasma concentration of rosuvastatin reduced by erythromycin Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Ethinylestradiol rosuvastatin increases plasma concentration of ethinylestradiol
Fosamprenavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with fosamprenavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant useFosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Indinavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with indinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Lopinavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with lopinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant useIn combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir
Nelfinavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with nelfinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Norgestimate rosuvastatin increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of norgestimate
Norgestrel rosuvastatin increases plasma concentration of norgestrel
Phenindione rosuvastatin possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of phenindione Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Ritonavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with ritonavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Saquinavirpossible increased risk of myopathy when rosuvastatin given with saquinavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use
Tipranavirplasma concentration of rosuvastatin possibly increased by tipranavir—manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

Fluvastatin belongs to Lipid-regulating Drugs and has the following interaction information:

Coumarins fluvastatin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Fluconazoleplasma concentration of fluvastatin increased by fluconazole In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Glibenclamide fluvastatin possibly increases plasma concentration of glibenclamide
Phenytoincombination of fluvastatin with phenytoin may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)
Rifampicinmetabolism of fluvastatin accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)
