Oestrogens

Oestrogens has the following interaction information:

Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
ACE Inhibitors oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors
Adrenergic Neurone Blockers oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers
Alpha-blockers oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of alpha-blockers
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Antidepressants, Tricyclic oestrogens antagonise antidepressant effect of tricyclics (but side-effects of tricyclics possibly increased due to increased plasma concentration)
Antidiabetics oestrogens antagonise hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics
Antifungals, Imidazoleanecdotal reports of contraceptive failure when oestrogens given with imidazoles
Aprepitantpossible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing oestrogens when given with aprepitant (alternative contraception recommended)
Beta-blockers oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Boceprevirpossible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing oestrogens when given with boceprevir (alternative contraception recommended)
Bosentanpossible contraceptive failure of hormonal contraceptives containing oestrogens when given with bosentan (alternative contraception recommended)
Calcium-channel Blockers oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Carbamazepinemetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Ciclosporin oestrogens possibly increase plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Clonidine oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of clonidine
Corticosteroidsoral contraceptives containing oestrogens increase plasma concentration of corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Coumarins oestrogens may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam oestrogens possibly increase plasma concentration of diazepam
Diuretics oestrogens antagonise diuretic effect of diuretics
Eslicarbazepinemetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by eslicarbazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Griseofulvinanecdotal reports of contraceptive failure and menstrual irregularities when oestrogens given with griseofulvin
Hydralazine oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of hydralazine
Lamotrigine oestrogens reduce plasma concentration of lamotrigine—consider increasing dose of lamotrigine
Melatonin oestrogens increase plasma concentration of melatonin
Methyldopa oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of methyldopa
Minoxidil oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of minoxidil
Modafinilmetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by modafinil (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Moxonidine oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of moxonidine
Nelfinavirmetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by nelfinavir (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Nevirapinemetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by nevirapine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Nitrates oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of nitrates
Oxcarbazepinemetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by oxcarbazepine (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Phenindione oestrogens antagonise anticoagulant effect of phenindione Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Phenobarbitalmetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Phenytoinmetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by phenytoin (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Rifamycinsmetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by rifamycins (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)Interactions do not apply to rifaximin
Ritonavirmetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by ritonavir (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Ropinirole oestrogens increase plasma concentration of ropinirole
Rufinamidemetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by rufinamide (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Selegiline oestrogens increase plasma concentration of selegiline—manufacturer of selegiline advises avoid concomitant useSelegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sodium Nitroprusside oestrogens antagonise hypotensive effect of sodium nitroprusside
Somatropin oestrogens (when used as oral replacement therapy) may increase dose requirements of somatropin
St John's Wortcontraceptive effect of oestrogens reduced by St John's wort (avoid concomitant use)
Terbinafineoccasional reports of breakthrough bleeding when oestrogens (used for contraception) given with terbinafine
Theophylline oestrogens increase plasma concentration of theophylline (consider reducing dose of theophylline)
Thyroid Hormones oestrogens may increase requirements for thyroid hormones in hypothyroidism
Tizanidine oestrogens possibly increase plasma concentration of tizanidine (increased risk of toxicity)
Topiramatemetabolism of oestrogens accelerated by topiramate (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)
Voriconazole oestrogens increase plasma concentration of voriconazole

Estradiol belongs to Oestrogens and has the following interaction information:

Erythromycinplasma concentration of estradiol increased by erythromycin Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Estriol belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.

Estrone belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.

Ethinylestradiol belongs to Oestrogens and has the following interaction information:

Atazanavirplasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by atazanavir
Atorvastatinplasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by atorvastatin
Colesevelamabsorption of ethinylestradiol reduced by colesevelam Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption
Etoricoxibplasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by etoricoxib
Rosuvastatinplasma concentration of ethinylestradiol increased by rosuvastatin
Tacrolimus ethinylestradiol possibly increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Telaprevirplasma concentration of ethinylestradiol possibly reduced by telaprevir—manufacturer of telaprevir advises additional contraceptive precautions
Valproate ethinylestradiol possibly reduces plasma concentration of valproate

Mestranol belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.

Oestrogens, conjugated belongs to Oestrogens but has no specific interaction information.
