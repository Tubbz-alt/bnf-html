Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics has the following interaction information:

ACE Inhibitors

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with ACE inhibitors

Adrenergic Neurone Blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with adrenergic neurone blockers

Alcohol

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with alcohol

Alpha-blockers

enhanced hypotensive and sedative effects when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with alpha-blockers

Anaesthetics, General

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with general anaesthetics

Note: See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with tricyclics

Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with tricyclic-related antidepressants

Antihistamines

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with antihistamines

Note: Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)

Antipsychotics

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with antipsychotics

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Baclofen

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with baclofen

Beta-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Calcium-channel Blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with calcium-channel blockers

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Clonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with clonidine

Diazoxide

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with diazoxide

Diuretics

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with diuretics

Hydralazine

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with hydralazine

Lofexidine

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with lofexidine

Methyldopa

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with methyldopa

Minoxidil

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with minoxidil

Mirtazapine

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with mirtazapine

Moxonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with moxonidine

Nitrates

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with nitrates

Opioid Analgesics

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with opioid analgesics

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of anxiolytics and hypnotics possibly increased by ritonavir

Sodium Nitroprusside

enhanced hypotensive effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with sodium nitroprusside

Tizanidine

increased sedative effect when anxiolytics and hypnotics given with tizanidine

Melatonin belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidine

plasma concentration of melatonin increased by cimetidine

Fluvoxamine

plasma concentration of melatonin increased by fluvoxamine —avoid concomitant use

Oestrogens

plasma concentration of melatonin increased by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Benzodiazepines belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidine

metabolism of benzodiazepines inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Clozapine

serious adverse events reported with concomitant use of benzodiazepines and clozapine (causality not established)

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Disulfiram

metabolism of benzodiazepines inhibited by disulfiram (increased sedative effects)

Fluvoxamine

plasma concentration of some benzodiazepines increased by fluvoxamine

Levodopa

benzodiazepines possibly antagonise effects of levodopa

Moxonidine

sedative effects possibly increased when benzodiazepines given with moxonidine

Olanzapine

increased risk of hypotension, bradycardia and respiratory depression when parenteral benzodiazepines given with intramuscular olanzapine

Phenytoin

benzodiazepines possibly increase or decrease plasma concentration of phenytoin

Rifampicin

metabolism of benzodiazepines possibly accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

Sodium Oxybate

benzodiazepines enhance effects of sodium oxybate (avoid concomitant use)

Theophylline

effects of benzodiazepines possibly reduced by theophylline

Alprazolam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Digoxin

alprazolam increases plasma concentration of digoxin (increased risk of toxicity)

Fluoxetine

plasma concentration of alprazolam increased by fluoxetine

Haloperidol

alprazolam possibly increases plasma concentration of haloperidol

Indinavir

increased risk of prolonged sedation when alprazolam given with indinavir —avoid concomitant use

Itraconazole

plasma concentration of alprazolam increased by itraconazole

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of alprazolam increased by ketoconazole

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of alprazolam possibly increased by ritonavir (risk of extreme sedation and respiratory depression—avoid concomitant use)

Chlordiazepoxide belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics but has no specific interaction information.

Clobazam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Stiripentol

plasma concentration of clobazam increased by stiripentol

Valproate

clobazam possibly increases plasma concentration of valproate

Clonazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of clonazepam often reduced by carbamazepine

Lithium

increased risk of neurotoxicity when clonazepam given with lithium

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of clonazepam often reduced by phenobarbital

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of clonazepam often reduced by phenytoin

Valproate

increased risk of side-effects when clonazepam given with valproate

Diazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Esomeprazole

metabolism of diazepam possibly inhibited by esomeprazole (increased plasma concentration)

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of diazepam increased by fluconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Isoniazid

metabolism of diazepam inhibited by isoniazid

Oestrogens

plasma concentration of diazepam possibly increased by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Omeprazole

metabolism of diazepam possibly inhibited by omeprazole (increased plasma concentration)

Phenytoin

diazepam increases or decreases plasma concentration of phenytoin

Progestogens

plasma concentration of diazepam possibly increased by progestogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

Rifampicin

metabolism of diazepam accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of diazepam possibly increased by ritonavir (risk of extreme sedation and respiratory depression—avoid concomitant use)

Valproate

plasma concentration of diazepam possibly increased by valproate

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of diazepam increased by voriconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Flurazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of flurazepam possibly increased by ritonavir (risk of extreme sedation and respiratory depression—avoid concomitant use)

Loprazolam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics but has no specific interaction information.

Lorazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Probenecid

excretion of lorazepam reduced by probenecid (increased plasma concentration)

Valproate

plasma concentration of lorazepam possibly increased by valproate

Lormetazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics but has no specific interaction information.

Midazolam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Aprepitant

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by aprepitant (risk of prolonged sedation)

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by atazanavir —avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam

Atorvastatin

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by atorvastatin

Boceprevir

plasma concentration of oral midazolam increased by boceprevir —manufacturer of boceprevir advises avoid concomitant use

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of midazolam reduced by carbamazepine

Clarithromycin

metabolism of midazolam inhibited by clarithromycin (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Deferasirox

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly reduced by deferasirox

Diltiazem

metabolism of midazolam inhibited by diltiazem (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Efavirenz

increased risk of prolonged sedation when midazolam given with efavirenz —avoid concomitant use

Erythromycin

metabolism of midazolam inhibited by erythromycin (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Fentanyl

metabolism of midazolam possibly inhibited by fentanyl

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by fluconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by fosamprenavir (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of oral midazolam possibly increased by grapefruit juice

Indinavir

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by indinavir (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

Itraconazole

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by itraconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by ketoconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Lercanidipine

midazolam increases absorption of lercanidipine

Nelfinavir

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by nelfinavir (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

Nilotinib

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by nilotinib

Posaconazole

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by posaconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by ritonavir (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

Saquinavir

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by saquinavir (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of oral midazolam possibly reduced by St John's wort

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of midazolam possibly increased by telaprevir (risk of prolonged sedation—avoid concomitant use of oralmidazolam)

Telithromycin

metabolism of midazolam inhibited by telithromycin (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Verapamil

metabolism of midazolam inhibited by verapamil (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of midazolam increased by voriconazole (risk of prolonged sedation)

Nitrazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Probenecid

excretion of nitrazepam possibly reduced by probenecid (increased plasma concentration)

Oxazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics but has no specific interaction information.

Temazepam belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Disulfiram

increased risk of temazepam toxicity when given with disulfiram

Buspirone belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Diltiazem

plasma concentration of buspirone increased by diltiazem (reduce dose of buspirone)

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of buspirone increased by erythromycin (reduce dose of buspirone)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of buspirone increased by grapefruit juice

Haloperidol

buspirone increases plasma concentration of haloperidol

Itraconazole

plasma concentration of buspirone increased by itraconazole (reduce dose of buspirone)

MAOIs

manufacturer of buspirone advises avoid concomitant use with MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Methylthioninium

possible risk of CNS toxicity when buspirone given with methylthioninium —avoid concomitant use (if avoidance not possible, use lowest possible dose of methylthioninium and observe patient for up to 4 hours after administration)

Rifampicin

metabolism of buspirone possibly accelerated by rifampicin

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of buspirone increased by ritonavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Tranylcypromine

avoidance of buspirone for 10 days after stopping tranylcypromine advised by manufacturer of tranylcypromine

Verapamil

plasma concentration of buspirone increased by verapamil (reduce dose of buspirone)

Chloral belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Coumarins

chloral may transiently enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Furosemide

administration of chloral with parenteral furosemide may displace thyroid hormone from binding sites

Clomethiazole belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidine

metabolism of clomethiazole inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Meprobamate belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics but has no specific interaction information.

Zaleplon belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidine

metabolism of zaleplon inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Rifampicin

metabolism of zaleplon accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

Zolpidem belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Rifampicin

metabolism of zolpidem accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration and reduced effect)

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of zolpidem possibly increased by ritonavir (risk of extreme sedation and respiratory depression—avoid concomitant use)

Sertraline

sedative effects possibly increased when zolpidem given with sertraline

Zopiclone belongs to Anxiolytics and Hypnotics and has the following interaction information:

Erythromycin

metabolism of zopiclone inhibited by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of zopiclone significantly reduced by rifampicin
