    Antibacterials

    Antibacterials has the following interaction information:

    Typhoid Vaccine (oral)

    antibacterials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine—see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4

    Tigecycline belongs to Antibacterials and has the following interaction information:

    Coumarins

    tigecycline possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Daptomycin belongs to Antibacterials and has the following interaction information:

    Ciclosporin

    increased risk of myopathy when daptomycin given with ciclosporin (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Fibrates

    increased risk of myopathy when daptomycin given with fibrates (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Statins

    increased risk of myopathy when daptomycin given with statins (preferably avoid concomitant use)

    Co-trimoxazole belongs to Antibacterials but has no specific interaction information.

    Note: Co-trimoxazole interactions as for trimethoprim and sulfamethoxazole

    Telithromycin belongs to Antibacterials and has the following interaction information:

    Amiodarone

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with amiodarone

    Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

    Antidepressants, Tricyclic

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with tricyclics

    Aprepitant

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of aprepitant

    Atazanavir

    manufacturer of telithromycin advises avoid concomitant use with atazanavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment

    Atorvastatin

    increased risk of myopathy when telithromycin given with atorvastatin (avoid concomitant use)

    Axitinib

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of axitinib (reduce dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Cabazitaxel

    avoidance of telithromycin advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel

    Calcium-channel Blockers

    telithromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of calcium-channel blockers (increased risk of side-effects)

    Carbamazepine

    plasma concentration of telithromycin reduced by carbamazepine (avoid during and for 2 weeks after carbamazepine)

    Chlorpromazine

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with chlorpromazine

    Ciclosporin

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

    Citalopram

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with citalopram

    Colchicine

    telithromycin possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)

    Crizotinib

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of crizotinib—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Digoxin

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of digoxin

    Disopyramide

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with disopyramide

    Dronedarone

    increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with dronedarone—avoid concomitant use

    Eplerenone

    telithromycin increases plasma concentration of eplerenone—avoid concomitant use

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of ergotism when telithromycin given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use

    Everolimus

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use

    Fesoterodine

    manufacturer of fesoterodine advises dose reduction when telithromycin given with fesoterodine—consult fesoterodine product literature

    Fosamprenavir

    manufacturer of telithromycin advises avoid concomitant use with fosamprenavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment

    Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

    Indinavir

    manufacturer of telithromycin advises avoid concomitant use with indinavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment

    Ivabradine

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of ivabradine—avoid concomitant use

    Ketoconazole

    manufacturer of telithromycin advises avoid concomitant use with ketoconazole in severe renal and hepatic impairment

    Lapatinib

    avoidance of telithromycin advised by manufacturer of lapatinib

    Lopinavir

    manufacturer of telithromycin advises avoid concomitant use with lopinavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment

    Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

    Maraviroc

    telithromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of maraviroc (consider reducing dose of maraviroc)

    Methadone

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with methadone

    Midazolam

    telithromycin inhibits metabolism of midazolam (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)

    Moxifloxacin

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with moxifloxacin

    Nilotinib

    avoidance of telithromycin advised by manufacturer of nilotinib

    Oxycodone

    telithromycin inhibits the metabolism of oxycodone

    Pazopanib

    avoidance of telithromycin advised by manufacturer of pazopanib

    Pentamidine Isetionate

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with parenteral pentamidine isetionate

    Phenobarbital

    plasma concentration of telithromycin reduced by phenobarbital (avoid during and for 2 weeks after phenobarbital)

    Phenytoin

    plasma concentration of telithromycin reduced by phenytoin (avoid during and for 2 weeks after phenytoin)

    Pimozide

    increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when telithromycin given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use

    possible increased risk of myopathy when telithromycin given with pravastatin

    Tacrolimus

    Telaprevir

    plasma concentration of both drugs possibly increased when telithromycin given with telaprevir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

    Tipranavir

    manufacturer of telithromycin advises avoid concomitant use with tipranavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment

    Chloramphenicol belongs to Antibacterials and has the following interaction information:
