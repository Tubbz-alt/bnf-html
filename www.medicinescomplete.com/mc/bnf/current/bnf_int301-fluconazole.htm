Fluconazole

Fluconazole has the following interaction information:

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Alfentanil

fluconazole inhibits metabolism of alfentanil (risk of prolonged or delayed respiratory depression)

Amitriptyline

fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of amitriptyline

Bosentan

fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of bosentan —avoid concomitant use

Carbamazepine

fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine

Celecoxib

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of celecoxib (halve dose of celecoxib)

Ciclosporin

fluconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)

Clopidogrel

fluconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

Coumarins

fluconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Cyclophosphamide

fluconazole possibly increases side-effects of cyclophosphamide

Diazepam

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of diazepam (risk of prolonged sedation)

Eplerenone

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)

Erythromycin

manufacturer of fluconazole advises avoid concomitant use with erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Flurbiprofen

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of flurbiprofen

Fluvastatin

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of fluvastatin —possible increased risk of myopathy

Hydrochlorothiazide

plasma concentration of fluconazole increased by hydrochlorothiazide

Ibuprofen

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ibuprofen

Ivabradine

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ivabradine —reduce initial dose of ivabradine

Methadone

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of methadone

Midazolam

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)

Nateglinide

fluconazole possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide

Nevirapine

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of nevirapine

Nortriptyline

fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of nortriptyline

Parecoxib

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of parecoxib (reduce dose of parecoxib)

Phenytoin

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of phenytoin (consider reducing dose of phenytoin)

Rifabutin

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)

Rifampicin

metabolism of fluconazole accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)

Ritonavir

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ritonavir

Simvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when fluconazole given with simvastatin —avoid concomitant use

Sirolimus

fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of sirolimus

Sulfonylureas

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of sulfonylureas

Tacrolimus

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Terbinafine

plasma concentration of fluconazole increased by terbinafine

Theophylline

fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of theophylline

Tipranavir

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of tipranavir

Tretinoin

fluconazole possibly increases risk of tretinoin toxicity

Zidovudine

fluconazole increases plasma concentration of zidovudine (increased risk of toxicity)

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Fluconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and will have the following interaction information:

Amphotericin

triazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atorvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when triazoles given with atorvastatin

Ergotamine and Methysergide

increased risk of ergotism when triazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide —avoid concomitant use

Fentanyl

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of fentanyl

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when triazoles given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Quetiapine

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine —manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use

Reboxetine

avoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine

Rifabutin

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)

Saquinavir

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Fluconazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.
