Tipranavir

Tipranavir has the following interaction information:

Abacavir

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of abacavir

Antacids

absorption of tipranavir reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Apixaban

avoidance of tipranavir advised by manufacturer of apixaban

Artemether with Lumefantrine

caution with tipranavir advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of tipranavir increased by atazanavir (also plasma concentration of atazanavir reduced)

Atorvastatin

tipranavir increases plasma concentration of atorvastatin (increased risk of myopathy—reduce dose of atorvastatin)

Bosentan

manufacturer of tipranavir advises avoid concomitant use with bosentan

Buprenorphine

plasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by buprenorphine

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by carbamazepine

Clarithromycin

tipranavir increases plasma concentration of clarithromycin (reduce dose of clarithromycin in renal impairment), also plasma concentration of tipranavir increased by clarithromycin

Darifenacin

avoidance of tipranavir advised by manufacturer of darifenacin

Didanosine

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of didanosine

Note: Antacids in tablet formulation may affect absorption of other drugs

Esomeprazole

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of esomeprazole

Etravirine

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of etravirine , also plasma concentration of tipranavir increased (avoid concomitant use)

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of tipranavir increased by fluconazole

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fosamprenavir

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of fosamprenavir

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Lopinavir

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of lopinavir

Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Metoprolol

manufacturer of tipranavir advises avoid concomitant use with metoprolol for heart failure

Omeprazole

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of omeprazole

Quetiapine

tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quetiapine —manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use

Quinine

tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of quinine (increased risk of toxicity)

Ranolazine

tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine —manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use

Rifabutin

tipranavir increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (reduce dose of rifabutin)

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by rifampicin —avoid concomitant use

Rivaroxaban

avoidance of tipranavir advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban

Rosuvastatin

tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of rosuvastatin —manufacturer of rosuvastatin advises avoid concomitant use

Salmeterol

manufacturer of tipranavir advises avoid concomitant use with salmeterol

Saquinavir

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of saquinavir

Simvastatin

tipranavir possibly increases plasma concentration of simvastatin —avoid concomitant use

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of tipranavir possibly reduced by St John's wort —avoid concomitant use

Telithromycin

avoidance of concomitant tipranavir in severe renal and hepatic impairment advised by manufacturer of telithromycin

Vardenafil

manufacturer of tipranavir advises caution with vardenafil

Vitamin E

increased risk of bleeding when tipranavir given with high doses of vitamin E

Zidovudine

tipranavir reduces plasma concentration of zidovudine

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Tipranavir belongs to Antivirals but Antivirals has no interactions information.
