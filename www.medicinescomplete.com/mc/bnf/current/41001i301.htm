Fluconazole

Fluconazole has the following interaction information:

In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Alfentanil fluconazole inhibits metabolism of alfentanil (risk of prolonged or delayed respiratory depression)
Amitriptyline fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of amitriptyline
Bosentan fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of bosentan—avoid concomitant use
Carbamazepine fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Celecoxib fluconazole increases plasma concentration of celecoxib (halve dose of celecoxib)
Ciclosporin fluconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Clopidogrel fluconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Coumarins fluconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam fluconazole increases plasma concentration of diazepam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Eplerenone fluconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)
Flurbiprofen fluconazole increases plasma concentration of flurbiprofen
Fluvastatin fluconazole increases plasma concentration of fluvastatin
Hydrochlorothiazideplasma concentration of fluconazole increased by hydrochlorothiazide
Ibuprofen fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ibuprofen
Ivabradine fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ivabradine—reduce initial dose of ivabradine
Midazolam fluconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Nateglinide fluconazole possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide
Nevirapine fluconazole increases plasma concentration of nevirapine
Parecoxib fluconazole increases plasma concentration of parecoxib (reduce dose of parecoxib)
Phenytoin fluconazole increases plasma concentration of phenytoin (consider reducing dose of phenytoin)
Rifabutin fluconazole increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)
Rifampicinmetabolism of fluconazole accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)
Ritonavir fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ritonavir
Simvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when fluconazole given with simvastatin—avoid concomitant use
Sulfonylureas fluconazole increases plasma concentration of sulfonylureas
Tacrolimus fluconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Theophylline fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of theophylline
Tipranavir fluconazole increases plasma concentration of tipranavir
Zidovudine fluconazole increases plasma concentration of zidovudine (increased risk of toxicity)Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Fluconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and will have the following interactions:

Amphotericin triazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when triazoles given with atorvastatin
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when triazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Fentanyl triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of fentanyl
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when triazoles given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quetiapine triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Reboxetineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine
Rifabutin triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)
Saquinavir triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Fluconazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.
