Phenindione

Phenindione has the following interaction information:

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Alcohol

anticoagulant control with phenindione may be affected by major changes in consumption of alcohol

Amiodarone

metabolism of phenindione inhibited by amiodarone (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Anabolic Steroids

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by anabolic steroids

Aspirin

increased risk of bleeding when phenindione given with aspirin (due to antiplatelet effect)

Clopidogrel

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced due to antiplatelet action of clopidogrel

Colestyramine

anticoagulant effect of phenindione may be enhanced or reduced by colestyramine

Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption

Corticosteroids

anticoagulant effect of phenindione may be enhanced or reduced by corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Dipyridamole

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced due to antiplatelet action of dipyridamole

Dronedarone

anticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by dronedarone

Enteral Foods

anticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by vitamin K (present in some enteral feeds )

Fibrates

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by fibrates

Iloprost

increased risk of bleeding when phenindione given with iloprost

Levofloxacin

anticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by levofloxacin

NSAIDs

anticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by NSAIDs

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Neomycin

experience in anticoagulant clinics suggests that INR possibly altered when phenindione is given with neomycin (given for local action on gut)

Oestrogens

anticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Penicillins

an interaction between phenindione and broad-spectrum penicillins has not been demonstrated in studies, but common experience in anticoagulant clinics is that INR can be altered

Prasugrel

possible increased risk of bleeding when phenindione given with prasugrel

Progestogens

anticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by progestogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

Ritonavir

anticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by ritonavir

Rosuvastatin

anticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by rosuvastatin

Sulfonamides

metabolism of phenindione possibly inhibited by sulfonamides

Testolactone

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by testolactone

Testosterone

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by testosterone

Tetracyclines

anticoagulant effect of phenindione possibly enhanced by tetracyclines

Thyroid Hormones

anticoagulant effect of phenindione enhanced by thyroid hormones

Vitamin K (Phytomenadione)

anticoagulant effect of phenindione antagonised by vitamin K

Phenindione belongs to Anticoagulants and will have the following interaction information:

Diclofenac

increased risk of haemorrhage when anticoagulants given with intravenous diclofenac (avoid concomitant use, including low-dose heparins)

Ketorolac

increased risk of haemorrhage when anticoagulants given with ketorolac (avoid concomitant use, including low-dose heparins)
