Methadone

Methadone has the following interaction information:

Abacavirplasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by abacavir
Amisulprideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with amisulpride—avoid concomitant use
Antipsychoticsincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT intervalIncreased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with atomoxetine
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of methadone reduced by carbamazepine
Didanosine methadone possibly reduces plasma concentration of didanosine Antacids in tablet formulation may affect absorption of other drugs
Efavirenzplasma concentration of methadone reduced by efavirenz
Fluoxetineplasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by fluoxetine
Fluvoxamineplasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by fluvoxamine
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of methadone reduced by fosamprenavir Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Nelfinavirplasma concentration of methadone reduced by nelfinavir
Nevirapineplasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by nevirapine
Paroxetineplasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by paroxetine
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of methadone reduced by phenobarbital
Phenytoinmetabolism of methadone accelerated by phenytoin (reduced effect and risk of withdrawal effects)
Rifampicinmetabolism of methadone accelerated by rifampicin (reduced effect)
Ritonavirplasma concentration of methadone reduced by ritonavir
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when methadone given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Sertralineplasma concentration of methadone possibly increased by sertraline
St John's Wortplasma concentration of methadone possibly reduced by St John's wort
Telaprevircaution with methadone advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Voriconazoleplasma concentration of methadone increased by voriconazole (consider reducing dose of methadone)
Zidovudine methadone possibly increases plasma concentration of zidovudine Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Methadone belongs to Opioid Analgesics and will have the following interactions:

Alcoholenhanced hypotensive and sedative effects when opioid analgesics given with alcohol
Anaesthetics, General (intravenous) opioid analgesics possibly enhance effects of intravenous general anaesthetics
Anaesthetics, General (volatile liquids) opioid analgesics possibly enhance effects of volatile liquid general anaesthetics
Antidepressants, Tricyclicsedative effects possibly increased when opioid analgesics given with tricyclics
Antihistamines, Sedatingsedative effects possibly increased when opioid analgesics given with sedating antihistamines
Antipsychoticsenhanced hypotensive and sedative effects when opioid analgesics given with antipsychotics Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsincreased sedative effect when opioid analgesics given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Cimetidinemetabolism of opioid analgesics inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)
Domperidone opioid analgesics antagonise effects of domperidone on gastro-intestinal activity
MAOIspossible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when opioid analgesics given with MAOIs—some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIsFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Metoclopramide opioid analgesics antagonise effects of metoclopramide on gastro-intestinal activity
Moclobemidepossible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when opioid analgesics given with moclobemide—manufacturer of moclobemide advises consider reducing dose of opioid analgesics
Selegilineavoidance of opioid analgesics advised by manufacturer of selegiline Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sodium Oxybate opioid analgesics enhance effects of sodium oxybate (avoid concomitant use)

Methadone belongs to Analgesics but Analgesics has no interactions information.
