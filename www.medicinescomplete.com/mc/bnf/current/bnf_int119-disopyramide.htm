Disopyramide

Disopyramide has the following interaction information:

Acetazolamide

increased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with acetazolamide

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Amisulpride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with amisulpride —avoid concomitant use

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with tricyclics

Antimuscarinics

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when disopyramide given with antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Arsenic Trioxide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with arsenic trioxide

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Atomoxetine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with atomoxetine

Azithromycin

plasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by azithromycin (increased risk of toxicity)

Clarithromycin

plasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by clarithromycin (increased risk of toxicity)

Diuretics, Loop

increased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with loop diuretics

Diuretics, Thiazide and related

increased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics

Dronedarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with dronedarone —avoid concomitant use

Droperidol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with droperidol —avoid concomitant use

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of disopyramide increased by erythromycin (increased risk of toxicity)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Fingolimod

possible increased risk of bradycardia when disopyramide given with fingolimod

Gliclazide

disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of gliclazide

Haloperidol

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with haloperidol —avoid concomitant use

Insulin

disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of insulin

Itraconazole

avoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of itraconazole

Ivabradine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with ivabradine

Ketoconazole

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with ketoconazole —avoid concomitant use

Metformin

disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of metformin

Mizolastine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with mizolastine —avoid concomitant use

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Nitrates

disopyramide reduces effects of sublingual tablets of nitrates (failure to dissolve under tongue owing to dry mouth)

Pentamidine Isetionate

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with pentamidine isetionate

Phenobarbital

metabolism of disopyramide accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced plasma concentration)

Phenothiazines

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with phenothiazines

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of disopyramide reduced by phenytoin

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Ranolazine

avoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of ranolazine

Rifamycins

metabolism of disopyramide accelerated by rifamycins (reduced plasma concentration)

Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by ritonavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Sildenafil

manufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with sildenafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Sotalol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with sotalol —avoid concomitant use

Sulpiride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with sulpiride

Tadalafil

manufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with tadalafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Telaprevir

avoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Tolterodine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with tolterodine

Vandetanib

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with vandetanib —avoid concomitant use

Vardenafil

manufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with vardenafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Verapamil

increased risk of myocardial depression and asystole when disopyramide given with verapamil

Warfarin

disopyramide may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of warfarin

Zuclopenthixol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with zuclopenthixol —avoid concomitant use

Disopyramide belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and will have the following interaction information:

Anti-arrhythmics

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with other anti-arrhythmics

Antipsychotics

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Beta-blockers

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Bupivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with bupivacaine

Levobupivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with levobupivacaine

Prilocaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with prilocaine

Ropivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with ropivacaine
