Antifungals

Antifungals has no specific interaction information.

Micafungin belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Amphotericin micafungin possibly increases plasma concentration of amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Ciclosporin micafungin possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin
Itraconazole micafungin increases plasma concentration of itraconazole (consider reducing dose of itraconazole)
Nifedipine micafungin increases plasma concentration of nifedipine
Sirolimus micafungin increases plasma concentration of sirolimus

Caspofungin belongs to Antifungals and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepineplasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by carbamazepine—consider increasing dose of caspofungin
Ciclosporinplasma concentration of caspofungin increased by ciclosporin (manufacturer of caspofungin recommends monitoring liver enzymes)
Dexamethasoneplasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by dexamethasone—consider increasing dose of caspofungin
Efavirenzplasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by efavirenz—consider increasing dose of caspofungin
Nevirapineplasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by nevirapine—consider increasing dose of caspofungin
Phenytoinplasma concentration of caspofungin possibly reduced by phenytoin—consider increasing dose of caspofungin
Rifampicinplasma concentration of caspofungin initially increased and then reduced by rifampicin (consider increasing dose of caspofungin)
Tacrolimus caspofungin reduces plasma concentration of tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5
