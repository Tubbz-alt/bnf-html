Hydroxycarbamide

Hydroxycarbamide is an orally active drug used mainly in the treatment of chronic myeloid leukaemia. It is also licensed for the treatment of cancer of the cervix in conjunction with radiotherapy. It is occasionally used for polycythaemia (the usual treatment is venesection); patients receiving long-term therapy with hydroxycarbamide should be advised to protect skin from sun exposure and should be monitored for secondary malignancies. Myelosuppression, nausea, and skin reactions are the most common toxic effects.
