Antifungals, Triazole

Antifungals, Triazole has the following interaction information:

Amphotericin triazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when triazoles given with atorvastatin
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when triazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Fentanyl triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of fentanyl
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when triazoles given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quetiapine triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Reboxetineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine
Rifabutin triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)
Saquinavir triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Antifungals, Triazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.

Posaconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and has the following interaction information:

Apixabanavoidance of posaconazole advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Atazanavir posaconazole increases plasma concentration of atazanavir
Atorvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when posaconazole given with atorvastatin (avoid concomitant use)
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced by carbamazepine
Ciclosporin posaconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Cimetidineplasma concentration of posaconazole reduced by cimetidine
Dronedaroneavoidance of posaconazole advised by manufacturer of dronedarone
Efavirenzplasma concentration of posaconazole reduced by efavirenz
Everolimus posaconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Glipizide posaconazole possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of glipizide
Histamine H2-antagonistsmanufacturer of posaconazole advises avoid concomitant use with histamine H2-antagonists (plasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced)
Lapatinibavoidance of posaconazole advised by manufacturer of lapatinib
Midazolam posaconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced by phenobarbital
Phenytoinplasma concentration of posaconazole reduced by phenytoin
Proton Pump Inhibitorsmanufacturer of posaconazole advises avoid concomitant use with proton pump inhibitors (plasma concentration of posaconazole possibly reduced)
Ranolazine posaconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use
Rifabutin posaconazole increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (also plasma concentration of posaconazole reduced)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of posaconazole reduced by rifampicin
Rivaroxabanavoidance of posaconazole advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban
Simvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when posaconazole given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)
Sirolimus posaconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of sirolimus
Tacrolimus posaconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Telaprevirplasma concentration of posaconazole possibly increased by telaprevir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Vinblastine posaconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of vinblastine (increased risk of neurotoxicity)
Vincristine posaconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of vincristine (increased risk of neurotoxicity)

Voriconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and has the following interaction information:

Alfentanil voriconazole increases plasma concentration of alfentanil (consider reducing dose of alfentanil)
Apixabanavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Cabazitaxelavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly reduced by carbamazepine—avoid concomitant use
Ciclosporin voriconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Clopidogrel voriconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Coumarins voriconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam voriconazole increases plasma concentration of diazepam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Diclofenac voriconazole increases plasma concentration of diclofenac
Dronedaroneavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of dronedarone
Efavirenzplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by efavirenz , also plasma concentration of efavirenz increased (increase voriconazole dose and reduce efavirenz dose)
Esomeprazole voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of esomeprazole
Everolimus voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Ibuprofen voriconazole increases plasma concentration of ibuprofen
Lapatinibavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of lapatinib
Methadone voriconazole increases plasma concentration of methadone (consider reducing dose of methadone)
Midazolam voriconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Nilotinibavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of nilotinib
Oestrogensplasma concentration of voriconazole increased by oestrogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Omeprazole voriconazole increases plasma concentration of omeprazole (consider reducing dose of omeprazole)
Oxycodone voriconazole increases plasma concentration of oxycodone
Pazopanibavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly reduced by phenobarbital—avoid concomitant use
Phenytoin voriconazole increases plasma concentration of phenytoin , also phenytoin reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole (increase dose of voriconazole and also monitor for phenytoin toxicity)
Progestogensplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly increased by progestogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5
Ranolazine voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use
Rifabutin voriconazole increases plasma concentration of rifabutin , also rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole (increase dose of voriconazole and also monitor for rifabutin toxicity)
Rifampicinplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by rifampicin—avoid concomitant use
Ritonavirplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by ritonavir—avoid concomitant use
Rivaroxabanavoidance of voriconazole advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban
Simvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when voriconazole given with simvastatin
Sirolimus voriconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus—avoid concomitant use
St John's Wortplasma concentration of voriconazole reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Sulfonylureas voriconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of sulfonylureas
Tacrolimus voriconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Telaprevirplasma concentration of voriconazole possibly affected by telaprevir (possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Fluconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and has the following interaction information:

In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Alfentanil fluconazole inhibits metabolism of alfentanil (risk of prolonged or delayed respiratory depression)
Amitriptyline fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of amitriptyline
Bosentan fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of bosentan—avoid concomitant use
Carbamazepine fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Celecoxib fluconazole increases plasma concentration of celecoxib (halve dose of celecoxib)
Ciclosporin fluconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Clopidogrel fluconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Coumarins fluconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Diazepam fluconazole increases plasma concentration of diazepam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Eplerenone fluconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)
Flurbiprofen fluconazole increases plasma concentration of flurbiprofen
Fluvastatin fluconazole increases plasma concentration of fluvastatin
Hydrochlorothiazideplasma concentration of fluconazole increased by hydrochlorothiazide
Ibuprofen fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ibuprofen
Ivabradine fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ivabradine—reduce initial dose of ivabradine
Midazolam fluconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Nateglinide fluconazole possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide
Nevirapine fluconazole increases plasma concentration of nevirapine
Parecoxib fluconazole increases plasma concentration of parecoxib (reduce dose of parecoxib)
Phenytoin fluconazole increases plasma concentration of phenytoin (consider reducing dose of phenytoin)
Rifabutin fluconazole increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)
Rifampicinmetabolism of fluconazole accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)
Ritonavir fluconazole increases plasma concentration of ritonavir
Simvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when fluconazole given with simvastatin—avoid concomitant use
Sulfonylureas fluconazole increases plasma concentration of sulfonylureas
Tacrolimus fluconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Theophylline fluconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of theophylline
Tipranavir fluconazole increases plasma concentration of tipranavir
Zidovudine fluconazole increases plasma concentration of zidovudine (increased risk of toxicity)Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Itraconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and has the following interaction information:

Alfentanil itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of alfentanil
Aliskiren itraconazole increases plasma concentration of aliskiren—avoid concomitant use
Alprazolam itraconazole increases plasma concentration of alprazolam
Antacidsabsorption of itraconazole reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Apixabanavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Aripiprazole itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)
Atorvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when itraconazole given with atorvastatin
Bosentan itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of bosentan
Budesonide itraconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled budesonide
Buspirone itraconazole increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)
Busulfan itraconazole inhibits metabolism of busulfan (increased risk of toxicity)
Cabazitaxelavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel
Calcium-channel Blockersnegative inotropic effect possibly increased when itraconazole given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Calcium-channel Blockers (dihydropyridines) itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of dihydropyridines (increased plasma concentration)
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by carbamazepine
Ciclosporin itraconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of itraconazole increased by clarithromycin
Clopidogrel itraconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Colchicine itraconazole possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)
Corticosteroids itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Coumarins itraconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Cyclophosphamide itraconazole possibly increases side-effects of cyclophosphamide
Dabigatran Etexilate itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate—manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use
Darifenacinavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of darifenacin
Digoxin itraconazole increases plasma concentration of digoxin
Disopyramidemanufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use with disopyramide
Dronedaroneavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of dronedarone
Efavirenzplasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by efavirenz
Eletriptan itraconazole increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Eplerenone itraconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone—avoid concomitant use
Everolimus itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Felodipine itraconazole inhibits metabolism of felodipine (increased plasma concentration)
Fesoterodinemanufacturer of fesoterodine advises dose reduction when itraconazole given with fesoterodine—consult fesoterodine product literature
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of both drugs may increase when itraconazole given with fosamprenavir Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Gefitinib itraconazole increases plasma concentration of gefitinib
Haloperidol itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of haloperidol
Histamine H2-antagonistsabsorption of itraconazole reduced by histamine H2-antagonists
Indinavir itraconazole increases plasma concentration of indinavir (consider reducing dose of indinavir)
Ivabradine itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ivabradine—avoid concomitant use
Lapatinibavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of lapatinib
Lercanidipineavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine
Methylprednisolone itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of methylprednisolone
Micafunginplasma concentration of itraconazole increased by micafungin (consider reducing dose of itraconazole)
Midazolam itraconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Mizolastine itraconazole inhibits metabolism of mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Nevirapineplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by nevirapine—consider increasing dose of itraconazole
Nilotinibavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of nilotinib
Pazopanibavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by phenobarbital
Phenytoinplasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by phenytoin—avoid concomitant use
Proton Pump Inhibitorsabsorption of itraconazole reduced by proton pump inhibitors
