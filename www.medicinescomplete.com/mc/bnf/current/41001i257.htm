MAOIs

MAOIs has the following interaction information:

For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
ACE Inhibitors MAOIs possibly enhance hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors
Adrenergic Neurone Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with adrenergic neurone blockers
Alcohol MAOIs interact with tyramine found in some beverages containing alcohol and some dealcoholised beverages (hypertensive crisis)—if no tyramine, enhanced hypotensive effect
Alpha-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with alpha-blockers
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists MAOIs possibly enhance hypotensive effect of angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Antidepressants, SSRI MAOIs increase CNS effects of SSRIs (risk of serious toxicity)
Antidepressants, Tricyclicincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when MAOIs given with tricyclics , tricyclics should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs (3 weeks if starting clomipramine or imipramine), also MAOIs should not be started for at least 1–2 weeks after stopping tricyclics (3 weeks in the case of clomipramine or imipramine)
Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)after stopping MAOIs do not start tricyclic-related antidepressants for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1–2 weeks after stopping tricyclic-related antidepressants
Antidiabetics MAOIs possibly enhance hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics
Antiepileptics MAOIs possibly antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)
Antihistaminesincreased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when MAOIs given with antihistamines Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)
Antimuscarinicsincreased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when MAOIs given with antimuscarinics Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
Apraclonidineavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of apraclonidine
Atomoxetineafter stopping MAOIs do not start atomoxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 2 weeks after stopping atomoxetine
Beta-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Brimonidineavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of brimonidine
Bupropionavoidance of bupropion for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of bupropion
Buspironeavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of buspirone
Calcium-channel Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Carbamazepineavoidance for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of carbamazepine , also antagonism of anticonvulsant effect
Citalopramafter stopping MAOIs do not start citalopram for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping citalopram
Clonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with clonidine
ClozapineCNS effects of MAOIs possibly increased by clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Diazoxideenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with diazoxide
Diureticsenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with diuretics
Doxapram MAOIs enhance effects of doxapram
Duloxetineafter stopping MAOIs do not start duloxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 5 days after stopping duloxetine
Entacaponeavoid concomitant use of non-selective MAOIs with entacapone
Escitalopramafter stopping MAOIs do not start escitalopram for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping escitalopram
Fluoxetineafter stopping MAOIs do not start fluoxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetine
Fluvoxamineafter stopping MAOIs do not start fluvoxamine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping fluvoxamine
Histamineavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of histamine
Hydralazineenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with hydralazine
Indoraminavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of indoramin
Insulin MAOIs enhance hypoglycaemic effect of insulin
Levodoparisk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with levodopa , avoid levodopa for at least 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs
MAOIsincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when MAOIs given with other MAOIs (avoid for at least 2 weeks after stopping previous MAOIs and then start at a reduced dose)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Metformin MAOIs enhance hypoglycaemic effect of metformin
Methyldopaavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of methyldopa
Methylphenidaterisk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with methylphenidate , some manufacturers advise avoid methylphenidate for at least 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs
Minoxidilenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with minoxidil
Mirtazapineafter stopping MAOIs do not start mirtazapine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 2 weeks after stopping mirtazapine
Moclobemideafter stopping MAOIs do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week
Moxonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with moxonidine
Nefopamavoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of nefopam
Nicorandilenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with nicorandil
Nitratesenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with nitrates
Opioid Analgesicspossible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when MAOIs given with opioid analgesics—some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs
Paroxetineafter stopping MAOIs do not start paroxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping paroxetine
PethidineCNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when MAOIs given with pethidine—avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs
Pholcodineavoidance of pholcodine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of pholcodine
Promethazineavoidance of promethazine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of promethazine
Rasagilinerisk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with rasagiline , avoid MAOIs for at least 2 weeks after stopping rasagilineRasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Reboxetineincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when MAOIs given with reboxetine (MAOIs should not be started until 1 week after stopping reboxetine, avoid reboxetine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs)
Rizatriptanrisk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with rizatriptan (avoid rizatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)
Selegilineenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with selegiline—manufacturer of selegiline advises avoid concomitant useSelegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sertralineafter stopping MAOIs do not start sertraline for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping sertraline
Sodium Nitroprussideenhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with sodium nitroprusside
Sulfonylureas MAOIs enhance hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas
Sumatriptanrisk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with sumatriptan (avoid sumatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)
Sympathomimeticsrisk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with sympathomimetics
Tetrabenazinerisk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with tetrabenazine (avoid tetrabenazine for 2 weeks after MAOIs)
Tolcaponeavoid concomitant use of MAOIs with tolcapone
Tramadolpossible increased serotonergic effects and increased risk of convulsions when MAOIs given with tramadol—some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs
TryptophanCNS excitation and confusion when MAOIs given with tryptophan (reduce dose of tryptophan)
Venlafaxineenhanced CNS effects and toxicity when MAOIs given with venlafaxine (venlafaxine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, avoid MAOIs for 1 week after stopping venlafaxine)
Zolmitriptanincreased risk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with zolmitriptan

MAOIs belongs to Antidepressants and will have the following interactions:

Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atomoxetinepossible increased risk of convulsions when antidepressants given with atomoxetine

Isocarboxazid belongs to MAOIs but has no specific interaction information.

Phenelzine belongs to MAOIs and has the following interaction information:

Suxamethonium phenelzine enhances effects of suxamethonium

Tranylcypromine belongs to MAOIs and has the following interaction information:

Buspironemanufacturer of tranylcypromine advises avoid buspirone for 10 days after stopping tranylcypromine
