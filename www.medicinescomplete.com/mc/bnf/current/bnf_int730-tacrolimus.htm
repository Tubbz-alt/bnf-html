Tacrolimus

Tacrolimus has the following interaction information:

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Aciclovir

possible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with aciclovir

Note: Interactions do not apply to topical aciclovir preparations

Aminoglycosides

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with aminoglycosides

Amphotericin

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with amphotericin

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists

increased risk of hyperkalaemia when tacrolimus given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists

Antifungals, Imidazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by imidazoles

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by atazanavir

Caspofungin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by caspofungin

Chloramphenicol

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by chloramphenicol

Ciclosporin

tacrolimus increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin (increased risk of nephrotoxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Clarithromycin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by clarithromycin

Dabigatran Etexilate

tacrolimus possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate —manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use

Danazol

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by danazol

Diltiazem

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by diltiazem

Diuretics, Potassium-sparing and Aldosterone Antagonists

increased risk of hyperkalaemia when tacrolimus given with potassium-sparing diuretics and aldosterone antagonists

Dronedarone

caution with tacrolimus advised by manufacturer of dronedarone

Droperidol

avoidance of tacrolimus advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly affected by efavirenz

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Ethinylestradiol

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by ethinylestradiol

Felodipine

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by felodipine

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by fluconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by fosamprenavir

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Ganciclovir

possible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with ganciclovir

Note: Increased risk of myelosuppression with other myelosuppressive drugs—consult product literature

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by grapefruit juice

Ibuprofen

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with ibuprofen

Itraconazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by itraconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by ketoconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Mifamurtide

avoidance of tacrolimus advised by manufacturer of mifamurtide

NSAIDs

possible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with NSAIDs

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Nelfinavir

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by nelfinavir

Nicardipine

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by nicardipine

Nifedipine

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by nifedipine

Omeprazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by omeprazole

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by phenobarbital

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by phenytoin , also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased

Posaconazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by posaconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Potassium Salts

increased risk of hyperkalaemia when tacrolimus given with potassium salts

Note: Includes salt substitutes

Rifabutin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly reduced by rifabutin

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by rifampicin

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by ritonavir

Saquinavir

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by saquinavir (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Sevelamer

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly reduced by sevelamer

St John's Wort

plasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by St John's wort —avoid concomitant use

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of both drugs increased when tacrolimus given with telaprevir (reduce dose of tacrolimus)

Telithromycin

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by telithromycin

Vancomycin

possible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with vancomycin

Verapamil

plasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by verapamil

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by voriconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)
