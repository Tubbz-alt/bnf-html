    5HT1-receptor Agonists

    5HT1-receptor Agonists has the following interaction information:

    Citalopram

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when 5HT1 agonists given with citalopram (manufacturer of citalopram advises avoid concomitant use)

    Duloxetine

    possible increased serotonergic effects when 5HT1 agonists given with duloxetine

    Selegiline

    avoidance of 5HT1 agonists advised by manufacturer of selegiline

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    St John's Wort

    increased serotonergic effects when 5HT1 agonists given with St John's wort—avoid concomitant use

    Venlafaxine

    possible increased serotonergic effects when 5HT1 agonists given with venlafaxine

    Frovatriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when frovatriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 24 hours after frovatriptan, avoid frovatriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    Fluvoxamine

    metabolism of frovatriptan inhibited by fluvoxamine

    Eletriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Clarithromycin

    plasma concentration of eletriptan increased by clarithromycin (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when eletriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 24 hours after eletriptan, avoid eletriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    Erythromycin

    plasma concentration of eletriptan increased by erythromycin (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

    Indinavir

    plasma concentration of eletriptan increased by indinavir (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Itraconazole

    plasma concentration of eletriptan increased by itraconazole (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Ketoconazole

    plasma concentration of eletriptan increased by ketoconazole (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Ritonavir

    plasma concentration of eletriptan increased by ritonavir (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Almotriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when almotriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after almotriptan, avoid almotriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    Ketoconazole

    plasma concentration of almotriptan increased by ketoconazole (increased risk of toxicity)

    Naratriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Antidepressants, SSRI

    possible increased serotonergic effects when naratriptan given with SSRIs

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when naratriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 24 hours after naratriptan, avoid naratriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    Rizatriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when rizatriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after rizatriptan, avoid rizatriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    MAOIs

    risk of CNS toxicity when rizatriptan given with MAOIs (avoid rizatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Moclobemide

    risk of CNS toxicity when rizatriptan given with moclobemide (avoid rizatriptan for 2 weeks after moclobemide)

    Propranolol

    plasma concentration of rizatriptan increased by propranolol (manufacturer of rizatriptan advises halve dose and avoid within 2 hours of propranolol)

    Sumatriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Citalopram

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with citalopram

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when sumatriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after sumatriptan, avoid sumatriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    Escitalopram

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with escitalopram

    Fluoxetine

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with fluoxetine

    Fluvoxamine

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with fluvoxamine

    Lithium

    possible risk of toxicity when sumatriptan given with lithium

    MAOIs

    risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with MAOIs (avoid sumatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Moclobemide

    risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with moclobemide (avoid sumatriptan for 2 weeks after moclobemide)

    Paroxetine

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when sumatriptan given with paroxetine

    Sertraline

    CNS toxicity reported when sumatriptan given with sertraline

    Zolmitriptan belongs to 5HT1-receptor Agonists and has the following interaction information:

    Cimetidine

    metabolism of zolmitriptan inhibited by cimetidine (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

    Ergotamine and Methysergide

    increased risk of vasospasm when zolmitriptan given with ergotamine and methysergide (avoid ergotamine and methysergide for 6 hours after zolmitriptan, avoid zolmitriptan for 24 hours after ergotamine and methysergide)

    Fluvoxamine

    metabolism of zolmitriptan possibly inhibited by fluvoxamine (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

    MAOIs

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when zolmitriptan given with MAOIs

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Moclobemide

    risk of CNS toxicity when zolmitriptan given with moclobemide (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

    Quinolones

    metabolism of zolmitriptan possibly inhibited by quinolones (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)
