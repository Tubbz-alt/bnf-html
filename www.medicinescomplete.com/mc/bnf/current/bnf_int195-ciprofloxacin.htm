Ciprofloxacin

Ciprofloxacin has the following interaction information:

Agomelatine

avoidance of ciprofloxacin advised by manufacturer of agomelatine

Antacids

absorption of ciprofloxacin reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Calcium Salts

absorption of ciprofloxacin reduced by calcium salts

Note: see also Antacids

Clopidogrel

ciprofloxacin possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

Clozapine

ciprofloxacin increases plasma concentration of clozapine

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Coumarins

ciprofloxacin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Dairy Products

absorption of ciprofloxacin reduced by dairy products

Duloxetine

ciprofloxacin inhibits metabolism of duloxetine —avoid concomitant use

Erlotinib

ciprofloxacin increases plasma concentration of erlotinib

Iron

absorption of ciprofloxacin reduced by oral iron

Methotrexate

ciprofloxacin possibly reduces excretion of methotrexate (increased risk of toxicity)

Olanzapine

ciprofloxacin possibly increases plasma concentration of olanzapine

Phenytoin

ciprofloxacin increases or decreases plasma concentration of phenytoin

Probenecid

excretion of ciprofloxacin reduced by probenecid (increased plasma concentration)

Rasagiline

ciprofloxacin increases plasma concentration of rasagiline

Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Ropinirole

ciprofloxacin inhibits metabolism of ropinirole (increased plasma concentration)

Sevelamer

bioavailability of ciprofloxacin reduced by sevelamer

Sucralfate

absorption of ciprofloxacin reduced by sucralfate

Theophylline

ciprofloxacin increases plasma concentration of theophylline

Tizanidine

ciprofloxacin increases plasma concentration of tizanidine (increased risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Zinc

absorption of ciprofloxacin reduced by zinc

Ciprofloxacin belongs to Quinolones and will have the following interaction information:

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of quinolones advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Ciclosporin

increased risk of nephrotoxicity when quinolones given with ciclosporin

Lanthanum

absorption of quinolones possibly reduced by lanthanum (give at least 2 hours before or 4 hours after lanthanum)

NSAIDs

possible increased risk of convulsions when quinolones given with NSAIDs

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Strontium Ranelate

absorption of quinolones reduced by strontium ranelate (manufacturer of strontium ranelate advises avoid concomitant use)

Theophylline

possible increased risk of convulsions when quinolones given with theophylline

Zolmitriptan

quinolones possibly inhibit metabolism of zolmitriptan (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

Ciprofloxacin belongs to Antibacterials and will have the following interaction information:

Typhoid Vaccine (oral)

antibacterials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine —see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4
