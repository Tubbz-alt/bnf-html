  1. Although evidence of hazard is uncertain, manufacturer advises avoid.

  2. Small amounts in medicines probably safe.

  3. Status epilepticus has been treated successfully with intravenous diazepam.

  5. May be used with caution if safer alternative not available.

  6. Buprenorphine, codeine, diamorphine, dihydrocodeine, fentanyl, methadone, morphine, pethidine, and tramadol are thought to be safe.

  7. Rifamycins have been used in a few patients without evidence of harm—use with caution if safer alternative not available.

  8. Contact Welsh Medicines Information Centre for further advice.
