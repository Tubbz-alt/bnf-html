2.2.4 Potassium-sparing diuretics with other diuretics

Additional information

 

interactions (Diuretics).

Although it is preferable to prescribe thiazides (section 2.2.1) and potassium-sparing diuretics (section 2.2.3) separately, the use of fixed combinations may be justified if compliance is a problem. Potassium-sparing diuretics are not usually necessary in the routine treatment of hypertension, unless hypokalaemia develops. For interactions, see Appendix 1 (diuretics).
