Itraconazole

Itraconazole has the following interaction information:

Alfentanil

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of alfentanil

Aliskiren

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of aliskiren —avoid concomitant use

Alprazolam

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of alprazolam

Antacids

absorption of itraconazole reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Apixaban

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of apixaban

Aripiprazole

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)

Atorvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when itraconazole given with atorvastatin

Bosentan

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of bosentan

Budesonide

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled budesonide

Buspirone

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)

Busulfan

itraconazole inhibits metabolism of busulfan (increased risk of toxicity)

Cabazitaxel

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel

Calcium-channel Blockers

negative inotropic effect possibly increased when itraconazole given with calcium-channel blockers

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Calcium-channel Blockers (dihydropyridines)

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of dihydropyridines (increased plasma concentration)

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by carbamazepine

Ciclosporin

itraconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)

Clarithromycin

plasma concentration of itraconazole increased by clarithromycin

Clopidogrel

itraconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

Colchicine

itraconazole possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)

Corticosteroids

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Coumarins

itraconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Cyclophosphamide

itraconazole possibly increases side-effects of cyclophosphamide

Dabigatran Etexilate

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate —manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use

Darifenacin

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of darifenacin

Digoxin

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of digoxin

Disopyramide

manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use with disopyramide

Dronedarone

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of dronedarone

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by efavirenz

Eletriptan

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Eplerenone

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone —avoid concomitant use

Everolimus

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus —manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use

Felodipine

itraconazole inhibits metabolism of felodipine (increased plasma concentration)

Fesoterodine

manufacturer of fesoterodine advises dose reduction when itraconazole given with fesoterodine —consult fesoterodine product literature

Fluticasone

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled fluticasone

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of both drugs may increase when itraconazole given with fosamprenavir

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Gefitinib

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of gefitinib

Haloperidol

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of haloperidol

Histamine H2-antagonists

absorption of itraconazole reduced by histamine H2-antagonists

Indinavir

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of indinavir (consider reducing dose of indinavir)

Ivabradine

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ivabradine —avoid concomitant use

Lapatinib

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of lapatinib

Lercanidipine

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine

Methylprednisolone

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of methylprednisolone

Micafungin

plasma concentration of itraconazole increased by micafungin (consider reducing dose of itraconazole)

Midazolam

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)

Mizolastine

itraconazole inhibits metabolism of mizolastine —avoid concomitant use

Nevirapine

plasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by nevirapine —consider increasing dose of itraconazole

Nilotinib

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of nilotinib

Pazopanib

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by phenobarbital

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by phenytoin —avoid concomitant use

Proton Pump Inhibitors

absorption of itraconazole reduced by proton pump inhibitors

Ranolazine

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine —manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use

Repaglinide

itraconazole possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of repaglinide

Rifabutin

plasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by rifabutin —manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by rifampicin —manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use

Ritonavir

combination of itraconazole with ritonavir may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)

Rivaroxaban

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban

Sildenafil

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of sildenafil —reduce initial dose of sildenafil

Simvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when itraconazole given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)

Sirolimus

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus —avoid concomitant use

Solifenacin

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of solifenacin

Tacrolimus

itraconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Tadalafil

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of tadalafil

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of itraconazole possibly increased by telaprevir

Tolterodine

avoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of tolterodine

Vardenafil

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of vardenafil —avoid concomitant use

Vincristine

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of vincristine (increased risk of neurotoxicity)

Vinflunine

itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of vinflunine —manufacturer of vinflunine advises avoid concomitant use

Vinorelbine

itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of vinorelbine (increased risk of neurotoxicity)

Itraconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and will have the following interaction information:

Amphotericin

triazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atorvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when triazoles given with atorvastatin

Ergotamine and Methysergide

increased risk of ergotism when triazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide —avoid concomitant use

Fentanyl

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of fentanyl

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when triazoles given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Quetiapine

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine —manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use

Reboxetine

avoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine

Rifabutin

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)

Saquinavir

triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Itraconazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.
