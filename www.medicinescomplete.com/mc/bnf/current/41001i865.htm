Clopidogrel

Clopidogrel has the following interaction information:

Aspirinincreased risk of bleeding when clopidogrel given with aspirin
Carbamazepineantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by carbamazepine
Chloramphenicolantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by chloramphenicol
Cimetidineantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by cimetidine
Ciprofloxacinantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by ciprofloxacin
Coumarinsantiplatelet action of clopidogrel enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Dipyridamoleincreased risk of bleeding when clopidogrel given with dipyridamole
Erythromycinantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by erythromycin Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Esomeprazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel reduced by esomeprazole
Etravirineantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by etravirine
Fluconazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by fluconazole In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Fluoxetineantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by fluoxetine
Fluvoxamineantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by fluvoxamine
Heparinsincreased risk of bleeding when clopidogrel given with heparins
Iloprostincreased risk of bleeding when clopidogrel given with iloprost
Itraconazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by itraconazole
Ketoconazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by ketoconazole
Lansoprazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by lansoprazole
Moclobemideantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by moclobemide
NSAIDsincreased risk of bleeding when clopidogrel given with NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Omeprazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel reduced by omeprazole
Oxcarbazepineantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by oxcarbazepine
Pantoprazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by pantoprazole
Phenindioneantiplatelet action of clopidogrel enhances anticoagulant effect of phenindione Change in patient’s clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Prasugrelpossible increased risk of bleeding when clopidogrel given with prasugrel
Rabeprazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by rabeprazole
Voriconazoleantiplatelet effect of clopidogrel possibly reduced by voriconazole
Warfarinmanufacturer of clopidogrel advises avoid concomitant use with warfarin
