Antidepressants

Antidepressants has the following interaction information:

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atomoxetine

possible increased risk of convulsions when antidepressants given with atomoxetine

Agomelatine belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Ciprofloxacin

manufacturer of agomelatine advises avoid concomitant use with ciprofloxacin

Fluvoxamine

metabolism of agomelatine inhibited by fluvoxamine (increased plasma concentration)

Mirtazapine belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Alcohol

increased sedative effect when mirtazapine given with alcohol

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

increased sedative effect when mirtazapine given with anxiolytics and hypnotics

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of mirtazapine reduced by carbamazepine

Cimetidine

plasma concentration of mirtazapine increased by cimetidine

Clonidine

mirtazapine possibly antagonises hypotensive effect of clonidine

Fluoxetine

possible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with fluoxetine

Fluvoxamine

possible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with fluvoxamine

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of mirtazapine increased by ketoconazole

MAOIs

mirtazapine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 2 weeks after stopping mirtazapine

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Methylthioninium

possible risk of CNS toxicity when mirtazapine given with methylthioninium —avoid concomitant use (if avoidance not possible, use lowest possible dose of methylthioninium and observe patient for up to 4 hours after administration)

Moclobemide

after stopping mirtazapine do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of mirtazapine reduced by phenytoin

Tramadol

possible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with tramadol

Venlafaxine

possible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with venlafaxine

Warfarin

mirtazapine enhances anticoagulant effect of warfarin

Antidepressants, Noradrenaline Re-uptake Inhibitors belongs to Antidepressants but has no specific interaction information.

Reboxetine belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Antifungals, Imidazole

manufacturer of reboxetine advises avoid concomitant use with imidazoles

Antifungals, Triazole

manufacturer of reboxetine advises avoid concomitant use with triazoles

Carbamazepine

plasma concentration of reboxetine possibly reduced by carbamazepine

Diuretics, Loop

possible increased risk of hypokalaemia when reboxetine given with loop diuretics

Diuretics, Thiazide and related

possible increased risk of hypokalaemia when reboxetine given with thiazides and related diuretics

Ergotamine and Methysergide

possible risk of hypertension when reboxetine given with ergotamine and methysergide

Fluvoxamine

manufacturer of reboxetine advises avoid concomitant use with fluvoxamine

MAOIs

increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when reboxetine given with MAOIs (MAOIs should not be started until 1 week after stopping reboxetine, avoid reboxetine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs)

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Macrolides

manufacturer of reboxetine advises avoid concomitant use with macrolides

Note: See also Telithromycin

Phenobarbital

plasma concentration of reboxetine possibly reduced by phenobarbital

Antidepressants, SSRI belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Alcohol

sedative effects possibly increased when SSRIs given with alcohol

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

SSRIs increase plasma concentration of some tricyclics

Antiepileptics

SSRIs antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)

Aspirin

increased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with aspirin

Coumarins

SSRIs possibly enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Cyproheptadine

antidepressant effect of SSRIs possibly antagonised by cyproheptadine

Duloxetine

possible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with duloxetine

Lithium

Increased risk of CNS effects when SSRIs given with lithium (lithium toxicity reported)

MAOIs

CNS effects of SSRIs increased by MAOIs (risk of serious toxicity)

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Methylphenidate

metabolism of SSRIs possibly inhibited by methylphenidate

Methylthioninium

risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with methylthioninium —avoid concomitant use (if avoidance not possible, use lowest possible dose of methylthioninium and observe patient for up to 4 hours after administration)

Metoclopramide

CNS toxicity reported when SSRIs given with metoclopramide

NSAIDs

increased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with NSAIDs

Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

Naratriptan

possible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with naratriptan

Pimozide

SSRIs possibly increase plasma concentration of pimozide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Rasagiline

increased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with rasagiline

Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of SSRIs possibly increased by ritonavir

St John's Wort

increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with St John's wort —avoid concomitant use

Tramadol

increased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with tramadol

Tryptophan

agitation and nausea may occur when SSRIs given with tryptophan

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Sumatriptan

increased risk of CNS toxicity when escitalopram given with sumatriptan

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Moclobemide

Selegiline

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Ticagrelor

Antidepressants, Tricyclic belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Adrenaline (epinephrine)

increased risk of hypertension and arrhythmias when tricyclics given with adrenaline (epinephrine) (but local anaesthetics with adrenaline appear to be safe)

Adrenergic Neurone Blockers

tricyclics antagonise hypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers

Alcohol

increased sedative effect when tricyclics given with alcohol

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Anaesthetics, General

increased risk of arrhythmias and hypotension when tricyclics given with general anaesthetics

Note: See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1

Antidepressants, SSRI

plasma concentration of some tricyclics increased by SSRIs

Antiepileptics

tricyclics antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)

Antihistamines

increased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when tricyclics given with antihistamines

Note: Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)

Antimuscarinics

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when tricyclics given with antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Antipsychotics

plasma concentration of tricyclics increased by antipsychotics —possibly increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

increased sedative effect when tricyclics given with anxiolytics and hypnotics

Apraclonidine

avoidance of tricyclics advised by manufacturer of apraclonidine

Atomoxetine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with atomoxetine

Baclofen

tricyclics enhance muscle relaxant effect of baclofen

Brimonidine

avoidance of tricyclics advised by manufacturer of brimonidine

Bupropion

plasma concentration of tricyclics possibly increased by bupropion (possible increased risk of convulsions)

Cannabis Extract

possible increased risk of hypertension and tachycardia when tricyclics given with cannabis extract

Carbamazepine

metabolism of tricyclics accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced plasma concentration and reduced effect)

Cimetidine

plasma concentration of tricyclics possibly increased by cimetidine

Clonidine

tricyclics antagonise hypotensive effect of clonidine , also increased risk of hypertension on clonidine withdrawal

Clozapine

possible increased antimuscarinic side-effects when tricyclics given with clozapine

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Coumarins

tricyclics may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Diltiazem

plasma concentration of tricyclics possibly increased by diltiazem

Disopyramide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with disopyramide

Dronedarone

avoidance of tricyclics advised by manufacturer of dronedarone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Droperidol

avoidance of tricyclics advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Entacapone

caution with tricyclics advised by manufacturer of entacapone

Flecainide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with flecainide

Histamine

tricyclics theoretically antagonise effects of histamine —manufacturer of histamine advises avoid concomitant use

Lithium

risk of toxicity when tricyclics given with lithium

MAOIs

increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when tricyclics given with MAOIs , tricyclics should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs (3 weeks if starting clomipramine or imipramine), also MAOIs should not be started for at least 1–2 weeks after stopping tricyclics (3 weeks in the case of clomipramine or imipramine)

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Methylphenidate

metabolism of tricyclics possibly inhibited by methylphenidate

Moclobemide

Moxifloxacin

Moxonidine

tricyclics possibly antagonise hypotensive effect of moxonidine (manufacturer of moxonidine advises avoid concomitant use)

Nefopam

side-effects possibly increased when tricyclics given with nefopam

Nicorandil

tricyclics possibly enhance hypotensive effect of nicorandil

Nitrates

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Opioid Analgesics

sedative effects possibly increased when tricyclics given with opioid analgesics

Pentamidine Isetionate

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with pentamidine isetionate

Phenothiazines

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when tricyclics given with phenothiazines

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of tricyclics possibly reduced by phenytoin

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Propafenone

increased risk of arrhythmias when tricyclics given with propafenone

Rasagiline

increased risk of CNS toxicity when tricyclics given with rasagiline

Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of tricyclics possibly increased by ritonavir

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Selegiline

CNS toxicity reported when tricyclics given with selegiline

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Sodium Oxybate

increased risk of side-effects when tricyclics given with sodium oxybate
