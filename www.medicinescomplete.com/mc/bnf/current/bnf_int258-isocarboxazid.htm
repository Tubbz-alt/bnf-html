Isocarboxazid

Isocarboxazid has no specific interaction information.

Isocarboxazid belongs to MAOIs and will have the following interaction information:

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

ACE Inhibitors

MAOIs possibly enhance hypotensive effect of ACE inhibitors

Adrenergic Neurone Blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with adrenergic neurone blockers

Alcohol

MAOIs interact with tyramine found in some beverages containing alcohol and some dealcoholised beverages (hypertensive crisis)—if no tyramine, enhanced hypotensive effect

Alpha-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with alpha-blockers

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists

MAOIs possibly enhance hypotensive effect of angiotensin-II receptor antagonists

Antidepressants, SSRI

MAOIs increase CNS effects of SSRIs (risk of serious toxicity)

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when MAOIs given with tricyclics , tricyclics should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs (3 weeks if starting clomipramine or imipramine), also MAOIs should not be started for at least 1–2 weeks after stopping tricyclics (3 weeks in the case of clomipramine or imipramine)

Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)

after stopping MAOIs do not start tricyclic-related antidepressants for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1–2 weeks after stopping tricyclic-related antidepressants

Antidiabetics

MAOIs possibly enhance hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics

Antiepileptics

MAOIs possibly antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)

Antihistamines

increased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when MAOIs given with antihistamines

Note: Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)

Antimuscarinics

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when MAOIs given with antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Apraclonidine

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of apraclonidine

Atomoxetine

after stopping MAOIs do not start atomoxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 2 weeks after stopping atomoxetine

Beta-blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Brimonidine

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of brimonidine

Bupropion

avoidance of bupropion for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of bupropion

Buspirone

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of buspirone

Calcium-channel Blockers

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with calcium-channel blockers

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Carbamazepine

avoidance for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of carbamazepine , also antagonism of anticonvulsant effect

Citalopram

after stopping MAOIs do not start citalopram for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping citalopram

Clonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with clonidine

Clozapine

CNS effects of MAOIs possibly increased by clozapine

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Diazoxide

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with diazoxide

Diuretics

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with diuretics

Doxapram

MAOIs enhance effects of doxapram

Duloxetine

after stopping MAOIs do not start duloxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 5 days after stopping duloxetine

Entacapone

avoid concomitant use of non-selective MAOIs with entacapone

Escitalopram

after stopping MAOIs do not start escitalopram for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping escitalopram

Fluoxetine

after stopping MAOIs do not start fluoxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetine

Fluvoxamine

after stopping MAOIs do not start fluvoxamine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping fluvoxamine

Histamine

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of histamine

Hydralazine

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with hydralazine

Indoramin

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of indoramin

Insulin

MAOIs enhance hypoglycaemic effect of insulin

Levodopa

risk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with levodopa , avoid levodopa for at least 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

MAOIs

increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when MAOIs given with other MAOIs (avoid for at least 2 weeks after stopping previous MAOIs and then start at a reduced dose)

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Metformin

MAOIs enhance hypoglycaemic effect of metformin

Methyldopa

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of methyldopa

Methylphenidate

risk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with methylphenidate , some manufacturers advise avoid methylphenidate for at least 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Minoxidil

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with minoxidil

Mirtazapine

after stopping MAOIs do not start mirtazapine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 2 weeks after stopping mirtazapine

Moclobemide

after stopping MAOIs do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week

Moxonidine

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with moxonidine

Nefopam

avoidance of MAOIs advised by manufacturer of nefopam

Nicorandil

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with nicorandil

Nitrates

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with nitrates

Opioid Analgesics

possible CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when MAOIs given with opioid analgesics —some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Paroxetine

after stopping MAOIs do not start paroxetine for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping paroxetine

Pethidine

CNS excitation or depression (hypertension or hypotension) when MAOIs given with pethidine —avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Pholcodine

avoidance of pholcodine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of pholcodine

Promethazine

avoidance of promethazine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs advised by manufacturer of promethazine

Rasagiline

risk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with rasagiline , avoid MAOIs for at least 2 weeks after stopping rasagiline

Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Reboxetine

increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when MAOIs given with reboxetine (MAOIs should not be started until 1 week after stopping reboxetine, avoid reboxetine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs)

Rizatriptan

risk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with rizatriptan (avoid rizatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)

Selegiline

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with selegiline —manufacturer of selegiline advises avoid concomitant use

Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

Sertraline

after stopping MAOIs do not start sertraline for 2 weeks, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping sertraline

Sodium Nitroprusside

enhanced hypotensive effect when MAOIs given with sodium nitroprusside

Sulfonylureas

MAOIs enhance hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas

Sumatriptan

risk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with sumatriptan (avoid sumatriptan for 2 weeks after MAOIs)

Sympathomimetics

risk of hypertensive crisis when MAOIs given with sympathomimetics

Tetrabenazine

risk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with tetrabenazine (avoid tetrabenazine for 2 weeks after MAOIs)

Tolcapone

avoid concomitant use of MAOIs with tolcapone

Tramadol

possible increased serotonergic effects and increased risk of convulsions when MAOIs given with tramadol —some manufacturers advise avoid concomitant use and for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Tryptophan

CNS excitation and confusion when MAOIs given with tryptophan (reduce dose of tryptophan)

Venlafaxine

enhanced CNS effects and toxicity when MAOIs given with venlafaxine (venlafaxine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, avoid MAOIs for 1 week after stopping venlafaxine)

Zolmitriptan

increased risk of CNS toxicity when MAOIs given with zolmitriptan

Isocarboxazid belongs to Antidepressants and will have the following interaction information:

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atomoxetine

possible increased risk of convulsions when antidepressants given with atomoxetine
