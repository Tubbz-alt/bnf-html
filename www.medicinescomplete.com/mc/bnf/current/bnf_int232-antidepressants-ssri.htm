    Antidepressants, SSRI

    Antidepressants, SSRI has the following interaction information:

    Alcohol

    sedative effects possibly increased when SSRIs given with alcohol

    Antidepressants, Tricyclic

    SSRIs increase plasma concentration of some tricyclics

    Antiepileptics

    SSRIs antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)

    Aspirin

    increased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with aspirin

    Coumarins

    SSRIs possibly enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Cyproheptadine

    antidepressant effect of SSRIs possibly antagonised by cyproheptadine

    Dabigatran Etexilate

    possible increased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with dabigatran etexilate

    Duloxetine

    possible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with duloxetine

    Lithium

    Increased risk of CNS effects when SSRIs given with lithium (lithium toxicity reported)

    MAOIs

    CNS effects of SSRIs increased by MAOIs (risk of serious toxicity)

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Methylphenidate

    metabolism of SSRIs possibly inhibited by methylphenidate

    Methylthioninium

    risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with methylthioninium—avoid concomitant use (if avoidance not possible, use lowest possible dose of methylthioninium and observe patient for up to 4 hours after administration)

    Metoclopramide

    CNS toxicity reported when SSRIs given with metoclopramide

    NSAIDs

    increased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with NSAIDs

    Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

    Naratriptan

    possible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with naratriptan

    Pimozide

    SSRIs possibly increase plasma concentration of pimozide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

    Rasagiline

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with rasagiline

    Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Ritonavir

    plasma concentration of SSRIs possibly increased by ritonavir

    St John's Wort

    increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with St John's wort—avoid concomitant use

    Tramadol

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with tramadol

    Tryptophan

    agitation and nausea may occur when SSRIs given with tryptophan

    Antidepressants, SSRI belongs to Antidepressants and will have the following interaction information:

    Artemether with Lumefantrine

    avoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of artemether with lumefantrine

    Atomoxetine

    possible increased risk of convulsions when antidepressants given with atomoxetine

    Piperaquine with Artenimol

    avoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of piperaquine with artenimol

    Note: Piperaquine has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for up to 3 months after treatment has been stopped

    Escitalopram belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

    Cimetidine

    plasma concentration of escitalopram increased by cimetidine

    MAOIs

    escitalopram should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping escitalopram

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Metoprolol

    escitalopram increases plasma concentration of metoprolol

    Moclobemide

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when escitalopram given with moclobemide, preferably avoid concomitant use

    Omeprazole

    plasma concentration of escitalopram increased by omeprazole

    Selegiline

    avoidance of escitalopram advised by manufacturer of selegiline

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Sumatriptan

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when escitalopram given with sumatriptan

    Citalopram belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

    5HT1-receptor Agonists

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when citalopram given with 5HT1 agonists (manufacturer of citalopram advises avoid concomitant use)

    Bupropion

    plasma concentration of citalopram possibly increased by bupropion

    Cimetidine

    plasma concentration of citalopram increased by cimetidine

    Clozapine

    citalopram possibly increases plasma concentration of clozapine (increased risk of toxicity)

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    MAOIs

    citalopram should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping citalopram

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Metoprolol

    citalopram increases plasma concentration of metoprolol

    Moclobemide

    after stopping citalopram do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week

    Selegiline

    avoidance of citalopram advised by manufacturer of selegiline

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Sumatriptan

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when citalopram given with sumatriptan

    Telithromycin

    possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when citalopram given with telithromycin

    Ticagrelor

    possible increased risk of bleeding when citalopram given with ticagrelor

    Fluoxetine belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

    Alprazolam

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of alprazolam

    Aripiprazole

    fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)

    Atomoxetine

    fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of atomoxetine

    Carbamazepine

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine

    Clopidogrel

    fluoxetine possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

    Clozapine

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of clozapine

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Droperidol

    avoidance of fluoxetine advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

    Flecainide

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of flecainide

    Haloperidol

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of haloperidol

    MAOIs

    fluoxetine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetine

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Methadone

    fluoxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone

    Mirtazapine

    possible increased serotonergic effects when fluoxetine given with mirtazapine

    Moclobemide

    after stopping fluoxetine do not start moclobemide for 5 weeks

    Nifedipine

    fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of nifedipine (increased plasma concentration)

    Phenytoin

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of phenytoin

    Propafenone

    fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of propafenone

    Rasagiline

    fluoxetine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping rasagiline, also rasagiline should not be started until at least 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetine

    Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Risperidone

    fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of risperidone

    Selegiline

    increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when fluoxetine given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetine, avoid fluoxetine for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Sumatriptan

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when fluoxetine given with sumatriptan

    Tamoxifen

    fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of tamoxifen to active metabolite (avoid concomitant use)

    Tryptophan

    CNS toxicity reported when fluoxetine given with tryptophan

    Fluvoxamine belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

    Agomelatine

    fluvoxamine inhibits metabolism of agomelatine (increased plasma concentration)

    Asenapine

    fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of asenapine

    Benzodiazepines

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of some benzodiazepines

    Carbamazepine

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine

    Clopidogrel

    fluvoxamine possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

    Clozapine

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of clozapine

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Droperidol

    avoidance of fluvoxamine advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

    Duloxetine

    fluvoxamine inhibits metabolism of duloxetine—avoid concomitant use

    Frovatriptan

    fluvoxamine inhibits the metabolism of frovatriptan

    Haloperidol

    fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of haloperidol

    Lansoprazole

    fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of lansoprazole

    MAOIs

    fluvoxamine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping fluvoxamine

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Melatonin

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of melatonin—avoid concomitant use

    Methadone

    fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone

    Mirtazapine

    possible increased serotonergic effects when fluvoxamine given with mirtazapine

    Moclobemide

    after stopping fluvoxamine do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week

    Olanzapine

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of olanzapine

    Phenytoin

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of phenytoin

    Propranolol

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of propranolol

    Rasagiline

    fluvoxamine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping rasagiline

    Note: Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Reboxetine

    avoidance of fluvoxamine advised by manufacturer of reboxetine

    Roflumilast

    fluvoxamine inhibits the metabolism of roflumilast

    Ropivacaine

    fluvoxamine inhibits metabolism of ropivacaine—avoid prolonged administration of ropivacaine

    Selegiline

    increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when fluvoxamine given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 1 week after stopping fluvoxamine, avoid fluvoxamine for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Sumatriptan

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when fluvoxamine given with sumatriptan

    Theophylline

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of theophylline (concomitant use should usually be avoided, but where not possible halve theophylline dose and monitor plasma-theophylline concentration)

    Tizanidine

    fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of tizanidine (increased risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

    Zolmitriptan

    fluvoxamine possibly inhibits metabolism of zolmitriptan (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

    Paroxetine belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

    Aripiprazole

    paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)

    Asenapine

    plasma concentration of paroxetine possibly increased by asenapine

    Atomoxetine

    paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of atomoxetine

    Clozapine

    paroxetine increases plasma concentration of clozapine

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Darifenacin

    paroxetine increases plasma concentration of darifenacin

    Darunavir

    plasma concentration of paroxetine possibly reduced by darunavir

    Galantamine

    paroxetine increases plasma concentration of galantamine

    MAOIs

    paroxetine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping paroxetine

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Methadone

    paroxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone

    Metoprolol

    paroxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of metoprolol (enhanced effect)

    Moclobemide

    after stopping paroxetine do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week

    Perphenazine

    paroxetine inhibits metabolism of perphenazine (reduce dose of perphenazine)

    Phenobarbital

    plasma concentration of paroxetine reduced by phenobarbital

    Phenytoin

    plasma concentration of paroxetine reduced by phenytoin

    Procyclidine

    paroxetine increases plasma concentration of procyclidine

    Propafenone

    paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of propafenone

    Ranolazine

    paroxetine increases plasma concentration of ranolazine

    Risperidone

    paroxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of risperidone (increased risk of toxicity)

    Ritonavir

    plasma concentration of paroxetine possibly reduced by ritonavir

    Selegiline

    increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when paroxetine given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping paroxetine, avoid paroxetine for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Sumatriptan

    increased risk of CNS toxicity when paroxetine given with sumatriptan

    Tamoxifen

    paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of tamoxifen to active metabolite (avoid concomitant use)

    Ticagrelor

    possible increased risk of bleeding when paroxetine given with ticagrelor

    Sertraline belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

    Cimetidine

    plasma concentration of sertraline increased by cimetidine

    Clozapine

    sertraline increases plasma concentration of clozapine

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Darunavir

    plasma concentration of sertraline possibly reduced by darunavir

    Droperidol

    avoidance of sertraline advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

    Grapefruit Juice

    plasma concentration of sertraline possibly increased by grapefruit juice

    MAOIs

    sertraline should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs, also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping sertraline

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Methadone

    sertraline possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone

    Moclobemide

    after stopping sertraline do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week

    Phenytoin

    plasma concentration of sertraline possibly reduced by phenytoin, also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased

    Selegiline

    increased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when sertraline given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 1 week after stopping sertraline, avoid sertraline for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)

    Note: Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor

    Sumatriptan

    CNS toxicity reported when sertraline given with sumatriptan

    Ticagrelor

    possible increased risk of bleeding when sertraline given with ticagrelor

    Zolpidem

    sedative effects possibly increased when sertraline given with zolpidem
