    Phenobarbital

    Phenobarbital has the following interaction information:

    Abacavir

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of abacavir

    Alcohol

    increased sedative effect when phenobarbital given with alcohol

    Antidepressants, Tricyclic

    phenobarbital possibly accelerates metabolism of tricyclics (reduced plasma concentration)

    Aprepitant

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of aprepitant

    Aripiprazole

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of aripiprazole—increase dose of aripiprazole

    Axitinib

    phenobarbital possibly decreases plasma concentration of axitinib (increase dose of axitinib—consult axitinib product literature)

    Boceprevir

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of boceprevir (plasma concentration of boceprevir possibly reduced)

    Cabazitaxel

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel

    Calcium-channel Blockers

    phenobarbital probably reduces effects of calcium-channel blockers

    Carbamazepine

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital possibly increased by carbamazepine

    Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors

    increased risk of osteomalacia when phenobarbital given with carbonic anhydrase inhibitors

    Chloramphenicol

    phenobarbital possibly accelerates metabolism of chloramphenicol (reduced plasma concentration)

    Chlorpromazine

    plasma concentration of both drugs reduced when phenobarbital given with chlorpromazine

    Ciclosporin

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of ciclosporin (reduced plasma concentration)

    Clonazepam

    phenobarbital often reduces plasma concentration of clonazepam

    Clozapine

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of clozapine

    Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

    Corticosteroids

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of corticosteroids (reduced effect)

    Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

    Coumarins

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of coumarins (reduced anticoagulant effect)

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Crizotinib

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of crizotinib—manufacturer of crizotinib advises avoid concomitant use

    Darunavir

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of darunavir

    Disopyramide

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of disopyramide (reduced plasma concentration)

    Doxycycline

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of doxycycline (reduced plasma concentration)

    Dronedarone

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of dronedarone—avoid concomitant use

    Eplerenone

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of eplerenone—avoid concomitant use

    Ethosuximide

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of ethosuximide

    Etoposide

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of etoposide

    Etravirine

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of etravirine

    Folates

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital possibly reduced by folates

    Fosamprenavir

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of fosamprenavir

    Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

    Gefitinib

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of gefitinib

    Griseofulvin

    phenobarbital reduces absorption of griseofulvin (reduced effect)

    Haloperidol

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of haloperidol (reduced plasma concentration)

    Indinavir

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of indinavir

    Irinotecan

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of irinotecan and its active metabolite

    Isradipine

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of isradipine

    Itraconazole

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of itraconazole

    Lamotrigine

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of lamotrigine

    Lopinavir

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of lopinavir

    Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

    Methadone

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of methadone

    Methylphenidate

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital possibly increased by methylphenidate

    Metronidazole

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of metronidazole (reduced effect)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to topical metronidazole preparations

    Mianserin

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of mianserin (reduced plasma concentration)

    Montelukast

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of montelukast

    Nimodipine

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of nimodipine (plasma concentration of nimodipine reduced)

    Oestrogens

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of oestrogens (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

    Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

    Oxcarbazepine

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital increased by oxcarbazepine, also plasma concentration of an active metabolite of oxcarbazepine reduced

    Paracetamol

    phenobarbital possibly accelerates metabolism of paracetamol (also isolated reports of hepatotoxicity)

    Paroxetine

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of paroxetine

    Phenytoin

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital often increased by phenytoin, plasma concentration of phenytoin often reduced but may be increased

    Piperaquine with Artenimol

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of piperaquine with artenimol

    Note: Piperaquine has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for up to 3 months after treatment has been stopped

    Posaconazole

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of posaconazole

    Progestogens

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of progestogens (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

    Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

    Propafenone

    phenobarbital possibly accelerates metabolism of propafenone

    Propranolol

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of propranolol

    Reboxetine

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of reboxetine

    Rifampicin

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of rifampicin

    Rilpivirine

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of rilpivirine (plasma concentration of rilpivirine possibly reduced)

    Rufinamide

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of rufinamide

    Saquinavir

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of saquinavir

    Sodium Oxybate

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of sodium oxybate

    Stiripentol

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital increased by stiripentol

    Tacrolimus

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of tacrolimus

    Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

    Telaprevir

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

    Telithromycin

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of telithromycin (avoid during and for 2 weeks after phenobarbital)

    Theophylline

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of theophylline (reduced effect)

    Thyroid Hormones

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of thyroid hormones (may increase requirements for thyroid hormones in hypothyroidism)

    Tiagabine

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of tiagabine

    Ticagrelor

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of ticagrelor

    Topiramate

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of topiramate

    Toremifene

    phenobarbital accelerates metabolism of toremifene (reduced plasma concentration)

    Ulipristal

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of ulipristal (contraceptive effect of ulipristal possibly reduced)

    Valproate

    plasma concentration of phenobarbital increased by valproate (also plasma concentration of valproate reduced)

    Vandetanib

    avoidance of phenobarbital advised by manufacturer of vandetanib (plasma concentration of vandetanib possibly reduced)

    Vitamin D

    phenobarbital possibly increases requirements for vitamin D

    Voriconazole

    phenobarbital possibly reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole—avoid concomitant use

    Zonisamide

    phenobarbital reduces plasma concentration of zonisamide

    Phenobarbital belongs to Antiepileptics and will have the following interaction information:

    Antidepressants, SSRI

    anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by SSRIs (convulsive threshold lowered)

    Antidepressants, Tricyclic

    anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by tricyclics (convulsive threshold lowered)

    Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)

    anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics possibly antagonised by tricyclic-related antidepressants (convulsive threshold lowered)

    Antipsychotics

    anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by antipsychotics (convulsive threshold lowered)

    Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

    MAOIs

    anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics possibly antagonised by MAOIs (convulsive threshold lowered)

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Mefloquine

    anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics antagonised by mefloquine

    Orlistat

    possible increased risk of convulsions when antiepileptics given with orlistat

    St John's Wort

    avoid concomitant use of antiepileptics with St John's wort

    Primidone belongs to Phenobarbital but has no specific interaction information.

    Note: Primidone interactions as for phenobarbital
