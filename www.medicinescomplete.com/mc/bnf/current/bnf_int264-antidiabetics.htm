    Antidiabetics

    Antidiabetics has the following interaction information:

    Alcohol

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics enhanced by alcohol

    Anabolic Steroids

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics possibly enhanced by anabolic steroids

    Beta-blockers

    warning signs of hypoglycaemia (such as tremor) with antidiabetics may be masked when given with beta-blockers

    Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

    Corticosteroids

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics antagonised by corticosteroids

    Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

    Diazoxide

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics antagonised by diazoxide

    Diuretics, Loop

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics antagonised by loop diuretics

    Diuretics, Thiazide and related

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics antagonised by thiazides and related diuretics

    MAOIs

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics possibly enhanced by MAOIs

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Oestrogens

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics antagonised by oestrogens

    Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

    Pasireotide

    requirements for antidiabetics possibly reduced by pasireotide

    Progestogens

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics antagonised by progestogens

    Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

    Testosterone

    hypoglycaemic effect of antidiabetics possibly enhanced by testosterone

    Dapagliflozin belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Diuretics, Loop

    dapagliflozin possibly enhances diuretic effect of loop diuretics

    Diuretics, Thiazide and related

    dapagliflozin possibly enhances diuretic effect of thiazides and related diuretics

    Pioglitazone

    manufacturer of dapagliflozin advises avoid concomitant use with pioglitazone

    Linagliptin belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Rifampicin

    effects of linagliptin possibly reduced by rifampicin

    Liraglutide belongs to Antidiabetics but has no specific interaction information.

    Saxagliptin belongs to Antidiabetics but has no specific interaction information.

    Vildagliptin belongs to Antidiabetics but has no specific interaction information.

    Exenatide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Note: Other oral drugs may be taken at least 1 hour before or 4 hours after exenatide injection, or taken with a meal when exenatide is not administered, to minimise possible interference with absorption

    Warfarin

    exenatide possibly enhances anticoagulant effect of warfarin

    Sitagliptin belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Digoxin

    sitagliptin increases plasma concentration of digoxin

    Acarbose belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Colestyramine

    hypoglycaemic effect of acarbose possibly enhanced by colestyramine

    Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 1 hour before or 4–6 hours after colestyramine to reduce possible interference with absorption

    Digoxin

    acarbose possibly reduces plasma concentration of digoxin

    Neomycin

    hypoglycaemic effect of acarbose possibly enhanced by neomycin, also severity of gastro-intestinal effects increased

    Orlistat

    avoidance of acarbose advised by manufacturer of orlistat

    Pancreatin

    hypoglycaemic effect of acarbose antagonised by pancreatin

    Insulin belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    ACE Inhibitors

    hypoglycaemic effect of insulin possibly enhanced by ACE inhibitors

    Beta-blockers

    hypoglycaemic effect of insulin enhanced by beta-blockers

    Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

    Disopyramide

    hypoglycaemic effect of insulin possibly enhanced by disopyramide

    Fibrates

    may be improved glucose tolerance and an additive effect when insulin given with fibrates

    Lanreotide

    requirements for insulin possibly reduced by lanreotide

    MAOIs

    hypoglycaemic effect of insulin enhanced by MAOIs

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Nifedipine

    glucose tolerance occasionally impaired when insulin given with nifedipine

    Octreotide

    requirements for insulin possibly reduced by octreotide

    Nateglinide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Fluconazole

    hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide possibly enhanced by fluconazole

    Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

    Gemfibrozil

    hypoglycaemic effect of nateglinide possibly enhanced by gemfibrozil

    Rifampicin

    plasma concentration of nateglinide reduced by rifampicin

    Repaglinide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Ciclosporin

    hypoglycaemic effect of repaglinide possibly enhanced by ciclosporin

    Clarithromycin

    effects of repaglinide enhanced by clarithromycin

    Deferasirox

    plasma concentration of repaglinide increased by deferasirox

    Gemfibrozil

    increased risk of severe hypoglycaemia when repaglinide given with gemfibrozil—avoid concomitant use

    Itraconazole

    hypoglycaemic effect of repaglinide possibly enhanced by itraconazole

    Lanreotide

    requirements for repaglinide possibly reduced by lanreotide

    Lapatinib

    avoidance of repaglinide advised by manufacturer of lapatinib

    Octreotide

    requirements for repaglinide possibly reduced by octreotide

    Rifampicin

    hypoglycaemic effect of repaglinide possibly antagonised by rifampicin

    Trimethoprim

    hypoglycaemic effect of repaglinide possibly enhanced by trimethoprim—manufacturer advises avoid concomitant use

    Biguanides belongs to Antidiabetics but has no specific interaction information.

    Metformin belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    ACE Inhibitors

    hypoglycaemic effect of metformin possibly enhanced by ACE inhibitors

    Alcohol

    increased risk of lactic acidosis when metformin given with alcohol

    Cimetidine

    excretion of metformin reduced by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

    Disopyramide

    hypoglycaemic effect of metformin possibly enhanced by disopyramide

    Ketotifen

    thrombocyte count depressed when metformin given with ketotifen (manufacturer of ketotifen advises avoid concomitant use)

    Lanreotide

    requirements for metformin possibly reduced by lanreotide

    MAOIs

    hypoglycaemic effect of metformin enhanced by MAOIs

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Octreotide

    requirements for metformin possibly reduced by octreotide

    Rilpivirine

    plasma concentration of metformin possibly increased by rilpivirine

    Topiramate

    plasma concentration of metformin possibly increased by topiramate

    Sulfonylureas belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    ACE Inhibitors

    hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas possibly enhanced by ACE inhibitors

    Chloramphenicol

    effects of sulfonylureas enhanced by chloramphenicol

    Cimetidine

    hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas enhanced by cimetidine

    Coumarins

    hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas possibly enhanced by coumarins, also possible changes to anticoagulant effect

    Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

    Fibrates

    may be improved glucose tolerance and an additive effect when sulfonylureas given with fibrates

    Fluconazole

    plasma concentration of sulfonylureas increased by fluconazole

    Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

    Lanreotide

    requirements for sulfonylureas possibly reduced by lanreotide

    MAOIs

    hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas enhanced by MAOIs

    Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

    Miconazole

    plasma concentration of sulfonylureas increased by miconazole

    NSAIDs

    effects of sulfonylureas possibly enhanced by NSAIDs

    Note: See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs

    Octreotide

    requirements for sulfonylureas possibly reduced by octreotide

    Phenothiazines

    hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas possibly antagonised by phenothiazines

    Rifamycins

    metabolism of sulfonylureas possibly accelerated by rifamycins (reduced effect)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

    Sulfinpyrazone

    effects of sulfonylureas enhanced by sulfinpyrazone

    Sulfonamides

    effects of sulfonylureas rarely enhanced by sulfonamides

    Tetracyclines

    hypoglycaemic effect of sulfonylureas possibly enhanced by tetracyclines

    Trimethoprim

    effects of sulfonylureas rarely enhanced by trimethoprim

    Voriconazole

    plasma concentration of sulfonylureas possibly increased by voriconazole

    Glibenclamide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Bosentan

    increased risk of hepatotoxicity when glibenclamide given with bosentan—avoid concomitant use

    Colesevelam

    absorption of glibenclamide reduced by colesevelam

    Note: Other drugs should be taken at least 4 hours before or after colesevelam to reduce possible interference with absorption

    Fluvastatin

    plasma concentration of glibenclamide possibly increased by fluvastatin

    Norfloxacin

    effects of glibenclamide possibly enhanced by norfloxacin

    Topiramate

    plasma concentration of glibenclamide possibly reduced by topiramate

    Gliclazide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Disopyramide

    hypoglycaemic effect of gliclazide possibly enhanced by disopyramide

    Miconazole

    hypoglycaemic effect of gliclazide enhanced by miconazole—avoid concomitant use

    Glimepiride belongs to Antidiabetics but has no specific interaction information.

    Glipizide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Miconazole

    hypoglycaemic effect of glipizide enhanced by miconazole—avoid concomitant use

    Posaconazole

    hypoglycaemic effect of glipizide possibly enhanced by posaconazole

    Tolbutamide belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Aprepitant

    plasma concentration of tolbutamide reduced by aprepitant

    Leflunomide

    hypoglycaemic effect of tolbutamide possibly enhanced by leflunomide

    Note: Increased risk of toxicity with other haematotoxic and hepatotoxic drugs

    Phenytoin

    tolbutamide transiently increases plasma concentration of phenytoin (possibility of toxicity)

    Rifamycins

    metabolism of tolbutamide accelerated by rifamycins (reduced effect)

    Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

    Ritonavir

    plasma concentration of tolbutamide possibly increased by ritonavir

    Thiazolidinediones belongs to Antidiabetics but has no specific interaction information.

    Pioglitazone belongs to Antidiabetics and has the following interaction information:

    Dapagliflozin

    avoidance of pioglitazone advised by manufacturer of dapagliflozin
