Disopyramide

Disopyramide has the following interaction information:

Acetazolamideincreased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with acetazolamide
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Amisulprideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with amisulpride—avoid concomitant use
Antidepressants, Tricyclicincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with tricyclics
Antimuscarinicsincreased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when disopyramide given with antimuscarinics Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
Arsenic Trioxideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with arsenic trioxide
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with atomoxetine
Azithromycinplasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by azithromycin (increased risk of toxicity)
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by clarithromycin (increased risk of toxicity)
Diuretics, Loopincreased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with loop diuretics
Diuretics, Thiazide and relatedincreased cardiac toxicity with disopyramide if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics
Dronedaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with dronedarone—avoid concomitant use
Droperidolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with droperidol—avoid concomitant use
Erythromycinplasma concentration of disopyramide increased by erythromycin (increased risk of toxicity)Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Fingolimodpossible increased risk of bradycardia when disopyramide given with fingolimod
Gliclazide disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of gliclazide
Haloperidolpossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with haloperidol—avoid concomitant use
Insulin disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of insulin
Itraconazoleavoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of itraconazole
Ivabradineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with ivabradine
Ketoconazoleincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with ketoconazole—avoid concomitant use
Metformin disopyramide possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of metformin
Mizolastineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Nitrates disopyramide reduces effects of sublingual tablets of nitrates (failure to dissolve under tongue owing to dry mouth)
Pentamidine Isetionatepossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with pentamidine isetionate
Phenobarbitalmetabolism of disopyramide accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced plasma concentration)
Phenothiazinesincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with phenothiazines
Phenytoinplasma concentration of disopyramide reduced by phenytoin
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Ranolazineavoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of ranolazine
Rifamycinsmetabolism of disopyramide accelerated by rifamycins (reduced plasma concentration)Interactions do not apply to rifaximin
Ritonavirplasma concentration of disopyramide possibly increased by ritonavir (increased risk of toxicity)
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Sildenafilmanufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with sildenafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Sotalolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with sotalol—avoid concomitant use
Sulpirideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with sulpiride
Tadalafilmanufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with tadalafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Telapreviravoidance of disopyramide advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Tolterodineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with tolterodine
Vardenafilmanufacturer of disopyramide advises avoid concomitant use with vardenafil (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Verapamilincreased risk of myocardial depression and asystole when disopyramide given with verapamil
Warfarin disopyramide may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of warfarin
Zuclopenthixolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when disopyramide given with zuclopenthixol—avoid concomitant use

Disopyramide belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and will have the following interactions:

Anti-arrhythmicsincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with other anti-arrhythmics
Antipsychoticsincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT intervalIncreased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Beta-blockersincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Bupivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with bupivacaine
Levobupivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with levobupivacaine
Prilocaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with prilocaine
Ropivacaineincreased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with ropivacaine
