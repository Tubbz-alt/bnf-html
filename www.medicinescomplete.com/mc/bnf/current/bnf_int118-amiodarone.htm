Amiodarone

Amiodarone has the following interaction information:

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Acetazolamide

increased cardiac toxicity with amiodarone if hypokalaemia occurs with acetazolamide

Agalsidase Alfa and Beta

amiodarone possibly inhibits effects of agalsidase alfa and beta (manufacturers of agalsidase alfa and beta advise avoid concomitant use)

Amisulpride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with amisulpride —avoid concomitant use

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with tricyclics —avoid concomitant use

Arsenic Trioxide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with arsenic trioxide

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of amiodarone advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of amiodarone possibly increased by atazanavir

Atomoxetine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with atomoxetine

Benperidol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with benperidol —manufacturer of benperidol advises avoid concomitant use

Beta-blockers

increased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when amiodarone given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine —avoid concomitant use

Ciclosporin

amiodarone possibly increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin

Cimetidine

plasma concentration of amiodarone increased by cimetidine

Colchicine

amiodarone possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity

Coumarins

amiodarone inhibits metabolism of coumarins (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Dabigatran Etexilate

amiodarone increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate (reduce dose of dabigatran etexilate)

Digoxin

amiodarone increases plasma concentration of digoxin (halve dose of digoxin)

Diltiazem

increased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when amiodarone given with diltiazem

Disopyramide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with disopyramide —avoid concomitant use

Diuretics, Loop

increased cardiac toxicity with amiodarone if hypokalaemia occurs with loop diuretics

Diuretics, Thiazide and related

increased cardiac toxicity with amiodarone if hypokalaemia occurs with thiazides and related diuretics

Dronedarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with dronedarone —avoid concomitant use

Droperidol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with droperidol —avoid concomitant use

Eplerenone

amiodarone increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)

Erythromycin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with parenteral erythromycin —avoid concomitant use

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Fidaxomicin

avoidance of amiodarone advised by manufacturer of fidaxomicin

Fingolimod

possible increased risk of bradycardia when amiodarone given with fingolimod

Flecainide

amiodarone increases plasma concentration of flecainide (halve dose of flecainide)

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of amiodarone possibly increased by fosamprenavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of amiodarone increased by grapefruit juice

Haloperidol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with haloperidol —avoid concomitant use

Indinavir

plasma concentration of amiodarone possibly increased by indinavir —avoid concomitant use

Ivabradine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with ivabradine

Levofloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with levofloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Lithium

manufacturer of amiodarone advises avoid concomitant use with lithium (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Mefloquine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with mefloquine —avoid concomitant use

Mizolastine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with mizolastine —avoid concomitant use

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Nelfinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with nelfinavir —avoid concomitant use

Orlistat

plasma concentration of amiodarone possibly reduced by orlistat

Pentamidine Isetionate

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with pentamidine isetionate —avoid concomitant use

Phenindione

amiodarone inhibits metabolism of phenindione (enhanced anticoagulant effect)

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Phenothiazines

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with phenothiazines —avoid concomitant use

Phenytoin

amiodarone inhibits metabolism of phenytoin (increased plasma concentration)

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Quinine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with quinine —avoid concomitant use

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of amiodarone increased by ritonavir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Simvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when amiodarone given with simvastatin

Sotalol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with sotalol —avoid concomitant use

Sulfamethoxazole

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with sulfamethoxazole (as co-trimoxazole)—manufacturer of amiodarone advises avoid concomitant use of co-trimoxazole

Sulpiride

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with sulpiride

Telaprevir

avoidance of amiodarone advised by manufacturer of telaprevir (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Thyroid Hormones

for concomitant use of amiodarone and thyroid hormones see section 2.3.2

Tolterodine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with tolterodine

Trimethoprim

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with trimethoprim (as co-trimoxazole)—manufacturer of amiodarone advises avoid concomitant use of co-trimoxazole

Vandetanib

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with vandetanib —avoid concomitant use

Verapamil

increased risk of bradycardia, AV block and myocardial depression when amiodarone given with verapamil

Zuclopenthixol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when amiodarone given with zuclopenthixol —avoid concomitant use

Amiodarone belongs to Anti-arrhythmics and will have the following interaction information:

Anti-arrhythmics

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with other anti-arrhythmics

Antipsychotics

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when anti-arrhythmics that prolong the QT interval given with antipsychotics that prolong the QT interval

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Beta-blockers

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Bupivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with bupivacaine

Levobupivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with levobupivacaine

Prilocaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with prilocaine

Ropivacaine

increased myocardial depression when anti-arrhythmics given with ropivacaine
