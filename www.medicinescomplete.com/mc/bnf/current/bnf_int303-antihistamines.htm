Antihistamines

Antihistamines has the following interaction information:

Note: Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)

Alcohol

increased sedative effect when antihistamines given with alcohol (possibly less effect with non-sedating antihistamines)

Antidepressants, Tricyclic

increased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when antihistamines given with tricyclics

Antidepressants, Tricyclic (related)

possible increased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when antihistamines given with tricyclic-related antidepressants

Antimuscarinics

increased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when antihistamines given with antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Anxiolytics and Hypnotics

increased sedative effect when antihistamines given with anxiolytics and hypnotics

Betahistine

antihistamines theoretically antagonise effect of betahistine

Histamine

antihistamines theoretically antagonise effects of histamine —manufacturer of histamine advises avoid concomitant use

MAOIs

increased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when antihistamines given with MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor

Antihistamines, Non-sedating belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of non-sedating antihistamines possibly increased by ritonavir

Bilastine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of bilastine reduced by grapefruit juice

Acrivastine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Rupatadine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of rupatadine increased by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of rupatadine increased by grapefruit juice —avoid concomitant use

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of rupatadine increased by ketoconazole

Desloratadine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Cetirizine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Fexofenadine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Antacids

absorption of fexofenadine reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Rifampicin

effects of fexofenadine possibly reduced by rifampicin

Levocetirizine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Loratadine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidine

manufacturer of loratadine advises plasma concentration possibly increased by cimetidine

Erythromycin

manufacturer of loratadine advises plasma concentration possibly increased by erythromycin

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Ketoconazole

manufacturer of loratadine advises plasma concentration possibly increased by ketoconazole

Mizolastine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antifungals, Imidazole

metabolism of mizolastine possibly inhibited by imidazoles (avoid concomitant use)

Disopyramide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with disopyramide —avoid concomitant use

Erythromycin

metabolism of mizolastine inhibited by erythromycin —avoid concomitant use

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Flecainide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with flecainide —avoid concomitant use

Itraconazole

metabolism of mizolastine inhibited by itraconazole —avoid concomitant use

Ketoconazole

metabolism of mizolastine inhibited by ketoconazole —avoid concomitant use

Macrolides

metabolism of mizolastine possibly inhibited by macrolides (avoid concomitant use)

Note: See also Telithromycin

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Propafenone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with propafenone —avoid concomitant use

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Sotalol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with sotalol —avoid concomitant use

Vandetanib

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mizolastine given with vandetanib —avoid concomitant use

Antihistamines, Sedating belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Opioid Analgesics

sedative effects possibly increased when sedating antihistamines given with opioid analgesics

Alimemazine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Antazoline belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Buclizine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Chlorphenamine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Lopinavir

plasma concentration of chlorphenamine possibly increased by lopinavir

Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Cinnarizine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Clemastine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Cyclizine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Cyproheptadine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Antidepressants, SSRI

cyproheptadine possibly antagonises antidepressant effect of SSRIs

Hydroxyzine belongs to Antihistamines but has no specific interaction information.

Ketotifen belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

Metformin

thrombocyte count depressed when ketotifen given with metformin (manufacturer of ketotifen advises avoid concomitant use)

Promethazine belongs to Antihistamines and has the following interaction information:

MAOIs

manufacturer of promethazine advises avoid for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs

Note: For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
