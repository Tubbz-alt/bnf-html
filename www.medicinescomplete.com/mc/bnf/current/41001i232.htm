Antidepressants, SSRI

Antidepressants, SSRI has the following interaction information:

Alcoholsedative effects possibly increased when SSRIs given with alcohol
Antidepressants, Tricyclic SSRIs increase plasma concentration of some tricyclics
Antiepileptics SSRIs antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)
Aspirinincreased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with aspirin
Coumarins SSRIs possibly enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Cyproheptadineantidepressant effect of SSRIs possibly antagonised by cyproheptadine
Duloxetinepossible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with duloxetine
LithiumIncreased risk of CNS effects when SSRIs given with lithium (lithium toxicity reported)
MAOIsCNS effects of SSRIs increased by MAOIs (risk of serious toxicity)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Methylphenidatemetabolism of SSRIs possibly inhibited by methylphenidate
MetoclopramideCNS toxicity reported when SSRIs given with metoclopramide
Naratriptanpossible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with naratriptan
NSAIDsincreased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Pimozide SSRIs possibly increase plasma concentration of pimozide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Rasagilineincreased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with rasagiline Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Ritonavirplasma concentration of SSRIs possibly increased by ritonavir
St John's Wortincreased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Tramadolincreased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with tramadol
Tryptophanagitation and nausea may occur when SSRIs given with tryptophan

Antidepressants, SSRI belongs to Antidepressants and will have the following interactions:

Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atomoxetinepossible increased risk of convulsions when antidepressants given with atomoxetine

Escitalopram belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

Cimetidineplasma concentration of escitalopram increased by cimetidine
MAOIs escitalopram should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping escitalopramFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Metoprolol escitalopram increases plasma concentration of metoprolol
Moclobemideincreased risk of CNS toxicity when escitalopram given with moclobemide , preferably avoid concomitant use
Omeprazoleplasma concentration of escitalopram increased by omeprazole
Selegilineavoidance of escitalopram advised by manufacturer of selegiline Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sumatriptanincreased risk of CNS toxicity when escitalopram given with sumatriptan

Citalopram belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

5HT1-receptor Agonistsincreased risk of CNS toxicity when citalopram given with 5HT1 agonists (manufacturer of citalopram advises avoid concomitant use)
Bupropionplasma concentration of citalopram possibly increased by bupropion
Cimetidineplasma concentration of citalopram increased by cimetidine
Clozapine citalopram possibly increases plasma concentration of clozapine (increased risk of toxicity)Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
MAOIs citalopram should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping citalopramFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Metoprolol citalopram increases plasma concentration of metoprolol
Moclobemideafter stopping citalopram do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week
Selegilineavoidance of citalopram advised by manufacturer of selegiline Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sumatriptanincreased risk of CNS toxicity when citalopram given with sumatriptan
Ticagrelorpossible increased risk of bleeding when citalopram given with ticagrelor

Fluoxetine belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

Alprazolam fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of alprazolam
Aripiprazole fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)
Atomoxetine fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of atomoxetine
Carbamazepine fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Clopidogrel fluoxetine possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Clozapine fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Droperidolavoidance of fluoxetine advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Flecainide fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of flecainide
Haloperidol fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of haloperidol
MAOIs fluoxetine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetineFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Methadone fluoxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone
Mirtazapinepossible increased serotonergic effects when fluoxetine given with mirtazapine
Moclobemideafter stopping fluoxetine do not start moclobemide for 5 weeks
Nifedipine fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of nifedipine (increased plasma concentration)
Phenytoin fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of phenytoin
Rasagiline fluoxetine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping rasagiline , also rasagiline should not be started until at least 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetineRasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Risperidone fluoxetine increases plasma concentration of risperidone
Selegilineincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when fluoxetine given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 5 weeks after stopping fluoxetine, avoid fluoxetine for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sumatriptanincreased risk of CNS toxicity when fluoxetine given with sumatriptan
Tamoxifen fluoxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of tamoxifen to active metabolite (avoid concomitant use)
TryptophanCNS toxicity reported when fluoxetine given with tryptophan

Fluvoxamine belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

Agomelatine fluvoxamine inhibits metabolism of agomelatine (increased plasma concentration)
Asenapine fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of asenapine
Benzodiazepines fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of some benzodiazepines
Carbamazepine fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Clopidogrel fluvoxamine possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Clozapine fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Droperidolavoidance of fluvoxamine advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Duloxetine fluvoxamine inhibits metabolism of duloxetine—avoid concomitant use
Frovatriptan fluvoxamine inhibits the metabolism of frovatriptan
Haloperidol fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of haloperidol
Lansoprazole fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of lansoprazole
MAOIs fluvoxamine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping fluvoxamineFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Melatonin fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of melatonin—avoid concomitant use
Methadone fluvoxamine possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone
Mirtazapinepossible increased serotonergic effects when fluvoxamine given with mirtazapine
Moclobemideafter stopping fluvoxamine do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week
Olanzapine fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of olanzapine
Phenytoin fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of phenytoin
Propranolol fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of propranolol
Rasagiline fluvoxamine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping rasagiline Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Reboxetineavoidance of fluvoxamine advised by manufacturer of reboxetine
Roflumilast fluvoxamine inhibits the metabolism of roflumilast
Ropivacaine fluvoxamine inhibits metabolism of ropivacaine—avoid prolonged administration of ropivacaine
Selegilineincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when fluvoxamine given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 1 week after stopping fluvoxamine, avoid fluvoxamine for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sumatriptanincreased risk of CNS toxicity when fluvoxamine given with sumatriptan
Theophylline fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of theophylline (concomitant use should usually be avoided, but where not possible halve theophylline dose and monitor plasma-theophylline concentration)
Tizanidine fluvoxamine increases plasma concentration of tizanidine (increased risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Zolmitriptan fluvoxamine possibly inhibits metabolism of zolmitriptan (reduce dose of zolmitriptan)

Paroxetine belongs to Antidepressants, SSRI and has the following interaction information:

Aripiprazole paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)
Asenapineplasma concentration of paroxetine possibly increased by asenapine
Atomoxetine paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of atomoxetine
Clozapine paroxetine increases plasma concentration of clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Darifenacin paroxetine increases plasma concentration of darifenacin
Darunavirplasma concentration of paroxetine possibly reduced by darunavir
Galantamine paroxetine increases plasma concentration of galantamine
MAOIs paroxetine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 1 week after stopping paroxetineFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Methadone paroxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of methadone
Metoprolol paroxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of metoprolol (enhanced effect)
Moclobemideafter stopping paroxetine do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week
Perphenazine paroxetine inhibits metabolism of perphenazine (reduce dose of perphenazine)
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of paroxetine reduced by phenobarbital
Phenytoinplasma concentration of paroxetine reduced by phenytoin
Procyclidine paroxetine increases plasma concentration of procyclidine
Propafenone paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of propafenone (increased risk of toxicity)
Ranolazine paroxetine increases plasma concentration of ranolazine
Risperidone paroxetine possibly increases plasma concentration of risperidone (increased risk of toxicity)
Ritonavirplasma concentration of paroxetine possibly reduced by ritonavir
Selegilineincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when paroxetine given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping paroxetine, avoid paroxetine for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Sumatriptanincreased risk of CNS toxicity when paroxetine given with sumatriptan
Tamoxifen paroxetine possibly inhibits metabolism of tamoxifen to active metabolite (avoid concomitant use)
Ticagrelorpossible increased risk of bleeding when paroxetine given with ticagrelor

Cimetidineplasma concentration of sertraline increased by cimetidine
Clozapine sertraline increases plasma concentration of clozapine Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Darunavirplasma concentration of sertraline possibly reduced by darunavir
Droperidolavoidance of sertraline advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Grapefruit Juiceplasma concentration of sertraline possibly increased by grapefruit juice

Selegilineincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when sertraline given with selegiline (selegiline should not be started until 1 week after stopping sertraline, avoid sertraline for 2 weeks after stopping selegiline)Selegiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
