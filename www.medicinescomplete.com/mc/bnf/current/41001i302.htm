Itraconazole

Itraconazole has the following interaction information:

Alfentanil itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of alfentanil
Aliskiren itraconazole increases plasma concentration of aliskiren—avoid concomitant use
Alprazolam itraconazole increases plasma concentration of alprazolam
Antacidsabsorption of itraconazole reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Apixabanavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of apixaban
Aripiprazole itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)
Atorvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when itraconazole given with atorvastatin
Bosentan itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of bosentan
Budesonide itraconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled budesonide
Buspirone itraconazole increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)
Busulfan itraconazole inhibits metabolism of busulfan (increased risk of toxicity)
Cabazitaxelavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel
Calcium-channel Blockersnegative inotropic effect possibly increased when itraconazole given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Calcium-channel Blockers (dihydropyridines) itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of dihydropyridines (increased plasma concentration)
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by carbamazepine
Ciclosporin itraconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of itraconazole increased by clarithromycin
Clopidogrel itraconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Colchicine itraconazole possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)
Corticosteroids itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Coumarins itraconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Cyclophosphamide itraconazole possibly increases side-effects of cyclophosphamide
Dabigatran Etexilate itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate—manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use
Darifenacinavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of darifenacin
Digoxin itraconazole increases plasma concentration of digoxin
Disopyramidemanufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use with disopyramide
Dronedaroneavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of dronedarone
Efavirenzplasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by efavirenz
Eletriptan itraconazole increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Eplerenone itraconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone—avoid concomitant use
Everolimus itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of everolimus—manufacturer of everolimus advises avoid concomitant use
Felodipine itraconazole inhibits metabolism of felodipine (increased plasma concentration)
Fesoterodinemanufacturer of fesoterodine advises dose reduction when itraconazole given with fesoterodine—consult fesoterodine product literature
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of both drugs may increase when itraconazole given with fosamprenavir Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Gefitinib itraconazole increases plasma concentration of gefitinib
Haloperidol itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of haloperidol
Histamine H2-antagonistsabsorption of itraconazole reduced by histamine H2-antagonists
Indinavir itraconazole increases plasma concentration of indinavir (consider reducing dose of indinavir)
Ivabradine itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ivabradine—avoid concomitant use
Lapatinibavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of lapatinib
Lercanidipineavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine
Methylprednisolone itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of methylprednisolone
Micafunginplasma concentration of itraconazole increased by micafungin (consider reducing dose of itraconazole)
Midazolam itraconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Mizolastine itraconazole inhibits metabolism of mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Nevirapineplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by nevirapine—consider increasing dose of itraconazole
Nilotinibavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of nilotinib
Pazopanibavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly reduced by phenobarbital
Phenytoinplasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by phenytoin—avoid concomitant use
Proton Pump Inhibitorsabsorption of itraconazole reduced by proton pump inhibitors
Ranolazine itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—manufacturer of ranolazine advises avoid concomitant use
Repaglinide itraconazole possibly enhances hypoglycaemic effect of repaglinide
Rifabutinplasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by rifabutin—manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use
Rifampicinplasma concentration of itraconazole reduced by rifampicin—manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use
Ritonavircombination of itraconazole with ritonavir may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)
Rivaroxabanavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of rivaroxaban
Sildenafil itraconazole increases plasma concentration of sildenafil—reduce initial dose of sildenafil
Simvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when itraconazole given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)
Sirolimus itraconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus—avoid concomitant use
Solifenacin itraconazole increases plasma concentration of solifenacin
Tacrolimus itraconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Tadalafil itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of tadalafil
Telaprevirplasma concentration of itraconazole possibly increased by telaprevir
Tolterodineavoidance of itraconazole advised by manufacturer of tolterodine
Vardenafil itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of vardenafil—avoid concomitant use
Vincristine itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of vincristine (increased risk of neurotoxicity)
Vinflunine itraconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of vinflunine—manufacturer of vinflunine advises avoid concomitant use
Vinorelbine itraconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of vinorelbine (increased risk of neurotoxicity)

Itraconazole belongs to Antifungals, Triazole and will have the following interactions:

Amphotericin triazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when triazoles given with atorvastatin
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when triazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Fentanyl triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of fentanyl
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when triazoles given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quetiapine triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Reboxetineavoidance of triazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine
Rifabutin triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)
Saquinavir triazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Itraconazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.
