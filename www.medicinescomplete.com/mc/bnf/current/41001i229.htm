Antidepressants

Antidepressants has the following interaction information:

Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of antidepressants advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atomoxetinepossible increased risk of convulsions when antidepressants given with atomoxetine

Agomelatine belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Ciprofloxacinmanufacturer of agomelatine advises avoid concomitant use with ciprofloxacin
Fluvoxaminemetabolism of agomelatine inhibited by fluvoxamine (increased plasma concentration)

Mirtazapine belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Alcoholincreased sedative effect when mirtazapine given with alcohol
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsincreased sedative effect when mirtazapine given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Carbamazepineplasma concentration of mirtazapine reduced by carbamazepine
Cimetidineplasma concentration of mirtazapine increased by cimetidine
Clonidine mirtazapine possibly antagonises hypotensive effect of clonidine
Fluoxetinepossible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with fluoxetine
Fluvoxaminepossible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with fluvoxamine
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of mirtazapine increased by ketoconazole
MAOIs mirtazapine should not be started until 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs , also MAOIs should not be started until at least 2 weeks after stopping mirtazapineFor interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Moclobemideafter stopping mirtazapine do not start moclobemide for at least 1 week
Phenytoinplasma concentration of mirtazapine reduced by phenytoin
Tramadolpossible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with tramadol
Venlafaxinepossible increased serotonergic effects when mirtazapine given with venlafaxine
Warfarin mirtazapine enhances anticoagulant effect of warfarin

Antidepressants, Noradrenaline Re-uptake Inhibitors belongs to Antidepressants but has no specific interaction information.

Ergotamine and Methysergidepossible risk of hypertension when reboxetine given with ergotamine and methysergide

MAOIsincreased risk of hypertension and CNS excitation when reboxetine given with MAOIs (MAOIs should not be started until 1 week after stopping reboxetine, avoid reboxetine for 2 weeks after stopping MAOIs)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of reboxetine possibly reduced by phenobarbital

Antidepressants, SSRI belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Alcoholsedative effects possibly increased when SSRIs given with alcohol
Antidepressants, Tricyclic SSRIs increase plasma concentration of some tricyclics
Antiepileptics SSRIs antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)
Aspirinincreased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with aspirin
Coumarins SSRIs possibly enhance anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Cyproheptadineantidepressant effect of SSRIs possibly antagonised by cyproheptadine
Duloxetinepossible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with duloxetine
LithiumIncreased risk of CNS effects when SSRIs given with lithium (lithium toxicity reported)
MAOIsCNS effects of SSRIs increased by MAOIs (risk of serious toxicity)For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Methylphenidatemetabolism of SSRIs possibly inhibited by methylphenidate
MetoclopramideCNS toxicity reported when SSRIs given with metoclopramide
Naratriptanpossible increased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with naratriptan
NSAIDsincreased risk of bleeding when SSRIs given with NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Pimozide SSRIs possibly increase plasma concentration of pimozide (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Rasagilineincreased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with rasagiline Rasagiline is a MAO-B inhibitor
Ritonavirplasma concentration of SSRIs possibly increased by ritonavir
St John's Wortincreased serotonergic effects when SSRIs given with St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Tramadolincreased risk of CNS toxicity when SSRIs given with tramadol
Tryptophanagitation and nausea may occur when SSRIs given with tryptophan

Antidepressants, Tricyclic belongs to Antidepressants and has the following interaction information:

Adrenaline (epinephrine)increased risk of hypertension and arrhythmias when tricyclics given with adrenaline (epinephrine) (but local anaesthetics with adrenaline appear to be safe)
Adrenergic Neurone Blockers tricyclics antagonise hypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers
Alcoholincreased sedative effect when tricyclics given with alcohol
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Anaesthetics, Generalincreased risk of arrhythmias and hypotension when tricyclics given with general anaesthetics See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1
Antidepressants, SSRIplasma concentration of some tricyclics increased by SSRIs
Antiepileptics tricyclics antagonise anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics (convulsive threshold lowered)
Antihistaminesincreased antimuscarinic and sedative effects when tricyclics given with antihistamines Sedative interactions apply to a lesser extent to the non-sedating antihistamines. Interactions do not generally apply to antihistamines used for topical action (including inhalation)
Antimuscarinicsincreased risk of antimuscarinic side-effects when tricyclics given with antimuscarinics Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
Antipsychoticsplasma concentration of tricyclics increased by antipsychotics—possibly increased risk of ventricular arrhythmiasIncreased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsincreased sedative effect when tricyclics given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Apraclonidineavoidance of tricyclics advised by manufacturer of apraclonidine
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when tricyclics given with atomoxetine
Baclofen tricyclics enhance muscle relaxant effect of baclofen
Brimonidineavoidance of tricyclics advised by manufacturer of brimonidine
Bupropionplasma concentration of tricyclics possibly increased by bupropion (possible increased risk of convulsions)
Cannabis Extractpossible increased risk of hypertension and tachycardia when tricyclics given with cannabis extract

Coumarins tricyclics may enhance or reduce anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
