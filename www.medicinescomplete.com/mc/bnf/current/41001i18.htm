Guanethidine

Guanethidine has the following interaction information:

Dexamfetaminehypotensive effect of guanethidine antagonised by dexamfetamine

Guanethidine belongs to Adrenergic Neurone Blockers and will have the following interactions:

Alcoholenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with alcohol
Alpha-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with alpha-blockers
Alprostadilenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with alprostadil
Anaesthetics, Generalenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with general anaesthetics See also Surgery and Long-term Medication, section 15.1
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonistsenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Antidepressants, Tricyclichypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by tricyclics
Anxiolytics and Hypnoticsenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with anxiolytics and hypnotics
Baclofenenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with baclofen
Beta-blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with beta-blockers Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind
Calcium-channel Blockersenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with calcium-channel blockers Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Chlorpromazinehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by higher doses of chlorpromazine
Clonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with clonidine
Corticosteroidshypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Diazoxideenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with diazoxide
Diureticsenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with diuretics
Ephedrinehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by ephedrine
Haloperidolhypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by haloperidol
Hydralazineenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with hydralazine
Isometheptenehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by isometheptene
Levodopaenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with levodopa
MAOIsenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with MAOIs For interactions of reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs) see Moclobemide, and for interactions of MAO-B inhibitors see Rasagiline and Selegiline; the antibacterial Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor
Metaraminolhypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by metaraminol
Methyldopaenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with methyldopa
Methylphenidatehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by methylphenidate
Minoxidilenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with minoxidil
Moxisylyteenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with moxisylyte
Moxonidineenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with moxonidine
Nitratesenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with nitrates
Noradrenaline (norepinephrine)hypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by noradrenaline (norepinephrine)
NSAIDshypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Oestrogenshypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by oestrogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Oxymetazolinehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by oxymetazoline
Phenothiazinesenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with phenothiazines
Phenylephrinehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by phenylephrine
Pizotifenhypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by pizotifen
Pseudoephedrinehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by pseudoephedrine
Sodium Nitroprussideenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with sodium nitroprusside
Tizanidineenhanced hypotensive effect when adrenergic neurone blockers given with tizanidine
Xylometazolinehypotensive effect of adrenergic neurone blockers antagonised by xylometazoline
