Aminophylline

Aminophylline has no specific interaction information.

Aminophylline belongs to Theophylline and will have the following interactions:

Acetazolamideincreased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with acetazolamide
Aciclovirplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by aciclovir Interactions do not apply to topical aciclovir preparations
Adenosine theophylline antagonises anti-arrhythmic effect of adenosine Possibility of interaction with drugs tending to impair myocardial conduction
Allopurinolplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by allopurinol
Benzodiazepines theophylline possibly reduces effects of benzodiazepines
Calcium-channel Blockersplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by calcium-channel blockers (enhanced effect)Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Carbamazepinemetabolism of theophylline accelerated by carbamazepine (reduced effect)
Cimetidinemetabolism of theophylline inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)
Ciprofloxacinplasma concentration of theophylline increased by ciprofloxacin
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by clarithromycin
Corticosteroidsincreased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Diltiazemplasma concentration of theophylline increased by diltiazem
Disulfirammetabolism of theophylline inhibited by disulfiram (increased risk of toxicity)
Diuretics, Loopincreased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with loop diuretics
Diuretics, Thiazide and relatedincreased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with thiazides and related diuretics
Doxapramincreased CNS stimulation when theophylline given with doxapram
Ephedrinemanufacturer of theophylline advises avoid concomitant use with ephedrine in children
Erythromycinplasma concentration of theophylline increased by erythromycin (also theophylline may reduce absorption of oral erythromycin)Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Febuxostatcaution with theophylline advised by manufacturer of febuxostat
Fluconazoleplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by fluconazole In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Fluvoxamineplasma concentration of theophylline increased by fluvoxamine (concomitant use should usually be avoided, but where not possible halve theophylline dose and monitor plasma-theophylline concentration)
Halothaneincreased risk of arrhythmias when theophylline given with halothane
Influenza Vaccineplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by influenza vaccine
Interferon Alfametabolism of theophylline inhibited by interferon alfa (consider reducing dose of theophylline)
Isoniazidplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by isoniazid
Ketamineincreased risk of convulsions when theophylline given with ketamine
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by ketoconazole
Lithium theophylline increases excretion of lithium (reduced plasma concentration)
Methotrexateplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by methotrexate
Norfloxacinplasma concentration of theophylline increased by norfloxacin
Oestrogensplasma concentration of theophylline increased by oestrogens (consider reducing dose of theophylline)Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Pentoxifyllineplasma concentration of theophylline increased by pentoxifylline
Phenobarbitalmetabolism of theophylline accelerated by phenobarbital (reduced effect)
Phenytoinplasma concentration of both drugs reduced when theophylline given with phenytoin
Propafenoneplasma concentration of theophylline increased by propafenone
Quinolonespossible increased risk of convulsions when theophylline given with quinolones
Rifampicinmetabolism of theophylline accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration)
Ritonavirmetabolism of theophylline accelerated by ritonavir (reduced plasma concentration)
Roflumilastavoidance of theophylline advised by manufacturer of roflumilast
St John's Wortplasma concentration of theophylline reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Sucralfateabsorption of theophylline possibly reduced by sucralfate (give at least 2 hours apart)
Sulfinpyrazoneplasma concentration of theophylline reduced by sulfinpyrazone
Sympathomimetics, Beta2increased risk of hypokalaemia when theophylline given with high doses of beta2 sympathomimetics—see Hypokalaemia, section 3.1.1.1
Verapamilplasma concentration of theophylline increased by verapamil (enhanced effect)
Zafirlukastplasma concentration of theophylline possibly increased by zafirlukast , also plasma concentration of zafirlukast reduced
