Ketoconazole

Ketoconazole has the following interaction information:

Alfuzosin

ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of alfuzosin

Aliskiren

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of aliskiren

Alitretinoin

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of alitretinoin

Almotriptan

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of almotriptan (increased risk of toxicity)

Alprazolam

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of alprazolam

Antacids

absorption of ketoconazole reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Antimuscarinics

absorption of ketoconazole reduced by antimuscarinics

Note: Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation

Apixaban

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of apixaban —manufacturer of apixaban advises avoid concomitant use

Aprepitant

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of aprepitant

Aripiprazole

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)

Boceprevir

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of boceprevir

Bortezomib

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of bortezomib

Bosentan

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of bosentan

Budesonide

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled and oral budesonide

Buprenorphine

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of buprenorphine (reduce dose of buprenorphine)

Cabazitaxel

avoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel

Calcium-channel Blockers (dihydropyridines)

ketoconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of dihydropyridines (increased plasma concentration)

Carbamazepine

ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine

Ciclesonide

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of ciclesonide

Ciclosporin

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)

Cilostazol

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of cilostazol (consider reducing dose of cilostazol)

Cinacalcet

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of cinacalcet (increased plasma concentration)

Clopidogrel

ketoconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel

Colchicine

ketoconazole possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)

Corticosteroids

ketoconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Coumarins

ketoconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Dabigatran Etexilate

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate —avoid concomitant use

Darifenacin

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of darifenacin —avoid concomitant use

Darunavir

plasma concentration of both drugs increased when ketoconazole given with darunavir

Dasatinib

ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of dasatinib

Disopyramide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when ketoconazole given with disopyramide —avoid concomitant use

Docetaxel

in vitro studies suggest a possible interaction between ketoconazole and docetaxel (consult docetaxel product literature)

Domperidone

manufacturer of ketoconazole advises avoid concomitant use with domperidone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Dronedarone

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of dronedarone —avoid concomitant use

Eletriptan

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use

Eplerenone

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone —avoid concomitant use

Erlotinib

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of erlotinib (increased plasma concentration)

Everolimus

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of everolimus —avoid concomitant use

Felodipine

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of felodipine (increased plasma concentration)

Fesoterodine

manufacturer of fesoterodine advises dose reduction when ketoconazole given with fesoterodine —consult fesoterodine product literature

Fidaxomicin

avoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of fidaxomicin

Fingolimod

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of fingolimod

Fluticasone

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled fluticasone

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of ketoconazole increased by fosamprenavir (also plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly increased)

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Galantamine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of galantamine

Histamine H2-antagonists

absorption of ketoconazole reduced by histamine H2-antagonists

Imatinib

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of imatinib

Indinavir

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of indinavir (consider reducing dose of indinavir)

Irinotecan

ketoconazole reduces plasma concentration of irinotecan (but concentration of active metabolite of irinotecan increased)—avoid concomitant use

Isoniazid

plasma concentration of ketoconazole possibly reduced by isoniazid

Ivabradine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of ivabradine —avoid concomitant use

Lanthanum

absorption of ketoconazole possibly reduced by lanthanum (give at least 2 hours apart)

Lapatinib

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of lapatinib —avoid concomitant use

Lercanidipine

avoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine

Loratadine

manufacturer of loratadine advises ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of loratadine

Maraviroc

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of maraviroc (consider reducing dose of maraviroc)

Mefloquine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of mefloquine

Methylprednisolone

ketoconazole inhibits the metabolism of methylprednisolone

Midazolam

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)

Mirtazapine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of mirtazapine

Mizolastine

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of mizolastine —avoid concomitant use

Mometasone

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled mometasone

Nevirapine

plasma concentration of ketoconazole reduced by nevirapine —avoid concomitant use

Nilotinib

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of nilotinib —avoid concomitant use

Paricalcitol

ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of paricalcitol

Pazopanib

avoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib

Phenytoin

plasma concentration of ketoconazole reduced by phenytoin

Proton Pump Inhibitors

absorption of ketoconazole reduced by proton pump inhibitors

Ranolazine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of ranolazine —avoid concomitant use

Rifampicin

metabolism of ketoconazole accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration), also plasma concentration of rifampicin may be reduced by ketoconazole

Ritonavir

combination of ketoconazole with ritonavir may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)

Rivaroxaban

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of rivaroxaban —avoid concomitant use

Rupatadine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of rupatadine

Salmeterol

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of salmeterol (increased plasma concentration)

Saquinavir

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of saquinavir

Sildenafil

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of sildenafil —reduce initial dose of sildenafil

Simvastatin

increased risk of myopathy when ketoconazole given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)

Sirolimus

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus —avoid concomitant use

Solifenacin

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of solifenacin

Sucralfate

absorption of ketoconazole reduced by sucralfate

Sunitinib

ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of sunitinib (increased plasma concentration)

Tacrolimus

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Tadalafil

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of tadalafil —manufacturer of tadalafil advises avoid concomitant use

Telaprevir

plasma concentration of both drugs possibly increased when ketoconazole given with telaprevir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Telithromycin

avoidance of concomitant ketoconazole in severe renal and hepatic impairment advised by manufacturer of telithromycin

Temsirolimus

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of temsirolimus —avoid concomitant use

Note: The main active metabolite of temsirolimus is sirolimus—see also interactions of sirolimus and consult product literature

Theophylline

ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of theophylline

Ticagrelor

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of ticagrelor —manufacturer of ticagrelor advises avoid concomitant use

Tolterodine

avoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of tolterodine

Tolvaptan

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of tolvaptan

Tretinoin

ketoconazole possibly increases risk of tretinoin toxicity

Vardenafil

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of vardenafil —avoid concomitant use

Vinflunine

ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of vinflunine —manufacturer of vinflunine advises avoid concomitant use

Ketoconazole belongs to Antifungals, Imidazole and will have the following interaction information:

Amphotericin

imidazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin

Note: Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of imidazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Atorvastatin

possible increased risk of myopathy when imidazoles given with atorvastatin

Ergotamine and Methysergide

increased risk of ergotism when imidazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide —avoid concomitant use

Mizolastine

imidazoles possibly inhibit metabolism of mizolastine (avoid concomitant use)

Oestrogens

anecdotal reports of contraceptive failure when imidazoles given with oestrogens

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when imidazoles given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Quetiapine

imidazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine —manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use

Reboxetine

avoidance of imidazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine

Saquinavir

imidazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir

Tacrolimus

imidazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Ketoconazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.
