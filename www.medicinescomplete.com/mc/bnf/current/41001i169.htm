Erythromycin

Erythromycin has the following interaction information:

Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Alfentanil erythromycin increases plasma concentration of alfentanil
Amiodaroneincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with amiodarone—avoid concomitant useAmiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped
Amisulprideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with amisulpride—avoid concomitant use
Arsenic Trioxideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with arsenic trioxide
Atomoxetineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with atomoxetine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when erythromycin given with atorvastatin
Bromocriptine erythromycin increases plasma concentration of bromocriptine (increased risk of toxicity)
Buspirone erythromycin increases plasma concentration of buspirone (reduce dose of buspirone)
Cabergoline erythromycin increases plasma concentration of cabergoline (increased risk of toxicity)
Calcium-channel Blockers erythromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of calcium-channel blockers (increased risk of side-effects)Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine
Carbamazepine erythromycin increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Ciclosporin erythromycin inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Cilostazol erythromycin increases plasma concentration of cilostazol (consider reducing dose of cilostazol)
Cimetidineplasma concentration of erythromycin increased by cimetidine (increased risk of toxicity, including deafness)
Clopidogrel erythromycin possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Clozapine erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of clozapine (possible increased risk of convulsions)Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis
Colchicine erythromycin possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)
Corticosteroids erythromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Coumarins erythromycin enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Darifenacin erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of darifenacin
Dienogest erythromycin increases plasma concentration of dienogest
Disopyramide erythromycin increases plasma concentration of disopyramide (increased risk of toxicity)
Docetaxelin vitro studies suggest a possible interaction between erythromycin and docetaxel (consult docetaxel product literature)
Domperidonepossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with domperidone
Dronedarone erythromycin increases plasma concentration of dronedarone (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias—avoid concomitant use)
Eletriptan erythromycin increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Eplerenone erythromycin increases plasma concentration of eplerenone (reduce dose of eplerenone)
Estradiol erythromycin increases plasma concentration of estradiol
Everolimus erythromycin increases plasma concentration of everolimus
Galantamine erythromycin increases plasma concentration of galantamine
Ivabradineincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with ivabradine—avoid concomitant use
Lercanidipineavoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine
Loratadinemanufacturer of loratadine advises erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of loratadine
Methylprednisolone erythromycin inhibits the metabolism of methylprednisolone
Midazolam erythromycin inhibits metabolism of midazolam (increased plasma concentration with increased sedation)
Mizolastine erythromycin inhibits metabolism of mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Moxifloxacinincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with moxifloxacin—avoid concomitant use
Pentamidine Isetionateincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with pentamidine isetionate
Pimozidepossible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Pravastatin erythromycin increases plasma concentration of pravastatin
Quetiapine erythromycin increases plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Rifabutin erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of rifabutin (increased risk of toxicity—reduce rifabutin dose)
Ritonavirplasma concentration of erythromycin possibly increased by ritonavir
Rosuvastatin erythromycin reduces plasma concentration of rosuvastatin
Rupatadine erythromycin increases plasma concentration of rupatadine
Saquinavirincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when erythromycin given with saquinavir—avoid concomitant use
Sildenafil erythromycin increases plasma concentration of sildenafil—reduce initial dose of sildenafil
Simvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when erythromycin given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)
Sirolimusplasma concentration of both drugs increased when erythromycin given with sirolimus
Sulpirideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with sulpiride
Tacrolimus erythromycin increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Tadalafil erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of tadalafil
Telaprevirplasma concentration of both drugs possibly increased when erythromycin given with telaprevir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Theophylline erythromycin increases plasma concentration of theophylline (also theophylline may reduce absorption of oral erythromycin)
Ticagrelor erythromycin possibly increases plasma concentration of ticagrelor
Tolterodineavoidance of erythromycin advised by manufacturer of tolterodine
Valproate erythromycin possibly inhibits metabolism of valproate (increased plasma concentration)
Vardenafil erythromycin increases plasma concentration of vardenafil (reduce dose of vardenafil)
Vinblastine erythromycin increases toxicity of vinblastine—avoid concomitant use
Zafirlukast erythromycin reduces plasma concentration of zafirlukast
Zopiclone erythromycin inhibits the metabolism of zopiclone
Zuclopenthixolincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when parenteral erythromycin given with zuclopenthixol—avoid concomitant use

Erythromycin belongs to Macrolides and will have the following interactions:

See also Telithromycin
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of macrolides advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Bromocriptine macrolides possibly increase plasma concentration of bromocriptine (increased risk of toxicity)
Cabergoline macrolides possibly increase plasma concentration of cabergoline (increased risk of toxicity)
Ciclosporin macrolides possibly inhibit metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Digoxin macrolides increase plasma concentration of digoxin (increased risk of toxicity)
Droperidolavoidance of macrolides advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when macrolides given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Mizolastine macrolides possibly inhibit metabolism of mizolastine (avoid concomitant use)
Reboxetineavoidance of macrolides advised by manufacturer of reboxetine

Erythromycin belongs to Antibacterials and will have the following interactions:

Typhoid Vaccine (oral) antibacterials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine—see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4
