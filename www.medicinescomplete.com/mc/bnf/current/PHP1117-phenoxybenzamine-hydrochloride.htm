PHENOXYBENZAMINE HYDROCHLORIDE

Additional information

 

interactions (Phenoxybenzamine).

Indications

 

hypertensive episodes in phaeochromocytoma

Cautions

 

elderly; congestive heart failure; severe ischaemic heart disease (see also Contra-indications); cerebrovascular disease (avoid if history of cerebrovascular accident); monitor blood pressure regularly during infusion; carcinogenic in animals; avoid in acute porphyria (section 9.8.2); avoid extravasation (irritant to tissues)

Contra-indications

 

history of cerebrovascular accident; during recovery period after myocardial infarction (usually 3–4 weeks); avoid infusion in hypovolaemia

Renal impairment

 

use with caution

Pregnancy

 

hypotension may occur in newborn

Breast-feeding

 

may be present in milk

Side-effects

 

postural hypotension with dizziness and marked compensatory tachycardia, lassitude, nasal congestion, miosis, inhibition of ejaculation; rarely gastro-intestinal disturbances; decreased sweating and dry mouth after intravenous infusion; idiosyncratic profound hypotension within few minutes of starting infusion; convulsions following rapid intravenous infusion also reported
