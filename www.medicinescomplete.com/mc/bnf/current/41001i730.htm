Tacrolimus

Tacrolimus has the following interaction information:

Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Aciclovirpossible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with aciclovir Interactions do not apply to topical aciclovir preparations
Aminoglycosidesincreased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with aminoglycosides
Amphotericinincreased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonistsincreased risk of hyperkalaemia when tacrolimus given with angiotensin-II receptor antagonists
Antifungals, Imidazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by imidazoles
Atazanavirplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by atazanavir
Caspofunginplasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by caspofungin
Chloramphenicolplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by chloramphenicol
Ciclosporin tacrolimus increases plasma concentration of ciclosporin (increased risk of nephrotoxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Clarithromycinplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by clarithromycin
Dabigatran Etexilate tacrolimus possibly increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate—manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use
Danazolplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by danazol
Diltiazemplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by diltiazem
Diuretics, Potassium-sparing and Aldosterone Antagonistsincreased risk of hyperkalaemia when tacrolimus given with potassium-sparing diuretics and aldosterone antagonists
Dronedaronecaution with tacrolimus advised by manufacturer of dronedarone
Droperidolavoidance of tacrolimus advised by manufacturer of droperidol (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Efavirenzplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly affected by efavirenz
Erythromycinplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by erythromycin Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically
Ethinylestradiolplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by ethinylestradiol
Felodipineplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by felodipine
Fluconazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by fluconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by fosamprenavir Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Ganciclovirpossible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with ganciclovir Increased risk of myelosuppression with other myelosuppressive drugs—consult product literature
Grapefruit Juiceplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by grapefruit juice
Ibuprofenincreased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with ibuprofen
Itraconazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by itraconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)
Ketoconazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by ketoconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)
Mifamurtideavoidance of tacrolimus advised by manufacturer of mifamurtide
Nelfinavirplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by nelfinavir
Nicardipineplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by nicardipine
Nifedipineplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by nifedipine
NSAIDspossible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with NSAIDs See also Aspirin. Interactions do not generally apply to topical NSAIDs
Omeprazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by omeprazole
Phenobarbitalplasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by phenobarbital
Phenytoinplasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by phenytoin , also plasma concentration of phenytoin possibly increased
Posaconazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by posaconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)
Potassium Saltsincreased risk of hyperkalaemia when tacrolimus given with potassium salts Includes salt substitutes
Rifabutinplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly reduced by rifabutin
Rifampicinplasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by rifampicin
Ritonavirplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by ritonavir
Saquinavirplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by saquinavir (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)
Sevelamerplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly reduced by sevelamer
St John's Wortplasma concentration of tacrolimus reduced by St John's wort—avoid concomitant use
Telaprevirplasma concentration of both drugs increased when tacrolimus given with telaprevir (reduce dose of tacrolimus)
Telithromycinplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by telithromycin
Vancomycinpossible increased risk of nephrotoxicity when tacrolimus given with vancomycin
Verapamilplasma concentration of tacrolimus possibly increased by verapamil
Voriconazoleplasma concentration of tacrolimus increased by voriconazole (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)
