    THIAMINE(Vitamin B1)

    Indications

     

    see notes above

    Cautions

     

    anaphylaxis may occasionally follow injection (see MHRA/CHM advice below)

    MHRA/CHM advice (September 2007)

    Although potentially serious allergic adverse reactions may rarely occur during, or shortly after, parenteral administration, the CHM has recommended that:

    1. This should not preclude the use of parenteral thiamine in patients where this route of administration is required, particularly in patients at risk of Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome where treatment with thiamine is essential;

    2. Intravenous administration should be by infusion over 30 minutes;

    3. Facilities for treating anaphylaxis (including resuscitation facilities) should be available when parenteral thiamine is administered.

    Breast-feeding

     

    severely thiamine-deficient mothers should avoid breast-feeding as toxic methyl-glyoxal present in milk

    Dose

    • Mild deficiency, by mouth, 25–100 mg daily; severe deficiency, 200–300 mg daily in divided doses
