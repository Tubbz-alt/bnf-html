Antifungals, Imidazole

Antifungals, Imidazole has the following interaction information:

Amphotericin imidazoles possibly antagonise effects of amphotericin Close monitoring required with concomitant administration of nephrotoxic drugs or cytotoxics
Artemether with Lumefantrineavoidance of imidazoles advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine
Atorvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when imidazoles given with atorvastatin
Ergotamine and Methysergideincreased risk of ergotism when imidazoles given with ergotamine and methysergide—avoid concomitant use
Mizolastine imidazoles possibly inhibit metabolism of mizolastine (avoid concomitant use)
Oestrogensanecdotal reports of contraceptive failure when imidazoles given with oestrogens Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1
Pimozideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when imidazoles given with pimozide—avoid concomitant use
Quetiapine imidazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of quetiapine—manufacturer of quetiapine advises avoid concomitant use
Reboxetineavoidance of imidazoles advised by manufacturer of reboxetine
Saquinavir imidazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of saquinavir
Tacrolimus imidazoles possibly increase plasma concentration of tacrolimus Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Antifungals, Imidazole belongs to Antifungals but Antifungals has no interactions information.

Ketoconazole belongs to Antifungals, Imidazole and has the following interaction information:

Alfuzosin ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of alfuzosin
Aliskiren ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of aliskiren
Alitretinoin ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of alitretinoin
Almotriptan ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of almotriptan (increased risk of toxicity)
Alprazolam ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of alprazolam
Antacidsabsorption of ketoconazole reduced by antacids Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption
Antimuscarinicsabsorption of ketoconazole reduced by antimuscarinics Many drugs have antimuscarinic effects; concomitant use of two or more such drugs can increase side-effects such as dry mouth, urine retention, and constipation; concomitant use can also lead to confusion in the elderly. Interactions do not generally apply to antimuscarinics used by inhalation
Apixaban ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of apixaban—manufacturer of apixaban advises avoid concomitant use
Aprepitant ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of aprepitant
Aripiprazole ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of aripiprazole (reduce dose of aripiprazole)
Boceprevir ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of boceprevir
Bortezomib ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of bortezomib
Bosentan ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of bosentan
Budesonide ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled and oral budesonide
Buprenorphine ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of buprenorphine (reduce dose of buprenorphine)
Cabazitaxelavoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel
Calcium-channel Blockers (dihydropyridines) ketoconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of dihydropyridines (increased plasma concentration)
Carbamazepine ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Ciclesonide ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of ciclesonide
Ciclosporin ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Cilostazol ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of cilostazol (consider reducing dose of cilostazol)
Cinacalcet ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of cinacalcet (increased plasma concentration)
Clopidogrel ketoconazole possibly reduces antiplatelet effect of clopidogrel
Colchicine ketoconazole possibly increases risk of colchicine toxicity—suspend or reduce dose of colchicine (avoid concomitant use in hepatic or renal impairment)
Corticosteroids ketoconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of corticosteroids Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified
Coumarins ketoconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Dabigatran Etexilate ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate—avoid concomitant use
Darifenacin ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of darifenacin—avoid concomitant use
Darunavirplasma concentration of both drugs increased when ketoconazole given with darunavir
Dasatinib ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of dasatinib
Disopyramideincreased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when ketoconazole given with disopyramide—avoid concomitant use
Docetaxelin vitro studies suggest a possible interaction between ketoconazole and docetaxel (consult docetaxel product literature)
Domperidonemanufacturer of ketoconazole advises avoid concomitant use with domperidone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Dronedarone ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of dronedarone—avoid concomitant use
Eletriptan ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of eletriptan (risk of toxicity)—avoid concomitant use
Eplerenone ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of eplerenone—avoid concomitant use
Erlotinib ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of erlotinib (increased plasma concentration)
Everolimus ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of everolimus—avoid concomitant use
Felodipine ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of felodipine (increased plasma concentration)
Fesoterodinemanufacturer of fesoterodine advises dose reduction when ketoconazole given with fesoterodine—consult fesoterodine product literature
Fingolimod ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of fingolimod
Fosamprenavirplasma concentration of ketoconazole increased by fosamprenavir (also plasma concentration of fosamprenavir possibly increased)Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir
Galantamine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of galantamine
Histamine H2-antagonistsabsorption of ketoconazole reduced by histamine H2-antagonists
Imatinib ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of imatinib
Indinavir ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of indinavir (consider reducing dose of indinavir)
Irinotecan ketoconazole reduces plasma concentration of irinotecan (but concentration of active metabolite of irinotecan increased)—avoid concomitant use
Isoniazidplasma concentration of ketoconazole possibly reduced by isoniazid
Ivabradine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of ivabradine—avoid concomitant use
Lanthanumabsorption of ketoconazole possibly reduced by lanthanum (give at least 2 hours apart)
Lapatinib ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of lapatinib—avoid concomitant use
Lercanidipineavoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of lercanidipine
Loratadinemanufacturer of loratadine advises ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of loratadine
Maraviroc ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of maraviroc (consider reducing dose of maraviroc)
Mefloquine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of mefloquine
Methylprednisolone ketoconazole inhibits the metabolism of methylprednisolone
Midazolam ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of midazolam (risk of prolonged sedation)
Mirtazapine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of mirtazapine
Mizolastine ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of mizolastine—avoid concomitant use
Mometasone ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of inhaled mometasone
Nevirapineplasma concentration of ketoconazole reduced by nevirapine—avoid concomitant use
Nilotinib ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of nilotinib—avoid concomitant use
Paricalcitol ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of paricalcitol
Pazopanibavoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of pazopanib
Phenytoinplasma concentration of ketoconazole reduced by phenytoin
Proton Pump Inhibitorsabsorption of ketoconazole reduced by proton pump inhibitors
Ranolazine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of ranolazine—avoid concomitant use
Rifampicinmetabolism of ketoconazole accelerated by rifampicin (reduced plasma concentration), also plasma concentration of rifampicin may be reduced by ketoconazole
Ritonavircombination of ketoconazole with ritonavir may increase plasma concentration of either drug (or both)
Rivaroxaban ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of rivaroxaban—avoid concomitant use
Rupatadine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of rupatadine
Salmeterol ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of salmeterol (increased plasma concentration)
Saquinavir ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of saquinavir
Sildenafil ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of sildenafil—reduce initial dose of sildenafil
Simvastatinincreased risk of myopathy when ketoconazole given with simvastatin (avoid concomitant use)
Sirolimus ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus—avoid concomitant use
Solifenacin ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of solifenacin
Sucralfateabsorption of ketoconazole reduced by sucralfate
Sunitinib ketoconazole inhibits metabolism of sunitinib (increased plasma concentration)
Tacrolimus ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of tacrolimus (consider reducing dose of tacrolimus)Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically
Tadalafil ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of tadalafil—manufacturer of tadalafil advises avoid concomitant use
Telaprevirplasma concentration of both drugs possibly increased when ketoconazole given with telaprevir (increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias)
Telithromycinavoidance of concomitant ketoconazole in severe renal and hepatic impairment advised by manufacturer of telithromycin
Temsirolimus ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of temsirolimus—avoid concomitant useThe main active metabolite of temsirolimus is sirolimus—see also interactions of sirolimus and consult product literature
Theophylline ketoconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of theophylline
Ticagrelor ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of ticagrelor—manufacturer of ticagrelor advises avoid concomitant use
Tolterodineavoidance of ketoconazole advised by manufacturer of tolterodine
Tolvaptan ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of tolvaptan
Vardenafil ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of vardenafil—avoid concomitant use
Vinflunine ketoconazole increases plasma concentration of vinflunine—manufacturer of vinflunine advises avoid concomitant use

Miconazole belongs to Antifungals, Imidazole and has the following interaction information:

Carbamazepine miconazole possibly increases plasma concentration of carbamazepine
Ciclosporin miconazole possibly inhibits metabolism of ciclosporin (increased plasma concentration)
Coumarins miconazole enhances anticoagulant effect of coumarins (miconazole oral gel and possibly vaginal and topical formulations absorbed)Change in patient’s clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control
Gliclazide miconazole enhances hypoglycaemic effect of gliclazide—avoid concomitant use
Glipizide miconazole enhances hypoglycaemic effect of glipizide—avoid concomitant use
Phenytoin miconazole enhances anticonvulsant effect of phenytoin (plasma concentration of phenytoin increased)
Simvastatinpossible increased risk of myopathy when miconazole given with simvastatin—avoid concomitant use
Sirolimus miconazole increases plasma concentration of sirolimus
Sulfonylureas miconazole increases plasma concentration of sulfonylureas
