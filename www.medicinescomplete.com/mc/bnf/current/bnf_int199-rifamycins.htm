Rifamycins

Rifamycins has the following interaction information:

Note: Interactions do not apply to rifaximin

Clarithromycin

rifamycins reduce plasma concentration of clarithromycin

Corticosteroids

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of corticosteroids (reduced effect)

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to corticosteroids used for topical action (including inhalation) unless specified

Coumarins

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of coumarins (reduced anticoagulant effect)

Note: Change in patient's clinical condition, particularly associated with liver disease, intercurrent illness, or drug administration, necessitates more frequent testing. Major changes in diet (especially involving salads and vegetables) and in alcohol consumption may also affect anticoagulant control

Dapsone

rifamycins reduce plasma concentration of dapsone

Disopyramide

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of disopyramide (reduced plasma concentration)

Oestrogens

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of oestrogens (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1

Phenytoin

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of phenytoin (reduced plasma concentration)

Progestogens

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of progestogens (reduced contraceptive effect—see section 7.3.1)

Note: Interactions of combined oral contraceptives may also apply to combined contraceptive patches and vaginal rings, see section 7.3.1. For further information on interactions of oral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.1; parenteral progestogen-only contraceptives, see also section 7.3.2.2; the intra-uterine progestogen-only device, see also section 7.3.2.3; hormonal emergency contraception, see also section 7.3.5

Sulfonylureas

rifamycins possibly accelerate metabolism of sulfonylureas (reduced effect)

Tolbutamide

rifamycins accelerate metabolism of tolbutamide (reduced effect)

Rifamycins belongs to Antibacterials and will have the following interaction information:

Typhoid Vaccine (oral)

antibacterials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine —see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4

Rifabutin belongs to Rifamycins and has the following interaction information:

Antifungals, Triazole

plasma concentration of rifabutin possibly increased by triazoles (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)

Aripiprazole

rifabutin possibly reduces plasma concentration of aripiprazole —increase dose of aripiprazole

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by atazanavir (reduce dose of rifabutin)

Atovaquone

avoidance of concomitant rifabutin advised by manufacturer of atovaquone (plasma concentration of both drugs reduced)

Azithromycin

increased risk of side-effects including neutropenia when rifabutin given with azithromycin

Cabazitaxel

avoidance of rifabutin advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel

Carbamazepine

rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of carbamazepine

Clarithromycin

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by clarithromycin (increased risk of toxicity—reduce rifabutin dose)

Darunavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by darunavir (reduce dose of rifabutin)

Efavirenz

plasma concentration of rifabutin reduced by efavirenz —increase dose of rifabutin

Erythromycin

plasma concentration of rifabutin possibly increased by erythromycin (increased risk of toxicity—reduce rifabutin dose)

Note: Interactions do not apply to small amounts of erythromycin used topically

Etravirine

plasma concentration of both drugs reduced when rifabutin given with etravirine

Fluconazole

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by fluconazole (increased risk of uveitis—reduce rifabutin dose)

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by fosamprenavir (reduce dose of rifabutin)

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Indinavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by indinavir —avoid concomitant use

Itraconazole

rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of itraconazole —manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use

Lapatinib

avoidance of rifabutin advised by manufacturer of lapatinib

Nelfinavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by nelfinavir (halve dose of rifabutin)

Nevirapine

plasma concentration of rifabutin possibly increased by nevirapine

Posaconazole

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by posaconazole (also plasma concentration of posaconazole reduced)

Rilpivirine

rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of rilpivirine —avoid concomitant use

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by ritonavir (increased risk of toxicity—reduce rifabutin dose)

Saquinavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by saquinavir (also plasma concentration of saquinavir reduced)

Sirolimus

rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of sirolimus —avoid concomitant use

Tacrolimus

rifabutin possibly reduces plasma concentration of tacrolimus

Note: Interactions do not generally apply to tacrolimus used topically; risk of facial flushing and skin irritation with alcohol consumption (see section 13.5.3) does not apply to tacrolimus taken systemically

Telaprevir

avoidance of rifabutin advised by manufacturer of telaprevir

Tipranavir

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by tipranavir (reduce dose of rifabutin)

Vemurafenib

avoidance of rifabutin advised by manufacturer of vemurafenib

Voriconazole

plasma concentration of rifabutin increased by voriconazole , also rifabutin reduces plasma concentration of voriconazole (increase dose of voriconazole and also monitor for rifabutin toxicity)

Rifampicin belongs to Rifamycins and has the following interaction information:

Abacavir

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of abacavir

Alfentanil

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of alfentanil (reduced effect)

Aliskiren

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of aliskiren

Antacids

absorption of rifampicin reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Apixaban

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of apixaban

Aprepitant

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of aprepitant

Aripiprazole

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of aripiprazole —increase dose of aripiprazole

Atazanavir

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of atazanavir —avoid concomitant use

Atorvastatin

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of atorvastatin

Atovaquone

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of atovaquone (and concentration of rifampicin increased)—avoid concomitant use

Benzodiazepines

rifampicin possibly accelerates metabolism of benzodiazepines (reduced plasma concentration)

Bisoprolol

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of bisoprolol (plasma concentration significantly reduced)

Boceprevir

avoidance of rifampicin advised by manufacturer of boceprevir (plasma concentration of boceprevir possibly reduced)

Bosentan

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of bosentan —avoid concomitant use

Buspirone

rifampicin possibly accelerates metabolism of buspirone

Cabazitaxel

avoidance of rifampicin advised by manufacturer of cabazitaxel

Carvedilol

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of carvedilol

Caspofungin

rifampicin initially increases and then reduces plasma concentration of caspofungin (consider increasing dose of caspofungin)

Celecoxib

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of celecoxib

Celiprolol

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of celiprolol

Chloramphenicol

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of chloramphenicol (reduced plasma concentration)

Ciclosporin

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of ciclosporin (reduced plasma concentration)

Cimetidine

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of cimetidine (reduced plasma concentration)

Clozapine

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of clozapine

Note: Avoid concomitant use of clozapine with drugs that have a substantial potential for causing agranulocytosis

Codeine

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of codeine (reduced effect)

Dabigatran Etexilate

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of dabigatran etexilate —manufacturer of dabigatran etexilate advises avoid concomitant use

Darunavir

rifampicin significantly reduces plasma concentration of darunavir —avoid concomitant use

Dasatinib

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of dasatinib (reduced plasma concentration—avoid concomitant use)

Deferasirox

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of deferasirox

Diazepam

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of diazepam (reduced plasma concentration)

Diclofenac

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of diclofenac

Digoxin

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of digoxin

Diltiazem

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of diltiazem (plasma concentration significantly reduced)

Doxycycline

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of doxycycline —consider increasing dose of doxycycline

Dronedarone

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of dronedarone —avoid concomitant use

Efavirenz

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of efavirenz —increase dose of efavirenz

Eplerenone

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of eplerenone —avoid concomitant use

Eribulin

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of eribulin

Erlotinib

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of erlotinib (reduced plasma concentration)

Etoricoxib

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of etoricoxib

Etravirine

avoidance of rifampicin advised by manufacturer of etravirine

Everolimus

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of everolimus

Exemestane

rifampicin possibly reduces plasma concentration of exemestane

Fentanyl

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of fentanyl (reduced effect)

Fesoterodine

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of active metabolite of fesoterodine

Fexofenadine

rifampicin possibly reduces effects of fexofenadine

Fluconazole

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of fluconazole (reduced plasma concentration)

Note: In general, fluconazole interactions relate to multiple-dose treatment

Fluvastatin

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of fluvastatin (reduced effect)

Fosamprenavir

rifampicin significantly reduces plasma concentration of fosamprenavir —avoid concomitant use

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Gefitinib

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of gefitinib —avoid concomitant use

Haloperidol

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of haloperidol (reduced plasma concentration)

Imatinib

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of imatinib —avoid concomitant use

Imidapril

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of active metabolite of imidapril (reduced antihypertensive effect)

Indinavir

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of indinavir (reduced plasma concentration—avoid concomitant use)

Isradipine

rifampicin possibly accelerates metabolism of isradipine (possible significantly reduced plasma concentration)

Itraconazole

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of itraconazole —manufacturer of itraconazole advises avoid concomitant use

Ketoconazole

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of ketoconazole (reduced plasma concentration), also plasma concentration of rifampicin may be reduced by ketoconazole

Lamotrigine

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of lamotrigine

Lapatinib

avoidance of rifampicin advised by manufacturer of lapatinib

Leflunomide

rifampicin possibly increases plasma concentration of active metabolite of leflunomide

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with other haematotoxic and hepatotoxic drugs

Levothyroxine

rifampicin accelerates metabolism of levothyroxine (may increase requirements for levothyroxine in hypothyroidism)

Linagliptin

rifampicin possibly reduces effects of linagliptin

Linezolid

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of linezolid (possible therapeutic failure of linezolid)

Note: Linezolid is a reversible, non-selective MAO inhibitor—see interactions of MAOIs

Lopinavir

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of lopinavir —avoid concomitant use

Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Losartan

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of losartan and its active metabolite

Maraviroc

rifampicin reduces plasma concentration of maraviroc —consider increasing dose of maraviroc

Mefloquine

Nifedipine

Nilotinib

Ondansetron

Oxycodone

Pazopanib

Vemurafenib
