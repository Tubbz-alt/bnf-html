ORAL REHYDRATION SALTS (ORS)

Indications

 

fluid and electrolyte loss in diarrhoea, see notes above

Dose

  • According to fluid loss, usually 200–400 mL solution after every loose motion; infant 1–1½ times usual feed volume; child 200 mL after every loose motion

Sub-sections

  • UK formulations
    • Dioralyte®
    • Dioralyte® Relief
    • Electrolade®

WHO formulation

Oral Rehydration Salts (Non-proprietary)

Oral powder, sodium chloride 2.6 g, potassium chloride 1.5 g, sodium citrate 2.9 g, anhydrous glucose 13.5 g. To be dissolved in sufficient water to produce 1 litre (providing Na+ 75 mmol, K+ 20 mmol, Cl– 65 mmol, citrate 10 mmol, glucose 75 mmol/litre)

Note

 

Recommended by the WHO and the United Nations Children's Fund but not commonly used in the UK.
