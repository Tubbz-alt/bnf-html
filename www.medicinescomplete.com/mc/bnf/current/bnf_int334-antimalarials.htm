Antimalarials

Antimalarials has the following interaction information:

Artemether with Lumefantrine

avoidance of antimalarials advised by manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine

Histamine

avoidance of antimalarials advised by manufacturer of histamine

Typhoid Vaccine (oral)

antimalarials inactivate oral typhoid vaccine —see under Typhoid vaccines, section 14.4

Artemether with Lumefantrine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with amiodarone (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antidepressants

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with antidepressants

Antifungals, Imidazole

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with imidazoles

Antifungals, Triazole

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with triazoles

Antimalarials

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with antimalarials

Antipsychotics

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with antipsychotics

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with myelosuppressive drugs

Atazanavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with atazanavir

Boceprevir

avoidance of artemether/lumefantrine advised by manufacturer of boceprevir

Cimetidine

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with cimetidine

Darunavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with darunavir

Disopyramide

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with disopyramide (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Flecainide

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with flecainide (risk of ventricular arrhythmias)

Fosamprenavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with fosamprenavir

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Grapefruit Juice

plasma concentration of artemether/lumefantrine possibly increased by grapefruit juice

Indinavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with indinavir

Lopinavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with lopinavir

Note: In combination with ritonavir as Kaletra® (ritonavir is present to inhibit lopinavir metabolism and increase plasma-lopinavir concentration)—see also Ritonavir

Macrolides

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with macrolides

Note: See also Telithromycin

Metoprolol

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with metoprolol

Nelfinavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with nelfinavir

Quinine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when artemether/lumefantrine given with quinine

Quinolones

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with quinolones

Ritonavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with ritonavir

Saquinavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with saquinavir

Sotalol

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises avoid concomitant use with sotalol

Tipranavir

manufacturer of artemether/lumefantrine advises caution with tipranavir

Vandetanib

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when artemether/lumefantrine given with vandetanib —avoid concomitant use

Lumefantrine belongs to Antimalarials but has no specific interaction information.

Note: Lumefantrine interactions as for artemether with lumefantrine

Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Agalsidase Alfa and Beta

chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly inhibit effects of agalsidase alfa and beta (manufacturers of agalsidase alfa and beta advise avoid concomitant use)

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antacids

absorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine reduced by antacids

Note: Antacids should preferably not be taken at the same time as other drugs since they may impair absorption

Ciclosporin

chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine increase plasma concentration of ciclosporin (increased risk of toxicity)

Cimetidine

metabolism of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Digoxin

chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly increase plasma concentration of digoxin

Droperidol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with droperidol —avoid concomitant use

Kaolin

absorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine reduced by kaolin

Lanthanum

absorption of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly reduced by lanthanum (give at least 2 hours apart)

Laronidase

chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine possibly inhibit effects of laronidase (manufacturer of laronidase advises avoid concomitant use)

Mefloquine

increased risk of convulsions when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with mefloquine

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Neostigmine

chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine have potential to increase symptoms of myasthenia gravis and thus diminish effect of neostigmine

Pyridostigmine

chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine have potential to increase symptoms of myasthenia gravis and thus diminish effect of pyridostigmine

Hydroxychloroquine belongs to Antimalarials but has no specific interaction information.

Note: Hydroxychloroquine interactions as for chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine

Mefloquine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Antiepileptics

mefloquine antagonises anticonvulsant effect of antiepileptics

Atomoxetine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with atomoxetine

Beta-blockers

increased risk of bradycardia when mefloquine given with beta-blockers

Note: Since systemic absorption may follow topical application of beta-blockers to the eye the possibility of interactions, in particular, with drugs such as verapamil should be borne in mind

Calcium-channel Blockers

possible increased risk of bradycardia when mefloquine given with calcium-channel blockers

Note: Dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers include amlodipine, felodipine, isradipine, lacidipine, lercanidipine, nicardipine, nifedipine, and nimodipine

Chloroquine and Hydroxychloroquine

increased risk of convulsions when mefloquine given with chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine

Digoxin

possible increased risk of bradycardia when mefloquine given with digoxin

Haloperidol

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with haloperidol —avoid concomitant use

Ivabradine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with ivabradine

Ketoconazole

plasma concentration of mefloquine increased by ketoconazole

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when mefloquine given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Quinine

increased risk of convulsions when mefloquine given with quinine (but should not prevent the use of intravenousquinine in severe cases)

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of mefloquine reduced by rifampicin —avoid concomitant use

Primaquine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Mepacrine

plasma concentration of primaquine increased by mepacrine (increased risk of toxicity)

Proguanil belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Magnesium Salts (oral)

absorption of proguanil reduced by oral magnesium salts (as magnesium trisilicate)

Pyrimethamine

increased antifolate effect when proguanil given with pyrimethamine

Warfarin

isolated reports that proguanil may enhance anticoagulant effect of warfarin

Pyrimethamine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Methotrexate

pyrimethamine increases antifolate effect of methotrexate

Pemetrexed

pyrimethamine increases antifolate effect of pemetrexed

Phenytoin

pyrimethamine antagonises anticonvulsant effect of phenytoin , also increased antifolate effect

Proguanil

increased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with proguanil

Sulfonamides

increased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with sulfonamides

Trimethoprim

increased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with trimethoprim

Zidovudine

increased antifolate effect when pyrimethamine given with zidovudine

Note: Increased risk of toxicity with nephrotoxic and myelosuppressive drugs—for further details consult product literature

Quinine belongs to Antimalarials and has the following interaction information:

Amantadine

quinine possibly increases plasma concentration of amantadine

Amiodarone

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with amiodarone —avoid concomitant use

Note: Amiodarone has a long half-life; there is a potential for drug interactions to occur for several weeks (or even months) after treatment with it has been stopped

Artemether with Lumefantrine

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with artemether/lumefantrine

Atazanavir

plasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by atazanavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Cimetidine

metabolism of quinine inhibited by cimetidine (increased plasma concentration)

Darunavir

plasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by darunavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Digoxin

quinine increases plasma concentration of digoxin

Droperidol

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with droperidol —avoid concomitant use

Flecainide

quinine increases plasma concentration of flecainide

Fosamprenavir

plasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by fosamprenavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Note: Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of amprenavir

Haloperidol

possible increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with haloperidol —avoid concomitant use

Indinavir

plasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by indinavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Mefloquine

increased risk of convulsions when quinine given with mefloquine (but should not prevent the use of intravenousquinine in severe cases)

Moxifloxacin

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with moxifloxacin —avoid concomitant use

Nelfinavir

plasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by nelfinavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Pimozide

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with pimozide —avoid concomitant use

Rifampicin

plasma concentration of quinine reduced by rifampicin

Ritonavir

plasma concentration of quinine increased by ritonavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Saquinavir

increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias when quinine given with saquinavir —avoid concomitant use

Suxamethonium

quinine possibly enhances effects of suxamethonium

Tipranavir

plasma concentration of quinine possibly increased by tipranavir (increased risk of toxicity)

Warfarin

plasma concentration of both drugs increased when quinine given with warfarin
